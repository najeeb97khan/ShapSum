[1] Hurricane Hugo is the latest of the many disasters faced by Charleston, S.C. in its eventful 300 year history. [2] Hugo hit Charleston head-on with winds up to 135 mph Thursday night. [3] A real killer, it was the first major hurricane to strike Charleston since Gracie made landfall 70 miles to the south in 1959. [4] The worst disaster in recent decades was in 1938 [5] when a tornado tore through the city killing 32 people, [6] injuring hundreds and causing $2 million in property damage. [7] The city has survived the effects of two wars and several lesser hurricanes, [8] but the worst natural disaster was the Charleston earthquake of Aug. 31, 1886 [9] which claimed 110 lives [10] and destroyed 102 buildings.
