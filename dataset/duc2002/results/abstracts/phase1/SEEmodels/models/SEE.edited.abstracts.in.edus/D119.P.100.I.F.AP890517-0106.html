[1] The Senate Foreign Relations Committee endorsed the nomination of Republican activist Peter Secchia to be ambassador to Italy. [2] The chairman, Senator Pell, had many misgivings about the nomination [3] but did not vote against Secchia. [4] Secchia pledged to be more careful in his use of language as a diplomat [5] that he had used as an activist. [6] Pell was not convinced. [7] He said that the nomination was a mistake [8] and that "in all likelihood Mr. Secchia will be an embarrassment to the administration and the United States". However, [9] since Secchia is the president's choice [10] he would not try to block it.
