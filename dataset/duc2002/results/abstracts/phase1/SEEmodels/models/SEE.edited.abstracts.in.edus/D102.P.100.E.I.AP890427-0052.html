[1] Residents of Lucille Ball's hometown, Jamestown N.Y., remembered the star on Wednesday, [2] following her death in Los Angeles. [3] She had starred in high school plays [4] and appeared on the vaudeville stage. [5] Although Miss Ball had not been to Jamestown since 1956, [6] she maintained friends [7] and donated money to charities and civic events. [8] After she became ill, [9] the town sent her 150 posters with 10,000 signatures. [10] She was to have been honored on May 20 with an honorary degree from the community college and a street renamed Lucy Lane in the village where she was raised. [11] The honors will now be awarded posthumously.
