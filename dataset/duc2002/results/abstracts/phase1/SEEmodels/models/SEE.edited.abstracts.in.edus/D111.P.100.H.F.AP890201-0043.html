[1] An Argentine supply ship, [2] which also carried tourists, [3] ran aground [4] and sank off Antarctica. [5] Tourists and crew were evacuated from the Bahia Paraiso to Palmer Station, a US facility. [6] No injuries were reported. [7] Leaks from the ship killed some krill, a vital part of the food chain, [8] and any further leaks would constitute an ecological disaster. [9] Argentine officials doubted further spilling since the ship's fuel and that being taken to the Argentine Esperanza station were in steel drums. [10] A cruise ship took the tourists to the South Shetland Islands, [11] but 104 crewmen remained at the station, [12] which is equipped for only 40 people.
