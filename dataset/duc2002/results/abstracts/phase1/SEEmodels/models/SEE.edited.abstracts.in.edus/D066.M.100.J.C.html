[1] Sam Walton relied on his personality, hard work and a willingness [2] to adopt new business practices [3] to build Wal-Mart into the country's largest discount retailer. [4] This made him the country's richest man, [5] but he continued to live a simple life. [6] Identification as the richest man made him uncomfortable. [7] He was criticized for anti-union policies [8] and causing the failure of small town businesses where he located his stores. [9] Walton often visited his workers [10] and kept them happy. [11] Shortly before his death in 1992 Walton wrote a folksy autobiography. [12] There is also a "scholarly study" on his business.
