[1] US Intelligence officials, a week before the Soviet coup, warned the White House [2] that Soviet hard-liners might act to block the signing of the new Union Treaty [3] although they knew too little to enable President Bush to act. [4] Predictably, the hard-liners would fail [5] because opposition to the coup was against "the illegality of the principle" [6] of usurping power, and fear that [7] if Gorbachev could be "rolled up" other democratically elected officials could be too. [8] Gorbachev will become a figurehead, abandon the Party [9] because it is "dead" and move more aggressively toward reform. [10] Restraints on cooperation with the West will weaken.
