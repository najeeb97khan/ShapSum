[1] Hurricane Hugo struck South Carolina Thursday [2] after thousands of coastal residents were evacuated inland. [3] By late afternoon the storm was upgraded to category 4. [4] Hurricane-force winds first hit the coast at 9:15 p.m. and by midnight 119 mph winds were reported in Charleston and up to 135 mph as the eye passed nearby. [5] Hugo was expected to turn north and cause severe flooding as it passed up the coast. [6] As of Thursday evening about 12,000 people had checked into 83 shelters across South Carolina. [7] A spokesman for the Red Cross said that tens of thousands of people were in shelters along the Eastern seaboard.
