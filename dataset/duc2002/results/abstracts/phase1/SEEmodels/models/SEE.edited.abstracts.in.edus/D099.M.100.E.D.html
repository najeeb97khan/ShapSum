[1] Marathons [2] attract both professional athletes as well as amateur runners. [3] Some marathons, such as Los Angeles', concentrate on the commercial and moneymaking side of marathons, while other cities such as New York, Boston and San Francisco attract world-class runners. [4] Still others, such as the Marine Corps marathon which bills itself as the "people's race," [5] are just for the sport. [6] Tour companies sponsor international trips organized around marathons, offering clients a chance [7] to run in some of the most popular marathons in the world. [8] Age is no barrier to marathon running. [9] John Kelley, 83, [10] plans to run in the Boston marathon.
