[1] Attended by a brass band and the foreign ministers of the four World War II allies, [2] Checkpoint Charlie was lifted into history today. [3] Checkpoint Charlie was erected after Communist East Germany built the Berlin Wall [4] and it came to symbolize the Cold War. [5] Attendance by Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze, the first visit of a Soviet foreign minister to Berlin, was seen as a gesture of reconciliation. [6] Former West German Chancellor Willy Brandt, who challenged the building of the wall in the 1960s as Mayor of Berlin, sat in the front row of attendees. [7] Checkpoint Charlie's cobblestone foundation disappeared to souvenir hunters after the ceremony.
