[1] The eyepiece [2] used by astronomer Edwin Hubble from the Mount Wilson Observatory's 100-inch Hooker Telescope, [3] once the world's most powerful, was carried into orbit by the shuttle [4] that launched the space telescope honoring him, a symbolic gesture from astronomy's past to its future. [5] Using the Hooker telescope, Hubble was the first [6] to determine the distance to a galaxy outside the Milky Way. [7] This provided evidence for the concept [8] that the universe is expanding. [9] His work helped convince scientists [10] that the universe was created in a giant explosion, [11] called the "big bang." [12] The Hooker Telescope was mothballed in 1985.
