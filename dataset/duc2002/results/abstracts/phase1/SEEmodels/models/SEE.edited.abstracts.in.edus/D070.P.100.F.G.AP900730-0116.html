[1] East Germany's deposed Communist leader, Erich Honecker, has been declared too sick to be in jail [2] but fit enough to be tried. [3] He is still recovering from kidney cancer surgery in January. [4] A medical panel concluded [5] that seven other former Communist officials -all close aides to Honecker- [6] were fit to prosecute [7] and to be placed in custody despite advanced age and poor health. [8] Along with Honecker, they are charged with corruption and abuse of office. [9] Honecker has been staying at a Soviet military hospital outside East Berlin. [10] Although their poor health often disrupted proceedings, [11] investigations against the former East German leaders will continue.
