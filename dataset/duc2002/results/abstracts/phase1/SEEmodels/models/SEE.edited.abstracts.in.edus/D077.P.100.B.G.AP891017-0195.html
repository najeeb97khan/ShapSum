[1] A major earthquake lasting about fifteen seconds [2] and measuring 6.5 on the Richter scale, rocked northern California on Tuesday evening. [3] One section of the San Francisco Bay Bridge collapsed. [4] Nearby buildings swayed several feet and some may have collapsed. [5] Phones and power were knocked out. [6] Fans at Candlestick Park waiting for the World Series to begin screamed [7] as the stadium swayed for about fifteen seconds. [8] Fans were evacuated [9] and there were reports of some injuries. [10] The quake shook buildings in Sacramento, 95 miles to the northeast, [11] and was felt as far away as Reno, NV, 225 miles to the east.
