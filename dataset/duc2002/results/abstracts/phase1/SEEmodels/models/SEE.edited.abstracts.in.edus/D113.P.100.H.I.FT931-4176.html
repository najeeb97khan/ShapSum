[1] A row has erupted between the Bombay stock exchange and the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) [2] over its first inspection of the stockbrokers' books. [3] SEBI accused the brokers of ignoring rules and authorities of failing to enforce rules and of poor administration. [4] The exchange denied the charges [5] and called SEBI heavy handed and inflexible. [6] The stock market has fallen 48 percent since early last year [7] including dropping 320 points [8] after the Indian budget was announced. [9] Foreign investors are concerned. [10] This is just the latest in a series of disputes between SEBI -backed by the finance minister -and brokers.
