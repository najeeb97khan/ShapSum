[1] Firing indiscriminately on the crowds as they advanced, [2] Chinese troops broke through barricades of reform-minded protesters late on 3 June1989 [3] and reached Tiananmen Square after being rebuffed in earlier attempts. [4] Early the next day they routed the surviving demonstrators [5] who fled the square that had occupied for three weeks in defiance of government orders. [6] Thousands died in the two days of violence. [7] The bloody weekend was the climax of trouble that had escalated since April. [8] Later demonstrations in other large Chinese cities protested the Beijing slaughter. [9] The United States suspended all military sales to China.
