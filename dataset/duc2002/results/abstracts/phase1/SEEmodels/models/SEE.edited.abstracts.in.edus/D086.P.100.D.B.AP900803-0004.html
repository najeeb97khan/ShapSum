[1] Iraq's President Saddam Hussein seemed determined to solve his financial problems [2] and fulfill territorial ambitions [3] by conquering Kuwait. [4] He accused Kuwait of costing Iraq billions in lost oil revenue by exceeding OPEC assigned quotas [5] and by "stealing" oil from a disputed field [6] that straddles their borders. [7] Further, he was incensed [8] that Kuwait was demanding repayment of its estimated $15 billion in 1980s wartime loans to Iraq. [9] It is believed Saddam would use the Kuwaiti precedent [10] to dominate neighboring countries on policy issues. [11] He has long sought to inherit the mantle of the late Egyptian President Nassar as leader of the Arab world.
