[1] After two weeks of accusations and maneuvering, [2] Iraq invaded tiny Kuwait on August 2 with 100,000 battle-trained troops, quickly taking the country. [3] While the Kuwaiti emir and refugees fled to Saudi Arabia, [4] Iraq began rounding up foreign nationals and repopulating the nation with Iraqi citizens. [5] Some foreigners were taken to Iraq, [6] while some were flown to Jordan. [7] Reactions to the invasion have been strong. [8] The United Nations Security Council unanimously passed trade and military sanctions and President Bush said [9] he would defend U.S. interests. [10] Iran rejoiced. [11] Most nations are strongly enforcing these sanctions [12] as oil prices soar [13] and Iraq begins defensive preparations.
