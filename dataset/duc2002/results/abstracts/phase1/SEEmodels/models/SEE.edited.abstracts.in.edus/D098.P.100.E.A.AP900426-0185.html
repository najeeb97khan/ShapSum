[1] Six hours after a failed attempt, ground controllers re-established contact Thursday with the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) [2] through its main antennas. Controllers sent positional information to the telescope [3] and widened the radio beam [4] to cover a larger area around the two high-speed antennas. [5] Space shuttle Discovery trailed 57 miles behind the HST [6] and was to stay nearby until the Hubble's aperture door is opened Friday morning. [7] If signals fail to open the door, astronauts will walk into space [8] and crank it open Saturday. [9] The shuttle, scheduled to return to earth on Sunday, would extend its mission if the space walk is necessary.
