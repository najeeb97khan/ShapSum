[1] On Friday a crane lifted Checkpoint Charlie, the Allied border crossing on the west side of the Berlin Wall, [2] and placed it on a flatbed truck. [3] The guardhouse was taken away in an elaborate ceremony attended by six foreign ministers, a brass band and hundreds of reporters. [4] The public was not invited to the ceremony [5] but many Berliners viewed it from nearby, reliving memories of attempted escapes through the checkpoint [6] that went up with the Berlin Wall in 1961. [7] Now with the wall being dismantled daily in anticipation of German unification, [8] U.S. officials decided to remove Checkpoint Charlie with a grand flourish.
