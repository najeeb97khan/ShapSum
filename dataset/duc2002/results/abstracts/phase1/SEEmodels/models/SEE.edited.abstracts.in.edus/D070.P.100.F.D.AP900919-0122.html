[1] East Germany's ousted secret police chief, Erich Mielke, [2] threatened to break former Communist leader Erich Honecker's neck when the two faced each other in jail. After the popular revolt last fall [3] that ousted the Communist regime [4] and opened the way for German unification, Mielke and other former Communist leaders were jailed. [5] Mielke remains in custody, [6] Honecker was released for health reasons. [7] Many of the prison guards had worked for Mielke's secret police [8] and might interpret his threat against Honecker as an order [9] to kill him. [10] Honecker will be moved to a West Berlin prison after unification [11] if his health improves.
