[1] As punishment for not withdrawing its forces from Kuwait, [2] the U.N. Security Council voted to impose trade and military sanctions against Iraq, including a ban on oil purchases. [3] It was only the third time in the U.N.'s 45-year existence [4] that it voted sanctions against a member state. [5] The sanctions are legally binding for all member nations, [6] although past resolutions have often been ignored or flouted. [7] Earlier, the Iraqi- installed military government of Kuwait warned other nations [8] to not take punishing measures against it or Iraq, [9] ominously saying [10] that such countries "have to remember they have interests and citizens in Kuwait."
