[1] Supplies, medical personnel, and condolences have begun to arrive in Iran [2] where about 40,000 died in an earthquake. [3] Responding to the humanitarian need, countries both friendly and hostile to Tehran's Islamic government have contributed to the relief effort. [4] The United States, which broke diplomatic relations with Iran, [5] will provide aid through the American Red Cross. [6] In addition to supplies, the Soviet Union sent a professional life saving team from the Armenian capital of Yerevan. [7] The French have said [8] they will send a mobile hospital unit if Iran wants it. [9] Pope John Paul II also donated to the effort.
