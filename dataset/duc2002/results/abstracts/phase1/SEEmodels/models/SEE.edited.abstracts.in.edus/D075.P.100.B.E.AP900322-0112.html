[1] Danish studies of the drug Verapamil, a calcium channel blocker, show [2] that the drug reduces the chance of a new heart attack in patients recovering from a heart attack. [3] This is the first trial [4] to indicate that calcium blockers may be helpful after a heart attack. [5] Verapamil is about as effective as beta blockers in protecting people after heart attacks [6] and may be useful in instances where beta blockers cannot be used. [7] Many people do not tolerate beate blockers, [8] especially those suffering from asthma and emphysema. [9] Verapamil was effective in decreasing mortality in the first 12 to 18 months following a heart attack.
