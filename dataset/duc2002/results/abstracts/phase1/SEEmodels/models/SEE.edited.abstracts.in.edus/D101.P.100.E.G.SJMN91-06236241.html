[1] Mikhail Gorbachev and Boris Yeltsin, meeting for the first time since the coup collapsed, [2] pressed ahead with a sweeping purge of coup supporters. [3] Gorbachev said at the Russian Parliament that the government would prosecute those responsible for the takeover. [4] He accused the Communist Party Central Committee of panicking during the coup, [5] and threw out his foreign minister. Yeltsin, who led the nationwide coup resistance, [6] suspended six Communist Party newspapers [7] and nationalized printing presses in the Russian Republic. [8] The Communist Party was banned from the KGB. [9] Gorbachev said he would meet with the leaders of the nine republics supporting the Union Treaty.
