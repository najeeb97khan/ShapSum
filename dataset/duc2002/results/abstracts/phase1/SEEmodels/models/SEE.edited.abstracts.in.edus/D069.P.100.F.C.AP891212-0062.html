[1] The East German Communist Party, through its official newspaper, Neues Deutschland, provided one of the bluntest statements [2] to date concerning the country's troubled situation. [3] It admitted [4] that the East German socialist system had failed, [5] and expressed support for some type of partnership with West Germany. [6] Forty thousand people demonstrated for unification in Karl Marx Stadt, [7] and in Leipzig and Dresden shouting matches broke out between pro and anti-unification demonstrators. [8] The rallies came shortly after the four victorious allies of World War II met in West Berlin [9] to discuss the latest developments in East Germany and Berlin.
