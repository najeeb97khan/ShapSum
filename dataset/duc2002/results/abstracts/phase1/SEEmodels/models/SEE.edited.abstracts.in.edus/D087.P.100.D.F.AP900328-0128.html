[1] The late Jesse Owens, hero of the 1936 Berlin Olympics [2] where he exploded the Hitler myth of Aryan superiority, [3] was honored at a White House ceremony [4] when President Bush presented the Congressional Gold Medal to his widow, Ruth Owens. [5] Bush cited Owens' four gold medals at the Berlin Games as "a victory for humanity". Bush, [6] borrowing a phrase from Ruth Owens, [7] said it was not the athletic feats but "his humanitarian contributions in the race of life" [8] that earned the medal for Owens. [9] Owens' name lives on through many activities and a foundation [10] that awards scholarships to needy youth.
