[1] Britain's Booker Prize for fiction novels is similar to America's Pulitzer Prize and National Book awards [2] but more excitement is associated with it. [3] British publishers each submit three new titles. [4] The total of around 100 books is narrowed down to a final six by a panel of learned judges [5] after they have read all the novels. [6] Odds are given with reviews, [7] and literary insiders bet on which book will win the prize. [8] The winner is announced at a yearly banquet. [9] The Prize is administered by Book Trust, an educational charity, [10] and sponsored by Booker, an international food and farming business.
