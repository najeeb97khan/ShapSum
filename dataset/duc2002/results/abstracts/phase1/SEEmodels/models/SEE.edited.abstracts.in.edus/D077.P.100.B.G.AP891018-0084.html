[1] Ever since the 1906 earthquake devastated San Francisco, [2] many scientists believed [3] the next "big one" would strike the Los Angles area. [4] Their studies gave various interpretations [5] of how much stress was released by the 1906 earthquake (magnitude 8.3). [6] The greater the quake, the more stress it relieves [7] and, thus, the longer it takes for the same size quake to occur again. [8] In the face of conflicting evidence, the US Geological Survey issued a 1988 report [9] that concluded the 1906 quake didn't release as much stress in the San Francisco region as believed. The 6.9 quake Tuesday confirmed the USGS study.
