[1] Troops of the former Soviet Union were ordered out of the Armenian enclave of Nagorno-Karabakh [2] after Armenian and Azeri militants fired on them. [3] The enclave is administered by Azerbaijan, [4] but largely inhabited by Armenians. [5] Armenians in the enclave want unification with Armenia, [6] but Azerbaijan refuses to give up the territory. [7] The commander of the Soviet troops has long warned [8] that he would pull out troops [9] rather than to allow them to become the targets of ethnic conflicts. [10] Troops under fire along the Armenian-Azerbaijan border will also be withdrawn. [11] Azeri and Armenian officials say [12] that guerrillas on both sides of the conflict are using sophisticated weapons.
