[1] The shuttle Discovery soared into space [2] to launch its history-making payload, the $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope. [3] While orbiting at 380 statute miles above Earth, [4] it will provide astronomers worldwide with 10 times better resolution and 25 times more sensitivity than ground-based observatories. [5] The telescope's release from the shuttle Discovery was delayed [6] because a solar power panel took three tries before it fully unfurled. [7] Once released, ground controllers were unable to link up the telescope's two high-gain antennas with their assigned relay satellite. [8] However, scientists, [9] optimistic that the Hubble will soon be operational, [10] authorized the shuttle Discovery crew [11] to return home.
