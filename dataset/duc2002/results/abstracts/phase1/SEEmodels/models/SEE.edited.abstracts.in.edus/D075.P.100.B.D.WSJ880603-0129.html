[1] Doctors should be cautious in prescribing aspirin [2] to prevent heart attacks until more research supports its value. [3] Studies do indicate that aspirin reduces the risk of first non- fatal heart attacks, [4] by reducing the ability of blood platelets [5] to form clots. [6] But for people who have [7] clotting problems or have high blood pressure aspirin may increase the risk for hemorrhagic stroke. [8] In one study, there were 10 hemorrhagic strokes recorded among the 11,000 subjects taking aspirin, [9] compared with 2 strokes in the 11,000 taking a substitute. [10] Overall death rates for the two groups were similar, [11] though the aspirin non-fatal heart attack rate was much lower.
