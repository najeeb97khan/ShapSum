[1] The Big Mac debuted to mixed review [2] as McDonald's opened restaurants in Korea, Yugoslavia, the Soviet Union, and China. [3] Large crowds stood in line for hours at openings in Belgrade, Moscow, and Peking. [4] Customers in Yugoslavia, the first communist country to get a McDonald's, and China raved about the taste of the Big Mac. [5] In Moscow, the Russians were less enthusiastic about its taste [6] but gave a thumbs up to the shakes. [7] The openings came after long negotiations which, in most cases, [8] saw McDonald's contracting with local farmers for the beef, potatoes and other supplies needed for its restaurants.
