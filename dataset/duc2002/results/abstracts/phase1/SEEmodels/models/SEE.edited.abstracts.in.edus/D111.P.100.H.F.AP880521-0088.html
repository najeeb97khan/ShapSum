[1] Greek divers have found the remains of a Turkish warship, the Bourloti Seimaz, [2] in the eastern Aegean near the island of Chios. [3] Greek revolutionaries had sunk the ship in 1822 in revenge for Turkish killing and looting on Chios. [4] Thousands died in Chios and this action put European countries behind the Greek struggle for independence from the Ottoman Empire, [5] which they won in 1830. [6] The Greeks had rammed the Turkish ship with a boatload of explosives. [7] The Greeks escaped the explosion [8] that killed the Ottoman commander. [9] The remains of the ship lie under 162 feet of water making recovery difficult.
