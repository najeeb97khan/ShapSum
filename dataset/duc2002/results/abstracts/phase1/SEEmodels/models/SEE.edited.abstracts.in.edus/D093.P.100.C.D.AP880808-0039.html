[1] Overcrowding and safety violations caused an aging ferry [2] to capsize in the Ganges River. [3] The ferry accident was said to be India's worst ever. [4] The ferry was overloaded with people and wheat [5] and lacked life jackets. [6] Authorities believed at least 300 people on the lower deck died and about 100 swam to safety. [7] The boat sank a short time [8] after it sailed about 100 yards from shore. [9] The boat trip is normally used by pilgrims crossing the river [10] to worship at a Hindu shrine. [11] Boat accidents are common during the monsoon season when rivers overflow their banks.
