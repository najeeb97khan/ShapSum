[1] The two largest property insurance companies in the Carolinas, State Farm and Nationwide, estimate Hurricane Hugo losses at $600 million, [2] making it the costliest hurricane ever. [3] Hugo, a Category 4 storm dubbed a "killer hurricane" by Charleston's mayor, [4] struck the city Thursday night. [5] Though there was significant damage, [6] only two were killed in Charleston, 17 total in South Carolina. [7] Officials credit the low number of fatalities to residents [8] who heeded Hurricane Center warnings [9] and evacuated before the storm's arrival. [10] Kingston, New York, assisted by Charleston during the Revolutionary War, [11] is repaying the kindness [12] by sending money [13] to help the city recover.
