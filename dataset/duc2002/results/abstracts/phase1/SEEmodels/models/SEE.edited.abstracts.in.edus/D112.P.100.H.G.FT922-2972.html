[1] About 20 banks were concerned last summer at the behavior of businesses [2] run by Robert Maxwell before his empire collapsed. [3] Although concerned about the impact of late payments, [4] the banks did not discuss those concerns with each other. [5] The banks were involved in a flurry of foreign exchange deals [6] in which the Maxwell Communications Corporation (MCC) [7] would buy currency in one financial center [8] while promising to make payment in another center. [9] Several times, MCC received the currency but didn't make the payment, [10] thereby giving themselves an unauthorized loan. [11] Last year, MCC made many repayments to banks by looting pension fund assets.
