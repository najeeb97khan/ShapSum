[1] Andrei D. Sakharov, 68-year-old physicist, human rights activist, and winner of the 1975 Nobel Peace Prize, died Thursday of an apparent heart attack in his Moscow apartment. [2] His widow is Yelena Bonner. [3] World leaders praised him as a pioneer [4] who helped change Soviet society, a force for freedom [5] who outlived his persecutors [6] and saw the liberty he championed take root throughout Eastern Europe. [7] He was once a national hero for his work on the Soviet hydrogen bomb [8] but later reviled for his uncompromising stands for human freedom. [9] Gorbachev freed him from seven years of internal exile. [10] The European Parliament gave Sakharov's name to its Human Rights Prize.
