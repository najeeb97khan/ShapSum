[1] Releasing a heart attack victim from the hospital as soon as three days after their attack are becoming more common with new medical treatments. [2] The early discharges are made possible with the introduction of tissue plasminogen activator, or TPA, [3] and balloon angioplasty. TPA, given right after a heart attack, can dissolve clots [4] while angioplasty can open up clogged arteries. [5] Patients sent home early can return to work earlier that those kept longer, [6] while the number of medical problems suffered by both groups after six months were similar. [7] The earlier release also has the benefit of significantly reducing medical costs.
