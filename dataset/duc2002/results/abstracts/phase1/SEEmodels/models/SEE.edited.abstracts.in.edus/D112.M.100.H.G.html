[1] British media entrepreneur Robert Maxwell, owner of the Mirror Group Newspaper Ltd. of Britain and publisher of Britain's Daily Mirror and the New York Daily News, [2] died in 1991 in the waters off Canary Island while on vacation. [3] His sons Kevin and Ian took over the business [4] but were soon implicated along with their father in an illegal scheme to deal with crushing debts. [5] Allegedly, unauthorized loans were acquired by using a Maxwell company [6] to buy currency in one financial center [7] while promising, but often failing, to make payments to another center. [8] Some repayments were made by looting pension funds.
