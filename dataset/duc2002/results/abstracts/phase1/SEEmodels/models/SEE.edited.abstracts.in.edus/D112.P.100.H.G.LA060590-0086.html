[1] British media entrepreneur Robert Maxwell, [2] who publishes Britain's Daily Mirror, [3] said Monday he is involved in negotiations [4] to buy a U.S. newspaper worth "hundreds of millions of dollars." [5] The newspaper was not identified [6] but there is speculation that it might be The New York Daily News, [7] which is struggling to restore profitability; [8] USA Today, which would mesh with Maxwell's efforts for the new paper The European; [9] or the Chicago Sun-Times that has been sliding in circulation since 1985. [10] Officials of each of these newspapers denied they were negotiating with Maxwell, [11] who recently bought three US tabloids: the Globe, National Examiner, and the Sun.
