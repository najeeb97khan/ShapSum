[1] Shahin Gobadi, spokesman for the People's Mojahedin of Iran charged [2] that the Iranian government is deliberately underreporting the number of casualties from the massive earthquake [3] that struck northern Iran. [4] He claims the government fears a political backlash [5] and is fearful of public outrage. [6] The government says that 36,000 have died but Gobadi's organization estimates the number closer to 100,000. [7] Gobadi said that shoddy housing [8] in which the government forced people [9] to live caused the widespread loss of life. [10] His listeners cheered and chanted [11] but spent little time expressing sympathy for the victims. [12] Gobadi's group helped depose the Shah, [13] but played no role organizing the Islamic republic.
