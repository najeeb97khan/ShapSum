[1] Experience of a reporter at Candlestick Park for the World Series the night of the 1989 San Francisco quake: [2] I was sitting in a trailer outside the stadium [3] when the earthquake hit. [4] I looked out [5] and the stadium was shaking. [6] A special concrete joint on top of the stadium was opening and closing like cardboard. [7] A few people were running out. [8] I walked into the stadium [9] to interview fans [10] and sensed little panic. [11] Most people stayed, waiting to hear [12] whether the game would be postponed. [13] When leaving, those in the upper deck seemed more shaken that those in lower levels.
