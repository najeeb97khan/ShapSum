[1] An experiment with more than 1700 high blood pressure patients showed [2] that a blood and urine test can pinpoint those patients most likely to have a heart attack. [3] The tests, which measure the activity of a blood protein called renin, [4] may also suggest a new risk factor for heart attacks. [5] The study was conducted on people found to have mild to moderately high blood pressure. [6] Only 12% of these people had high renin "profiles". [7] But 7 heart attacks in this small group constituted the highest incidents of heart attacks. [8] The study has implications for drug makers as well as patients.
