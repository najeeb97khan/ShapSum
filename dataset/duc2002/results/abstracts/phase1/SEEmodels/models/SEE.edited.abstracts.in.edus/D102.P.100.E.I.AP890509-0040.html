[1] Thousands of fans of Lucille Ball attended memorial services in three cities on Monday. [2] She had died on April 26. [3] More than 1,000 packed St. Monica's Catholic Church, [4] where fans had lined up as much as four hours early. [5] Rev. Robert Schuller asked the crowd to "applaud the Lord for Lucille Ball." [6] Diane Sawyer spoke at the service held at St. Ignatius Loyola Catholic Church in New York City, [7] which also drew about 1,000 people. [8] In Chicago, 200 gathered at Old St. Patrick Church. [9] The family scheduled the services for 8 p.m. Monday, the time most Americans had watched many of her shows.
