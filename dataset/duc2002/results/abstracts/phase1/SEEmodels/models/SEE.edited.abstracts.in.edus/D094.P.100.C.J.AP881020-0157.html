[1] Over several hours on Thursday Emperor Hirohito's blood pressure fell from 132 over 60 to 112 over 50 and his pulse dropped from 104 per minute to 84, [2] according to Imperial Household Agency spokesman Kenji Maeda. [3] Maeda said [4] the emperor's blood pressure has returned to its normal level, [5] but that the intestinal hemorrhaging that began a month ago resumed Thursday and the pace of the discharges was quickening. [6] Hirohito, 87, has been bedridden for a month. [7] He has received nearly 22 pints of blood [8] since his illness worsened Sept. 19. [9] A palace source said [10] he gets 600-700 calories a day, intravenously.
