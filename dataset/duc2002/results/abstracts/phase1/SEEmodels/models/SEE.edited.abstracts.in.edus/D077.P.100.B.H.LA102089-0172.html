[1] In San Francisco's 6.9 earthquake, buildings collapsed because of poor soil and structural design. [2] Chinatown's unreinforced masonry was thought vulnerable [3] but survived because of Chinatown's firm soil. [4] Telegraph, Nob, and Russian Hills, built on rock, escaped damage. [5] The Marina District, which lost 60 buildings, [6] is built on landfill dredged from the bay. [7] Most vulnerable were four-story apartment buildings with garages across the bottom, which pitched forward. [8] High-rises swayed with little damage [9] because of earthquake codes that demand construction to withstand an 8-magnitude quake with a San Francisco epicenter. [10] Earth and wind forces aren't considered as much with smaller buildings. [11] Elsewhere, Victorian homes shifted off foundations and chimneys crumbled.
