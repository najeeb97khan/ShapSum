[1] Hugo, a category 4 hurricane, struck South Carolina Thursday [2] after thousands of coastal residents in southeastern states fled inland. [3] A Red Cross spokesman said [4] that it was sheltering thousands of evacuees and resources were very strained. [5] A National Hurricane Center official said [6] hurricane-force winds first hit the coast by 9:15 p.m. EDT. [7] About three hours later the eye of the storm, 10 miles across and with top winds at 135 mph, [8] passed over the Isle of Palms east of Charleston. [9] By midnight, 119 mph winds were reported in Charleston. [10] The roof of City Hall, a 188- year-old brick building, cracked.
