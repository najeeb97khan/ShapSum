[1] Thunderstorms swept through eastern Ohio causing flooding [2] that killed at least ten people, [3] left many missing, [4] and drove hundreds from their home. [5] Ohio Governor Celeste declared a state of emergence [6] and surveyed the area. [7] Temporary morgues were set up at the Bellaire City Hospital and at a funeral home in Shadyside, a community of 4,300 about ten miles south of Wheeling, West Virginia. [8] Two creeks in this area [9] that empty into the Ohio River were the main area [10] where flooding occurred. [11] The Ohio Emergency Management Agency was assisted in coping with the emergency by other state agencies and the Red Cross.
