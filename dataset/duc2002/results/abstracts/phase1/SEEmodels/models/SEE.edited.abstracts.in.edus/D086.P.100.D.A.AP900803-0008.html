[1] Iraq's occupation of Kuwait yesterday has ended two weeks of tension and negotiations. [2] First, Saddam Hussein accused Kuwait of flooding the oil market and of stealing oil from wells along the disputed border. [3] Tension increased July 24-25 as the U.S. announced a "short-notice exercise" in the Persian Gulf, [4] Kuwait declared a state of alert, [5] and Iraq defied U.S. pressure. [6] Tension eased somewhat when Iraq and Kuwait agreed to meet for discussions, [7] but when they met on July 31 Iraq massed 100,000 troops on the border facing Kuwait's 20,000-strong armed forces. [8] The next day Iraq pulled out of the peace talks and yesterday invaded, [9] quickly occupying Kuwait.
