[1] A West German newspaper reported [2] that ousted East German leader Erich Honecker will not stand trial in East Germany as long as the formerly Communist country exists, [3] although he could be prosecuted in a united Germany for violation of property laws. [4] Honecker allegedly used $42 million for stocking a private housing estate for Communist government leaders. [5] However, the investigation is not far enough along [6] to determine whether charges would be filed against Honecker before the East German-West German merger. [7] He is under investigation for abuse of power, corruption, harboring terrorists, [8] and issuing shoot-to-kill orders against East Germans escaping to West Germany.
