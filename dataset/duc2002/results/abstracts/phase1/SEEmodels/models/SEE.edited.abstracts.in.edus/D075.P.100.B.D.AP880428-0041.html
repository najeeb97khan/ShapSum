[1] New treatments [2] that stop a heart attack in its tracks, [3] may help victims leave the hospital as early as three days after their attacks. [4] Early discharges were made possible by two medical advances, tissue plasminogen activator [5] (TPA) [6] and angioplasty. [7] TPA can dissolve clots in the heart's arteries [8] and minimize damage and angioplasty can clear arteries [9] by opening them with tiny balloons. [10] Hospital stays have been steadily shortening over the past decades as doctors learned [11] that prolonged inactivity caused patients to develop blood clots [12] and become demoralized. If 15% of heart attack patients were discharges after three days, [13] the annual savings would be $280 million.
