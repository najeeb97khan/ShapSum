[1] An overcrowded ferry sank Wednesday night [2] where the Ilisha, Tetulia, and Meghna Rivers meet in southern Bangladesh. [3] It went out of control while turning, [4] in 22 feet of water. [5] The ferry carried double its authorized capacity of 100. [6] People on the roof swam to safety but 100 drowned, including most of those in the hull. [7] Another ferry on the Meghna River, the M.V. Sagar, was attacked by river pirates Tuesday near Chandpur. [8] Fifty pirates boarded as passengers, [9] and shortly before midnight fired rifles [10] and hurled homemade bombs. [11] Two hundred passengers were wounded [12] and six thrown overboard. [13] The pirates took $83,000 [14] and escaped in waiting boats.
