[1] The annual Booker ballyhoo got going yesterday [2] when the judges for this year's #20,000 prize for British (and Commonwealth) fiction announced the shortlist. [3] The prize is no longer the richest literary award [4] but it guarantees great prestige and sales for the winner [5] and brings the novel to public attention. [6] Critics say the brouhaha distorts [7] the annual fiction industry and the intense media hype of the shortlisted authors does a disservice to other gifted but unlisted authors.
