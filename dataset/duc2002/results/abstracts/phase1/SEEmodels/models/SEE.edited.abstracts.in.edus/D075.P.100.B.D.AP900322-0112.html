[1] A drug that eases the heart's workload can cut the risk of new cardiac attacks and death by 20 percent in people [2] recovering from heart attacks. [3] The drug, Verapanil, a calcium channel blocker is about as effective as another variety of blood pressure medicines known as beta blockers at protecting people after heart attacks. [4] Since beta-blockers are generally not recommended for people with emphysema and asthma, [5] it is important to have alternatives. [6] Next, researchers plan [7] to study clot-dissolving drugs [8] to see what effect they have on unstable angina, a condition [9] in which people suffer spontaneous chest pain.
