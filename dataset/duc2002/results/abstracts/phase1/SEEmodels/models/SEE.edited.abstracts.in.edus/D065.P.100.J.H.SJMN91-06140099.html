[1] Huntington, Indiana, population 17,000, hosted [2] an exhibit in May honoring its most important resident ever, Vice-President Dan Quayle. [3] Quayle, 44 in February, grew up in a white, ranch-style house on Polk Street with parents James and Corinne and younger brother Chris. [4] He loves golf [5] and played on the high school team. [6] In 1972 Quayle was an aide to Indiana Gov. Edgar Whitcomb. Dan and wife Marilyn opened a joint law practice in Huntington. [7] His favorite restaurant is Nick's Kitchen. [8] Quayle is known as a nice, capable guy with good values, from a nice family that runs the local newspaper. [9] Quayle didn't attend the tribute, [10] citing a busy schedule.
