[1] New treatments are contributing to the decrease in deaths from heart attacks over the past decade. [2] Experts also credit the decline to healthier living habits, including fewer cigarettes and better food. [3] Aspirin has shown great promise, [4] cutting heart attack risk in half, [5] but for people who have clotting problems or high blood pressure, [6] aspirin seems to increase the risk of hemorrhagic stroke. [7] Other promising drugs include, diltiazen, [8] which can reduce the risk of a second heart attack, Verapanil, [9] which cuts the risk of new heart attacks and death by 20% in people recovering from heart attacks and a natural substance known as TGF [10] which appears to be able to limit damage to cardiac cells following a heart attack. [11] Another drug known as TPA dissolves clots in the heart's arteries [12] and is as effective for most patients as angioplasty. [13] The use of TPA may help heart attack victims leave the hospital in as early as three days. [14] Surgery bypassing one-third of the lower intestine reduces the amount of cholesterol [15] absorbed by the small intestine, [16] reducing the risk of second heart attacks and a blood and urine test, measuring a blood protein called renin, [17] can help to identify those at risk for a heart attack.
