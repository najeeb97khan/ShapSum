[1] The earthquake that hit Iran early Thursday exacted particularly large casualties [2] because people were asleep in fragile homes built on a flood plain of loosely deposited soil that shifts in an earthquake. [3] The quake that measured 7.7 on the Richter scale [4] hit a mountainous section of coastal Iran at 12:30 a.m. [5] and was the most powerful ever recorded in the area where two major plates of the earth's crust meet. [6] Most structures in the area are built of a ceramic-type brick or adobe that collapses easily. [7] At least 25,000 died and thousands were injured. [8] Thousands were buried in the rubble of their homes.
