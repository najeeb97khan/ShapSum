[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev returned to power [2] as the coup against him collapsed while he was vacationing in Crimea. [3] The coup unraveled swiftly [4] as the junta was unable to cope with thousands of people [5] who erected barricades around Yeltsin's stronghold, the Russian Parliament. [6] The national legislature demanded Gorbachev's reinstatement and nullified the coup leaders' decrees. [7] The Communist Party, seeking to salvage its image, denounced the takeover. [8] The coup people thought they could take over by neutralizing Gorbachev. [9] However, the real issue was not Gorbachev but the new political culture of freedom and democracy his reforms had created. [10] Gorbachev initiated the changes [11] but his standing was transformed when he was trumped by Russia's President Boris Yeltsin who, unlike Gorbachev, challenged the Communist Party and stayed close to the Russian people. [12] Gorbachev and Yeltsin at first appeared as equals while launching a purge of the Communist Party, [13] although Gorbachev balked at Yeltsin's proposals to ban the Communist Party. [14] Later, Yeltsin made it clear in the Russian Parliament [15] that Gorbachev has authority only if agreeing to share power [16] and intensify the march toward a democracy and a free economy. [17] There is speculation that Gorbachev orchestrated the coup [18] in order to ferret out the disloyal.
