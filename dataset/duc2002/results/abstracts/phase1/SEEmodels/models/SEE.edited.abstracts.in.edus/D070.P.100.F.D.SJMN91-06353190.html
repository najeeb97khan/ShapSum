[1] Michail Gorbachev urged all sides [2] to take their time in deciding the fate of Erich Honecker [3] as former East German leader spent a seventh day of refuge inside the Chilean Embassy in Moscow. [4] Germany wants to try Honecker for ordering the shooting of people [5] who attempted to flee to the West during his years in power. [6] But speculation lingers [7] that German politicians have little interest in trying a man [8] who may possess embarrassing secrets. [9] Gorbachev did not exclude the possibility [10] that Honecker would leave for North Korea in the next few days. [11] Outside the Chilean Embassy, several dozen demonstrators demanded humane treatment for Honecker.
