[1] In December 1993 the Foreign Ministers of Russia and The United Kingdom spoke out about the seriousness and the consequences of the ethnic and political conflicts in the former Soviet Union. [2] They agreed that the disputes are a legitimate concern of Russians [3] who are concerned about clashes close to their borders, [4] and about the welfare of ethnic Russians living within the borders of the now independent states that had been part of the Soviet Union. [5] Europe and the entire international community should share those concerns for humanitarian reasons and [6] because continuing conflicts risk promoting regional instability and threats to European security.
