[1] Hurricane Hugo advanced on Georgia and South Carolina today [2] as 150,000 coastal residents fled inland on jammed highways. [3] The leading edge of Hugo with winds up to 125 mph from 105 mph yesterday, is likely to hit between Savannah, Ga. And Charleston, S.C. any time after 8 p.m. [4] A hurricane warning was in effect between Fernandina Beach Fla., and Oregon Inlet, N.C. In South Carolina Gov. Campbell sent 400 National Guardsmen to help evacuate coastal counties [5] where about one-fifth of the state's 3.1 million residents live. [6] In Georgia Gov. Harris declared a state of emergency in six counties and 142,000 were advised to leave their homes.
