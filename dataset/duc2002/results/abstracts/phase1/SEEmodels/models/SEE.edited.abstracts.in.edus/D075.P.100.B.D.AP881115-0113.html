[1] A study found [2] that if heart attack victims quickly receive clot-dissolving drugs, [3] they usually don't need the extra step of a common balloon procedure, [4] known as angioplasty. [5] The clot-dissolving factor known as TPA, [6] dramatically improved the survival chances of heart attack victims. [7] When groups [8] who underwent angioplasty were compared to groups [9] who received TPA [10] and only those with lingering heart pain or other symptoms underwent angioplasty, there was no difference in survival rates. [11] The results mean [12] that most patients can be treated in community hospitals [13] where angioplasty is not available and that the nation could save $704 million in medical bills.
