[1] A ferry carrying 600 Somalis struck a reef [2] and sank Friday off Kenya, near the tourist resort of Malindi. [3] One hundred thirty bodies were recovered [4] but 50 more were believed still trapped. [5] Most who drowned were on the lower deck. [6] First word of the accident came when survivors staggered into a hotel, which sent rescue boats. [7] The ferry was one of a stream of vessels bringing Somali refugees to Kenya [8] after rebels overthrew Somali President Siad Barre in late January. [9] The month-long fighting had largely stopped [10] but refugees continued to flee to Kenya. [11] Eleven Somalis drowned when a boat capsized north of Malindi on February 1st.
