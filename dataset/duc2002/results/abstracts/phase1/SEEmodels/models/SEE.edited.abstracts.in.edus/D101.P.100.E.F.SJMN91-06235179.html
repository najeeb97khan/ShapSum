[1] US intelligence officers warned the White House a week before the Soviet coup of a "real possibility" that hard-liners in the KGB [2] or Soviet military might try to block the signing of the new Union Treaty. [3] The proposed treaty reduces Moscow's centralized control [4] and strengthens powers of the 15 Soviet Republics. [5] Soviet President Gorbachev was reinstated after the bungled coup attempt but may become "irrelevant" and a figurehead under the new treaty. [6] The coup's failure may open the way to further reform [7] as the Communist Party becomes less of a political force. [8] However, a benign Soviet Union cannot be automatically assumed.
