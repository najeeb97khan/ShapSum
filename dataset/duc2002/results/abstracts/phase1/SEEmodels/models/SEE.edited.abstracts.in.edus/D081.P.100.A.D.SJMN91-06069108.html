[1] A one-day strike by coal miners in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine has grown into a nationwide walkout involving up to 100,000 miners demanding [2] that President Gorbachev surrender power to the restive republics. [3] Most of the country's 1.2 million coal miners are still working, [4] but output in some key regions has been halved. [5] The strike is the new union's first major test of strength. [6] It began as a warning in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine [7] but gradually spread to all of the country's major regions. [8] Previous strikes have made only economic demands. [9] This time the miners want political concessions.
