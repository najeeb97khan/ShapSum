[1] Nelson Mandela joined the African National Congress in 1944 [2] and participated in black protests and civil disobedience opposing the apartheid government of South Africa. [3] He was arrested several times, [4] and in 1964, was given a life sentence for conspiring to overthrow the government. [5] Many countries pressured South Africa [6] to end its all-white rule, [7] and Mandela's imprisonment became a cause celebre. [8] In 1988, Mandela's 70th birthday was celebrated outside South Africa. [9] Northeastern University planned to award an honorary degree to Mandela. [10] Rumors began to spread that South Africa would release Mandela to gain international support. [11] President Botha would do so if he renounced violence. [12] Mandela refused. [13] Mandela contracted tuberculosis in 1988. [14] After being cured, he was moved to better quarters [15] and was permitted visits from family and even ANC leaders. [16] Mandela was released in 1990, [17] as world leaders cheered. [18] He visited New York City [19] and received accolades from politicians and black leaders. [20] However, American Jewish leaders were hesitant [21] since he had compared the Palestinian struggle to that in South Africa. [22] Mandela was among black leaders negotiating with President de Klerk [23] to end apartheid. [24] His task was complicated by violent factions among blacks and by his wife, Winnie, [25] being imprisoned for a violent crime.
