[1] Lucille Ball, TV's comic genius, [2] died at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center at the age of 77 following emergency heart surgery. [3] She earned her niche in television history in the comedy "I Love Lucy", [4] in which her husband, Desi Arnaz also starred. [5] The weekly show which premiered in 1951, [6] had a phenomenal 40 million viewers and department stores even changed the night [7] they remained open to accommodate the show. [8] In addition to being a talented entertainer, [9] Miss Ball was a shrewd businesswoman [10] who became along with her husband, [11] head of one of Hollywood's major studios. [12] The couple divorced in 1960.
