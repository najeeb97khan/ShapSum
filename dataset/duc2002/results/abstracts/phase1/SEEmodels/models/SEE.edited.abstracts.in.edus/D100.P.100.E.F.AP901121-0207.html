[1] John Lennon had been in virtual seclusion for five years, [2] taking care of his son, Sean, [3] and, admittedly out of songwriting ideas. [4] But, in 1980, he again felt inspired [5] and returned to the studio [6] to make an album. [7] Musicians who worked with Lennon on what were to be his final two albums considered him to be a hero. [8] Songs that the former Beatle wrote for these albums were mostly mainstream pop; no political anthems. [9] He was a happy man; his singing was higher and sweeter than before. [10] He wrote songs [11] that were tributes to his wife, Yoko Ono, [12] and to Sean.
