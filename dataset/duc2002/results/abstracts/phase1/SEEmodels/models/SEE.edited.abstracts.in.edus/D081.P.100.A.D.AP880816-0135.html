[1] More than 4,000 workers at a coal mine in Jastrzebie, Poland went on strike today [2] to demand legalization of Solidarity, higher wages and the reinstatement of workers [3] fired for union activities. [4] Workers at a copper mine had staged a protest rally earlier. [5] The strike comes on the eighth anniversary of the founding of the outlawed trade federation. [6] Solidarity was crushed in 1981, [7] when the government imposed martial law [8] and was banned nearly a year later by parliament. [9] In 1980 workers at factories around the port of Gdansk joined striking shipyard workers to form Solidarity, the first and only independent trade federation in the Soviet bloc.
