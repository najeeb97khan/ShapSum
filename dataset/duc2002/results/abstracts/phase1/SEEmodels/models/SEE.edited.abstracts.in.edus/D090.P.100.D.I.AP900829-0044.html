[1] Philippine President Corazon Aquino said [2] today she is willing to negotiate a ceasefire with Communist guerrillas and former soldiers, [3] reversing a long held policy. [4] The government blames almost daily explosions in Manila on the former soldiers and the Communists have been waging a 21-year rebellion. [5] Aquino rejected a call for negotiations in July, [6] but accepted urging on Tuesday by a pro-Aquino senator [7] to begin discussions. [8] Juan Ponce Enrile, [9] implicated in a 1986 coup attempt, [10] said he is willing to reconcile. [11] The last ceasefire with the Communists collapsed in early 1987. [12] Defense Secretary Fidel Ramos, [13] who is credited with crushing six coup attempts, [14] has refused any talks.
