[1] The engineer of a ferry [2] that sank off a Red Sea port, [3] leaving more than 400 people missing, [4] denied accusations of widespread negligence on the part of the ship's crew. [5] Investigators were looking into complaints from some survivors [6] that the crew abandoned the ferry without helping passengers and that some crew members pushed passengers aside to get to the lifeboats. [7] The ship's engineer acknowledged misconduct by some crew members, [8] but said the survival of only 10 of the 71 member crew, [9] indicated negligence was not widespread. [10] The ferry hit a reef and quickly sank. [11] The ferry was returning to Egypt from Jiddah, Saudi Arabia.
