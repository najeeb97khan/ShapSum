[1] The Boston Marathon is becoming a reckless speed duel and the last three have produced five of the top 10 times in the race's history. [2] They also have resulted in a race of attrition, [3] with many of the early speedsters faltering in the late stages. [4] Last year's winner, Gelindo Bordin, ran a patient, calculating race, [5] passing thee race leaders at the 21 mile mark [6] and winning in 2 hours, 8 minutes, 19 seconds, the second fastest time in the race's history. [7] Foremost among the women [8] entrants is Ingrid Kristiansen, [9] who holds the world record of 2:21:06 [10] and who won in Boston in 1986 and 1989.
