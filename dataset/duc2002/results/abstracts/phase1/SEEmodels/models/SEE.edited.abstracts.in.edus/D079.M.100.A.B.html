[1] Gilbert, a killer Category 5 hurricane with sustained winds over 155mph and a record low barometric pressure of 26.13 inches, was the most intense hurricane ever recorded in the western hemisphere. [2] At least 109 people died and damage is estimated at $8 billion. [3] It caused limited damage in the Dominican Republic, Haiti and Cuba, [4] but extensive death and damage in Jamaica [5] where 100,000 of the country's 500,000 homes were destroyed. [6] It then inflicted severe damage to the Yucatan Peninsula resort areas of Cancun and Cozumel [7] before weakening and moving on to the Texas-Mexico border. [8] In Texas it caused flooding [9] and spawned dozens of tornadoes.
