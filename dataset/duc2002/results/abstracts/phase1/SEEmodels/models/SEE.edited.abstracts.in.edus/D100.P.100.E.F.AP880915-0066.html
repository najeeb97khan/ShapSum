[1] A biography, The Lives of John Lennon, by Albert Goldman portrays the late Beatle as a violent, anorexic, bisexual, [2] drug-addicted drunk. [3] Lennon's widow, the normally reclusive Yoko Ono, has called the work "totally fiction" in her first broadcast comments on the book. [4] Goldman calls Ono a gold-digger [5] who snorted heroin [6] and who was responsible for Paul McCartney's 1980 marijuana bust in Japan. [7] Ono acknowledged having a heroin problem during the late 1970s, [8] but denied Goldman's assertion [9] that it was a $5,000-a-week habit. [10] Ono's interview was taped at the same New York apartment [11] (at the Dakorta) where she and Lennon lived.
