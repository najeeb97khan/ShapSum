[1] The Iraqi occupation of Kuwait began on August 2 [2] when masses of combat-experienced troops poured across the border, [3] quickly taking the country. [4] The Kuwait emir fled to Saudi Arabia. [5] The dispute began in mid-July [6] when Iraq, a nation with 17 million, [7] accused tiny Kuwait [8] of stealing oil and flooding the market. [9] As Iraqi troops mobilized, efforts to avoid a confrontation failed. [10] While Iraqi said it invaded at the request of Kuwaiti revolutionaries, [11] a Kuwaiti spokesman called this a false pretext. [12] President Bush said he would defend U.S. interests in the Persian Gulf. [13] Iran, which fought an 8-year war with Iraq, rejoiced [14] that the world saw the "Frankenstein" created by the United States and Kuwait. [15] The United Nations Security Council unanimously passed trade and military sanctions against Iraq and Kuwait. [16] Most nations strongly enforced these sanctions, [17] as oil prices soared. [18] Iraq rounded-up foreign nationals in Kuwait, including passengers of a British airliner, [19] and took many to Iraq. [20] An Iraqi jet finally brought some to Jordan on August 7. [21] Refugees poured into Saudi Arabia. [22] Some reported that Iraqis were moving in [23] and being given Kuwaiti identities. [24] Iraq began evacuation drills [25] and warned of U.S. or Israeli actions.
