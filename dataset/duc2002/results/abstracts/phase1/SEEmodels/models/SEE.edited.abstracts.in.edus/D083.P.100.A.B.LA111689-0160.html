[1] In Huntsville, Ala., a tornado slammed into a shopping district, a residential area and a school Wednesday, [2] killing at least 14 people, [3] injuring nearly 300 [4] and trapping many others in their cars and homes. [5] It touched down between 4 and 4:30 p.m., [6] when city streets were filled with rush-hour traffic. [7] One witness said [8] that it started picking up cars and strewing them around... heavy overhead power lines started snapping just like a whip. [9] Telephone communication and electricity were disrupted. [10] Only one radio station remained broadcasting. [11] Rescuers are digging in the rubble. [12] State police were called from around the state to help.
