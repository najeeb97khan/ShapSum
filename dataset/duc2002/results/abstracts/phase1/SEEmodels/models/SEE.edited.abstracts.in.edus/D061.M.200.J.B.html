[1] Tropical Storm Gilbert strengthened into an eastern Caribbean hurricane on Saturday night, September 10, 1988. [2] Government officials of the island nations in its westerly path issued alerts, warnings and orders to evacuate their southerly-exposed coastal areas. [3] Puerto Rico, Haiti and the Dominican escaped with only some coastal flooding and a few deaths [4] before Gilbert continued on to strike Jamaica with 110mph winds and torrential rains. [5] The storm, now one of the largest systems seen in the Caribbean for a long time, [6] slowly traversed the length of the island causing massive damage and nineteen deaths. [7] 100,000 of Jamaica's 500,000 homes were destroyed leaving 500,000 people homeless. [8] The storm then passed over the Cayman Islands located south of Cuba. [9] Cuba and our American military bases there both avoided the brunt of the storm [10] before it slammed full force into the Yucatan Peninsula. [11] By now Gilbert had strengthened into the most severe hurricane [12] ever recorded with barometric pressure of 26.31 inches [13] and sustained winds of 179mph, gusting to 218mph. [14] Relief efforts for the heavily damaged Cancun and Cozumel areas were hindered due to massive flooding and power and phone outages. [15] Scientists do not fully understand why some minor tropical storms strengthen into hurricanes, [16] while others do not.
