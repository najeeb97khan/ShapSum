[1] Poland's Solidarity Government's first definitive statement on the issue of a reunified German state said such a state "must be formed gradually [2] and respect Poland's borders". Some right-wing West German politicians have called for recovering the 40,000 square miles of territory [3] that Germany lost to Poland at the end of World War II. [4] The territory is about one-third of present-day Poland. [5] East and West Germany have each separately recognized the present border, [6] but there has been no official assurance that a reunified Germany would. [7] Poland's Foreign Minister Skubiszewski reassured Poles [8] that its government will insist on current borders.
