[1] The Broadway theatrical community said goodbye Thursday to Leonard Bernstein at the Majestic Theater. [2] The public started lining up at 7 a.m. to join invited friends, [3] relatives and theatrical personalities. [4] Arthur Laurents, the librettist for "West Side Story", was host. [5] Other collaborators, original cast members, and old friends, several coming from California, spoke and performed. [6] His son, Alexander, [7] delivered an affectionate talk, [8] while Michael Wager, a very close friend at the end, [9] spoke movingly about Bernstein's final illness. [10] The program closed with Marilyn Horne singing "Somewhere", the emotional anthem from "West Side Story." [11] A sustained standing ovation ended the evening.
