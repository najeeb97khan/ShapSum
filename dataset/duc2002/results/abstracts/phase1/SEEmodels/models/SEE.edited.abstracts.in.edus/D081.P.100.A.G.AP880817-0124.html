[1] Port workers in Szczecin and miners at the Morcinek mine declared sympathy strikes in support of strikers at the nearby July Manifesto mine in Jastrzebie [2] who are demanding the legalization of Solidarity and raised pay wages. [3] The strikes mark the eighth anniversary of the birth of Solidarity, [4] which Communist authorities crushed in 1989. [5] Miners, who earn nearly twice the average national salary, [6] base their wage demands on hardships [7] caused by Poland's worsening economy.
