[1] President Bush is expected to name a female ambassador to Nicaragua in view of the election of president Violeta Chamorro. [2] Front runner is Melissa Wells, 57, currently ambassador to Mozambique. [3] Wells was born in Estonia, [4] has no prior experience in Latin America, [5] but speaks Spanish. [6] The US and Nicaragua have not had ambassadors in each other's capitals since July 1988, [7] when the Sandinista government expelled US diplomats on charges [8] of interfering in Nicaragua's internal affairs. [9] Wells' 1986 nomination as ambassador to Mozambique was held up for 11 months by Senator Jesse Helms, [10] who wanted to pressure the administration [11] into dealing with rightist guerrillas opposed to Mozambique's Marxist government.
