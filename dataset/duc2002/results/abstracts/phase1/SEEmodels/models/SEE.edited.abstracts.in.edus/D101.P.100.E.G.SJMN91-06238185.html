[1] A specialist in Soviet affairs has written an article asking the question: [2] "Did Mikhail Gorbachev orchestrate the coup that almost overthrew him? " [3] Some views supporting this conspiracy theory are: [4] Gorbachev ignored clear warnings about attempts to seize power; [5] his description of his three days under arrest in the Crimea invites the question of why his 32 guards didn't act against the opposition delegation [6] and why weren't they incapacitate; [7] he may have sided with the conspirators [8] but waited out self-imposed confinement. [9] His motives were to ferret out disloyal elements, make himself a hero, and delay signing of a treaty lessening his powers.
