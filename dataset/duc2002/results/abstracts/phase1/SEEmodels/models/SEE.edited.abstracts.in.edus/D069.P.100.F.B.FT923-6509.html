[1] The Bundesbank, Germany's independent central bank, is resisting political pressure from the German government and the governments and central banks of Europe [2] to cut interest rates [3] in order to ease large German deficits and inflationary pressures. [4] The European currency bloc is overvalued against the dollar and yen as a result of the Bundesbank's policies. [5] Very high real interest rates threaten to turn a European recession into a depression. [6] European governments believe [7] the Bundesbank's intransigence is threatening nearly 10 years of effort for greater European economic and political integration and urge it [8] to become more responsive to broader European interests.
