[1] The earthquake that struck Iran reminded a US geophysicist of the 1988 quake in Armenia. [2] The Iran quake was of magnitude 7.7 [3] while the Armenian was 6.9 but both killed about 25,000 persons and for essentially the same reasons: [4] most victims were in houses [5] that were shoddily built [6] or otherwise fragile and could collapse easily. [7] The death toll in Iran was high [8] because it happened at night [9] when most people were asleep. [10] This quake was the strongest ever recorded in Iran, [11] which is in a very active seismic region [12] and has recorded 12 quakes of magnitude 7 in the past 30 years.
