[1] Torrential thunderstorms sent a flash flood into the Ohio River town of Shadyside, [2] killing at least 11 people, [3] leaving 51 missing and scores homeless Friday, [4] authorities said. [5] Also, about 200 people were evacuated in central Ohio. [6] The Federal Emergency Management Agency declared Belmont County, [7] which includes Shadyside, and Jefferson and Franklin counties disaster areas, [8] making federal aid available to residents. [9] The Ohio River and creeks around Shadyside are being searched for survivors and bodies. [10] Al Wheeler, deputy meteorologist in the Cleveland office of the National Weather Service, said [11] a flood watch, but not a warning, was issued before the disaster.
