[1] Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker was arrested Monday morning morning upon release from hospital and taken to prison. [2] Honecker, [3] who expected to be indicted for high treason, [4] was immediately taken to East Berlin's Rummelsburg prison. [5] He had previously been declared to ill to withstand imprisonment. [6] Honecker, the aging hard-line Stalinist [7] who ruled East Germany for 18 years, [8] had recently undergone surgery for a malignant kidney tumor. [9] Earlier this month, East German prosecutors said Honecker and former state security chief Erich Mielke would be charged with treason and corruption for misuse of their positions and state funds.
