[1] As recent actions by East Germany make German reunification more likely, [2] many policy wonks point out serious problems to be overcome. [3] A new structure or relationship between NATO and the Warsaw Pact may be needed. [4] Theodore Sorenson, a top aide to JFK, states [5] that the U.S. couldn't afford a united Germany outside the Western Alliance and the Soviets can't afford one inside the Western Alliance. [6] He hopes [7] unification can be forged within a reunited Europe. [8] William Hyland, a former aide to Henry Kissinger, and former National Security Advisor, Zbigniew Brzezinski, expressed similar concerns about conflicting European, U.S. and Soviet interests.
