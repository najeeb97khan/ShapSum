[1] A one-day coal miners' strike in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine has spread to all of the country's major coal regions. [2] It is the new union's first major test of strength. [3] The larger 1989 strike made only economic demands. [4] The government has not kept promises [5] to raise miners' living standards and this time [6] they also want three major political concessions. [7] First, "real sovereignty" for the 15 Soviet Republics; second, more power for the Federation Council which includes republic presidents; [8] and finally, they want a "low on property" defining what belongs to the government [9] and allow private ownership of land and industry, including some mines.
