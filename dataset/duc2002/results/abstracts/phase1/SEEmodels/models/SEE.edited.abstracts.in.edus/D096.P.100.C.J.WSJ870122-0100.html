[1] New, tougher tax laws on what can be claimed [2] as business entertainment expense haven't slowed demand for tickets to Sunday's Super Bowl XXI. [3] Now, only 80% of the face value to tickets [4] to sporting events can be deducted, [5] and then only if a substantial business discussion occurs. [6] The face value of a 50-yard-line seat at the Super Bowl is $75, not $1500, the going rate. [7] Meals are 80% deductible if business is discussed. [8] Still, hundreds of companies will send their officials and customers to Pasadena for a weekend of partying. [9] The IRS will have difficulty disputing claims [10] that business was discussed.
