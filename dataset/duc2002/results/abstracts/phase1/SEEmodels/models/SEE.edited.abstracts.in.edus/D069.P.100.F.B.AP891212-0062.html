[1] Since East Germans began agitating for an end to Communist domination two months ago, [2] public pressure has forced the opening of the Berlin Wall [3] and garnered the promise of free elections. [4] However, the issue of reunification could fracture the popular East German movement. [5] Although the Communists admit [6] that East Germany's socialist system has failed, [7] many East German's are seeking a new "democratic socialism" within a multi-party system. [8] They favor a type of partnership with West Germany rather than reunification. [9] Pro and anti reunification rallies came shortly after the four victorious World War II Allies met to discuss the latest East German developments.
