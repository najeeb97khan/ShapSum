[1] Spike Lee, in a lecture and question-and-answer session Monday at Syracuse University, shrugged off the icy treatment of his latest movie by the Academy Award voters. [2] Widely hailed by critics, the 1989 film "Do the Right Thing," [3] received only two Academy Award nominations [4] and was not recommended for best picture or best director. [5] Lee commented [6] that the academy's membership consists mainly of "old people" [7] more comfortable with the black chauffeur in "Driving Miss Daisy" than with the angry characters in his film. [8] The students warmly welcomed Lee. [9] As one student said, [10] "He's an African American" and he's addressing the issues "affecting our community."
