[1] Lucille Ball was mourned Wednesday in her western New York hometown [2] she left more than 50 years ago. [3] Miss Ball died earlier in Los Angeles. [4] Pauline Lopus recalled that Miss Ball dreamed of fame, [5] worked as an amusement park barker at age 15, [6] and appeared in school plays. [7] After becoming a model, [8] Miss Ball moved to Hollywood in 1933. [9] She kept contacts in Jamestown [10] and was going to visit on May 20 [11] to receive an honorary degree from the community college [12] and have a street renamed for her in nearby Celoron, [13] where she grew up. [14] These honors now will be awarded posthumously.
