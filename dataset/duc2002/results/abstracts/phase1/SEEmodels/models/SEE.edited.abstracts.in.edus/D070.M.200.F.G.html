[1] Former East German leader Erich Honecker was under house arrest outside East Berlin [2] since being ousted in October 1989. Honecker and other party officials have been investigated for corruption and treason. [3] After hospitalization for kidney surgery, [4] he was arrested and imprisoned to await trial. [5] Judged too ill for imprisonment [6] but fit enough for prosecution, [7] he was placed in a Soviet East Berlin hospital. [8] In August, Honecker was interrogated about his role in the killing of some 200 people trying to escape from East Berlin. [9] A murder charge would put him away for life. [10] His trial was delayed because of East German-West German merger negotiations. [11] In December, Soviet authorities refused to return him to Germany claiming [12] it would "burden" Moscow and Bonn's "outstanding relations." [13] In December 1991, Honecker took refuge in the Chilean Embassy in Moscow. [14] Gorbachev was his protector until his political fortunes declined [15] and he ordered Honecker to leave the country. [16] He was tried and imprisoned in Germany in early 1992 [17] but was released in 1993. [18] He traveled to Santiago, Chile to join his daughter [19] who had married a Chilean. [20] He lived with his wife in a wealthy Santiago area [21] where he died in 1994 at age 81.
