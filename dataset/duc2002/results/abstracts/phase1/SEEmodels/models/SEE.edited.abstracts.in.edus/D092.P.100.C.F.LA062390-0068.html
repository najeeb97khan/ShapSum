[1] The US government has joined with private humanitarian groups [2] to send aid to earthquake-ravaged Iran. [3] The government gave $300,000 of supplies, including hard hats, leather gloves, face masks, wool blankets, and tents. [4] Delivery of the goods would probably be by private charters rather than military transports [5] which would normally do the job. [6] Death count from the earthquake stands at 29,000 and about 400,000 are homeless. [7] Charitable organizations across are collecting money for Iranian relief. [8] The American Jewish World Service is in a quandary wondering [9] if they should provide assistance [10] since Iran has excluded Israel as a source of aid.
