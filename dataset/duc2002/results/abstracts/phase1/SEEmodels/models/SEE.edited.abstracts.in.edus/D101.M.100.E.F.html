[1] Soviet hard-liners staged a coup [2] in order to prevent implementation of Gorbachev's reforms. [3] Some viewers suspected a Gorbachev role in the coup as a ploy [4] to solidify his position. [5] The coup ended after three days due mainly to opposition by Boris Yeltsin [6] who rallied thousands in opposition. [7] Coup leaders were arrested [8] and Gorbachev was returned to the presidency. [9] Gorbachev still hoped to implement reforms [10] working through the Communist Party. [11] Yeltsin, however, had quit the party [12] and wanted to see its influence lessened. [13] Yeltsin was clearly moving into a greater leadership role in the new Russia.
