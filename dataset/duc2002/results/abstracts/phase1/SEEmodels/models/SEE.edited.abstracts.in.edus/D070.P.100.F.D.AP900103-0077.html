[1] Former East German leader, Erich Honecker, [2] may be moved to a monastery [3] to protect him from a possible lynching by enraged citizens. [4] He is under investigation for corruption [5] and living in luxury at the cost of the state. [6] He was placed under house arrest in December 1989. [7] Both Lutheran and Catholic churches have offered Honecker refuge. [8] It was the churches that became venues for pro-democracy groups [9] that eventually brought the overthrow of Honecker's 18 year leadership. [10] Recently, East German groups have called for punishing Honecker [11] and moving him to smaller quarters. [12] The current leadership fears [13] that in such a situation, Honecker might be attacked.
