[1] In the midst of tough economic times, McDonald's opened its second restaurant in Moscow as part of a 12 story office block. [2] As the value of the ruble plunges, Russian officials had reason to celebrate McDonald's hamburgers. [3] Although Russian authorities have been pleased with McDonald's, [4] citing the fact that most of their supplies come from Russia, the plunging ruble as well as high inflation and changing tax laws have proven difficult for McDonald's. [5] McDonald's officials admitted [6] that they do not know when they will recoup their original 50M investment. [7] McDonald's hopes to generate hard currency by renting out offices to western companies [8] to augment the rubles made from selling hamburgers.
