[1] Ousted East German leader, Erich Honecker will not stand trial in East Germany as long as the formerly communist country exists. [2] Honecker could be prosecuted in a united Germany, however, for violation of property laws. [3] Honecker is accused of using 42 million [4] to stock a private housing estate for leaders of the former Communist government. [5] Since being ousted in October 1989, [6] he remains confined in a Soviet hospital outside Berlin in poor health. [7] He is under investigation for abuse of power, corruption, [8] harboring terrorists [9] and issuing shoot to kill orders to prevent East Germans from escaping to West Germany.
