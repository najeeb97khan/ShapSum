[1] Physicist Andrei D. Sakharov became the youngest member of the Soviet Academy of Sciences [2] and helped develop the Soviet hydrogen bomb, [3] but urged limiting its production and testing. [4] He was a prominent 1970s dissident [5] and won the 1975 Nobel Peace Prize. [6] In January 1980, after Sakharov criticized Soviet intervention in Afghanistan, Breshnev exiled him to Gorky. [7] Gorbachev allowed Sakharov and his wife Yelena Bonner to return to Moscow in December 1986. [8] There they lived simply in a two-room, seventh-floor apartment [9] which became a mecca for Soviets seeking aid. [10] Sakharov supported Gorbachev's reforms [11] but called for speedier progress in human rights, political prisoner liberation, and a nuclear testing ban. [12] In November 1988 he visited the US, underwent medical tests, [13] and promoted a world peace foundation. [14] In April 1989, he was elected to parliament after Soviet physicists voted secretly to nominate him. [15] He warned of national economic catastrophe. [16] Sakharov died alone in his Moscow apartment in December 1989 at the age of 68, of an apparent heart attack, hours [17] after urging creation of an opposition party. [18] World and Soviet leaders and press eulogized him as a sincere man [19] who suffered great injustice [20] and helped change Soviet society. [21] 45,000 grievers filed past Sakharov's body. [22] Sakharov had four children and four grandchildren.
