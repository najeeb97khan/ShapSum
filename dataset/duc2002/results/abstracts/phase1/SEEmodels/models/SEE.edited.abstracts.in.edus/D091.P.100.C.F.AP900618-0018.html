[1] The death toll from flash floods in eastern Ohio rose to 20, including five children. [2] Sixteen people remain missing. [3] Dog teams from four states aided in the search for the missing and workers [4] dug into areas where the dogs detected human scent. [5] Most of the search was concentrated along the creek banks [6] but divers also searched in the Ohio River. [7] Cars were found deeply buried under debris along the creek banks. [8] The National Guard cleared roads and bridges into the damaged area. [9] West Virginia also had millions of dollars of damage [10] but no deaths or serious injuries were reported.
