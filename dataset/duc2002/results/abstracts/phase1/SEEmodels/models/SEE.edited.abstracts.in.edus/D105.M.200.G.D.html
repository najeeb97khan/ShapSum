[1] The collapse of the Soviet Union was accompanied by major civil unrest and ethnic conflict in the former republics. [2] The Caucasus, Baltic and Central Asian republics all experienced unrest. [3] The reasons for the strife included nationalism, ethnic tensions and old grievances. [4] One of the most serious conflicts was in the Nagorno-Karabakh region of Azerbaijan. [5] The majority of the population there is Armenian [6] and wanted unification with Armenia, Azerbaijan refused. [7] The conflict between the Azeris and the Armenians has existed for centuries. [8] Other conflicts were a direct result of the breakup of the Soviet Union. [9] In Moldova, the ethnic Romanian majority wanted to establish Moldova, which is close to Romanian as the official language. [10] The Russian minority dissented [11] and demanded its own autonomous republic. [12] The Baltic republics wanted to be independent nations [13] and many nationalities were demanding the return of lands [14] taken from them under Stalin. [15] Gorbachev tried to hold the Soviet Union together through tougher legislation and by appeals for support for his reforms. [16] Following the collapse of the Soviet Union there was even an attempt at peacekeeping in Georgia, [17] using Russian and Georgian troops. [18] There was even one call for an international peacekeeping mission in some of the hotspots.
