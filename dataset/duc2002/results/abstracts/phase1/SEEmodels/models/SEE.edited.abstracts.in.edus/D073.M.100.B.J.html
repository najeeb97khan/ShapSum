[1] Pinatubo began erupting about two weeks ago, after six centuries of dormancy. [2] Located 10 miles from Clark AFB, the explosions have become increasingly severe. [3] The U.S. has denied the possibility of radioactive contamination [4] if volcanic debris hits the weapons depots at Clark AFB, [5] which was evacuated a week ago. [6] At least four dead and 24 injured are attributed to the eruptions. [7] About 84,000 have been evacuated. [8] Heavy rains today have raised fears of catastrophic mudflows. [9] According to Foreign Secretary Manglapus, [10] the eruptions should not effect the stalled talks with the U.S. on new Clark and Subic lease agreements.
