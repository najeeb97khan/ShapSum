[1] Saddam [2] accused Kuwait of costing Iraq billions by exceeding OPEC production quotas [3] thus driving down oil prices. [4] He further accused Kuwait of "stealing" Iraqi oil from a common oil field on their border. [5] He then invaded Kuwait, [6] claiming to do so at the request of Kuwaiti revolutionary coup leaders. [7] The United States led the western world and the United Nations in calling the coup a sham, denouncing this invasion [8] and imposing strong economic and military sanctions on Iraq in an attempt [9] to get them to withdraw. [10] Oil prices surged [11] while Saddam warned against foreign intervention. [12] The U.S. dispatched warships to the area.
