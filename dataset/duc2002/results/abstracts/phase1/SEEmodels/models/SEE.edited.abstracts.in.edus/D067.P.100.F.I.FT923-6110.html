[1] Damage from Hurricane Andrew could rise to $20 billion, [2] making it one of the costliest this century. [3] It is expected to make landfall soon and officials in Mississippi, Louisiana, and Texas have ordered two million people [4] to evacuate and many refineries are closed. [5] Fifteen people have been killed [6] and damage in Florida is estimated to be $15 to $20 billion. [7] Homestead and the nearby air force base were virtually flattened. [8] The U.S. insurance industry will take the brunt of the losses. [9] Primary insurers include the State Farm Group, Allstate, Hartford Insurance, Aetna, and Travellers. [10] The last serious hurricane caused $4.2 billion in insured losses in 1989.
