[1] Tornados and severe thunderstorms have swept through the eastern half of the United States in the last two days, [2] killing at least 27 people, [3] injuring more than 500, [4] and leaving hundreds homeless. [5] The worst casualties occurred in Huntsville, Alabama, [6] where 17 were killed, and Newburgh, New York, [7] where at least seven children died. [8] A truck driver died in New Jersey, [9] and one woman died in Illinois. [10] Injuries occurred in Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, Ohio, and West Virginia. [11] Heavy damage, [12] but with no deaths or injuries, [13] was reported in the District of Columbia, Maryland, Delaware, Mississippi, Pennsylvania, and North and South Carolina.
