[1] After years of delay and a last-minute snag, the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) [2] was freed from the shuttle Discovery at 3:38 p.m. EDT on Wednesday [3] and placed in orbit 380 miles above the earth, [4] 70 miles higher than any shuttle has previously flown. [5] The telescope's 10-foot aperture door is to be opened Friday exposing the finely polished 94.5-inch mirror to starlight. [6] NASA expects its first image from the telescope on Tuesday. [7] The HST is named for the late American astronomer Edwin P. Hubble [8] who discovered during the 1920s that the universe was expanding, [9] giving rise to the Big Bang theory of creation.
