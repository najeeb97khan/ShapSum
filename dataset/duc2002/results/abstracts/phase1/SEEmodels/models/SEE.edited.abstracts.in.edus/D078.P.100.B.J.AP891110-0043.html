[1] Oscar, one of the most recognizable images in the entertainment industry, has no copyright protection, a federal judge has ruled. [2] The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences had sued Creative House Promotions (Chicago), [3] manufacturer of an employee- incentive award [4] that the judge acknowledged was "very similar" to the Oscar. [5] U.S. District Court Judge Laughlin Water rejected the academy's legal claims, [6] saying that the statuette was in the public domain prior to Jan. 1, 1978, the effective date of the Copyright Act of 1976. [7] The first Oscars and all those distributed until about 1941 did not bear a mark indicating copyright.
