[1] On August 2nd, 1990, 100,000 Iraqi troops massing on Kuwait's border crossed over, [2] occupying the country [3] and destroying all government buildings and installations except for the water system and oil equipment. [4] Over 200 Kuwaitis were killed or wounded. [5] Iraq facilitated residents' departures then trucked in Iraqis to replace Kuwaitis and change the population's make-up. [6] Thousands fled to Saudi Arabia, including the leadership. [7] Jordan also took in refugees. [8] Saddam Hussain accused Kuwait of exceeding OPEC production quotas to drive down prices, [9] and stealing oil from Iraqi border wells. [10] Iraq claimed it invaded at the request of Kuwaiti revolutionaries who staged a coup, [11] which Kuwait denied. [12] Kuwait said Iraq invaded because it demanded Iraq pay back billions loaned during the 1980-1988 Iran- Iraq War. [13] Iran rejoiced [14] that the world was waking up to Saddam Hussein. [15] Kuwait appealed for US and UN help. [16] Bush said the US would take any action necessary to defend its Persian Gulf interests [17] and diverted a naval group to the Gulf. [18] The US, UN, and EEC imposed trade and military sanctions against Iraq and Kuwait, [19] supported widely by other countries. [20] The Soviets and China stopped Iraqi arms sales. [21] Iraq rounded up Westerners in Kuwait [22] and threatened to hold hostage citizens of countries imposing sanctions. [23] Oil prices hit five-year highs.
