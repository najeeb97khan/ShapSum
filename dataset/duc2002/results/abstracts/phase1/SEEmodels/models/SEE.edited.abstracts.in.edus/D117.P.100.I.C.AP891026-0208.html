[1] It was announced Thursday [2] that Kazuo Ishiguro won the Booker Prize for his novel "The Remains of The Day". The prize is worth 32,000 dollars. [3] The winning novel is a love story about a butler's English vacation [4] that explores the provoking themes of class, tradition and duty. [5] Ishiguro was born in Japan, [6] and came to England at the age of six He lives in London. [7] He was a Booker finalist in 1986. [8] In accepting the award, Ishiguro paid tribute to Salman Rushdie, the 1988 runner-up for the Booker, who is now in hiding.
