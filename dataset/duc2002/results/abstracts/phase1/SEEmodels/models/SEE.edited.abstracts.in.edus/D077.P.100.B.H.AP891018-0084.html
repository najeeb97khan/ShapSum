[1] A great quake hit southern California in 1857 and an 8.3 quake hit San Francisco in 1906, [2] killing 2500. [3] The same size quake takes longer to happen again [4] because quakes relieve stress, [5] so scientists believed the next big one would strike the LA area. [6] In 1985 Geologist Christopher Scholz predicted a 6.9 quake any time in a northern portion of the San Andreas Fault, [7] believing the 1906 quake relieved less stress than thought. [8] A 1988 US Geological Survey report predicted a 20% likelihood of a 7 quake there within 30 years, [9] and a 30% chance of a 6.5 quake in the area where Tuesday's 6.9 quake was centered.
