[1] After killing 20 [2] and leaving about 100,000 homeless in the Caribbean, [3] Hurricane Hugo hit Charleston, S.C. head-on at midnight of Sept. 21, 1989 with winds of 135 mph. [4] Although seventeen were killed in the Carolinas and property damage there exceeded $750 million, [5] hundreds or thousands of lives were saved by advance warning of the storm from the National Hurricane Center and prompt response of officials and the populace. [6] Scientists have warned that if global warning should cause sea temperature to rise 3-6 degrees Fahrenheit, [7] we may well experience future hurricanes with half again the strength of Category 4 Hugo.
