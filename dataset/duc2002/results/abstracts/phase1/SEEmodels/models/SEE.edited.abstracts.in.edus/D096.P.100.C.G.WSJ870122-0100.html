[1] Businesses show few signs of cutting back on their activities despite the new tax law's tighter restrictions on what can be claimed as a business-entertainment expense. [2] One big change for tickets to sporting events like the Super Bowl: [3] only 80% of the face value of such tickets can be deducted [4] and there must be a "substantial and bona fide business discussion" [5] before, during, or after the game. [6] As long as business is discussed, airfare and lodging are still 100% deductible and means are 80% deductible. [7] Banquets get 100% deduction if they have at least 40 persons and a speaker.
