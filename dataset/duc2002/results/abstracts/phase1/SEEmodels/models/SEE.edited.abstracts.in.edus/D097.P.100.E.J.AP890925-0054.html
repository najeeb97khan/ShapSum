[1] Charleston Mayor Joseph Riley attributed the low number of fatalities from Hurricane Hugo to residents [2] who monitored the strength of the hurricane [3] and heeded evacuation warnings. [4] The storm, which caused billions in damage, [5] claimed 17 lives in South Carolina, only two of which were in the Charleston area [6] where Hugo's 135 mph winds were most prevalent. [7] In the last decade several big storms have veered away from South Carolina, [8] and officials feared [9] that the latest warnings wouldn't be heeded. [10] Instead, tens of thousands in Charleston and the three surrounding counties went to shelters [11] or evacuated inland.
