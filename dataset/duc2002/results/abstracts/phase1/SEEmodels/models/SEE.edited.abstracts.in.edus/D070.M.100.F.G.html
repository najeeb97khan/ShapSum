[1] In October 1989, after being hospitalized for kidney surgery, [2] former East German leader Erich Honecker [3] -along with other Party officials- [4] was arrested and imprisoned to await trial for treason and corruption. [5] Judged too ill from imprisonment, [6] he was placed in a Soviet East Berlin hospital. [7] He also faced a murder charge about his role in killing people trying to escape from East Berlin. [8] In 1992, Honecker took refuge in the Chilean Embassy in Moscow [9] but was soon ordered to leave. [10] In Germany, he was tried, [11] imprisoned and released in 1993. [12] He died in Santiago, Chile in 1994 at age 81.
