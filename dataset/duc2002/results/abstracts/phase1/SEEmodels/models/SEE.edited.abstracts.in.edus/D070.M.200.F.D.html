[1] Erich Honecker, former head of East Germany, [2] died on 29 May 1994 in Santiago Chile. [3] He and his wife Margot had been living with their daughter in Santiago since his release from prison in 1993. [4] Honecker and the Communist regime [5] he headed, [6] were toppled in October 1989 by pro-democracy demonstrators, leading to the eventual unification of Germany. [7] He along with several members of his Politburo were arrested [8] and charged with treason, corruption and abuse of power. [9] Honecker was also charged with manslaughter, [10] stemming from his shoot-to-kill order, [11] which resulted in the deaths of East Germans attempting to flee to the West. [12] Honecker had been seriously ill since his downfall [13] and had spent much time in hospital and under house arrest. [14] Honecker ruled East Germany for 18 years using his secret police, [15] known as Stasi, [16] which built up files on 6 million East Germans [17] and kept a tight rein on the population. [18] As protests gathered strength in the fall of 1989, Honecker "lost touch with reality" according to former members of his regime. [19] Club swinging police brutally beat demonstrators, [20] when Soviet President Gorbachev was in town [21] to commemorate the country's 40th anniversary. [22] The uprising was a turning point [23] that speeded Honecker's downfall days later.
