[1] Philippine President Corazon Aquino's administration was beset with problems from June 1988 to October 1990. [2] Her predecessor, Ferdinand Marcos was exiled to Hawaii, [3] but had allies in the Philippines [4] and continued to create dissension. [5] Swiss bankers required a criminal prosecution [6] to release information on the $10 billion he allegedly stole [7] and deposited in Switzerland. [8] After he became terminally ill, [9] his wife, Imelda, and his supporters wanted him to be allowed to die, or at least be buried, in his homeland. [10] Aquino refused all requests citing security reasons. [11] From a landlord family, Aquino's agrarian reform policy suffered a number of setbacks. [12] Economic problems were the focus of her 1989 European trip, [13] where she touted improvements in the growth rate under her leadership [14] and sought debt relief and foreign investments. [15] The Philippine military created a continuing problem, [16] leading coups and mutinies. [17] The December 1989 attempted coup divided Filipinos in Los Angeles [18] and involved Aquino asking for help from United States forces stationed there. [19] Criticized for past indecisive actions, [20] Aquino quickly ended the October 1990 coup attempt on Mindanao. [21] Communist insurgents continued their 21-year campaign. [22] In April 1990, she said she was willing to negotiate with the insurgents and renegade military.
