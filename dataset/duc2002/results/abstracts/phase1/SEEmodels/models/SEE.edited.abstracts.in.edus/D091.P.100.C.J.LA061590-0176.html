[1] A flash flood Thursday night sent a wall of water 15 to 20 feet high through communities along the Ohio River, [2] killing at least 10 people [3] and leaving 40 to 60 missing, authorities said. [4] The flood occurred after thunderstorms [5] Thursday night dumped about 4 inches of rain in about an hour. [6] Two creeks outside of Shadyside, Ohio overflowed, [7] knocking homes off their foundations and washing away a Shadyside bar filled with people. [8] Gov. Richard Celeste declared a state of emergency. [9] The Shadyside Fire Department established a command center at the Jefferson Elementary School [10] to coordinate rescue efforts.
