[1] Hurricane Gilbert, [2] packing 110mph winds and torrential rain, [3] moved over the Jamaican capital city of Kingston today after skirting Puerto Rico, Haiti and the Dominican Republic. [4] It's tropical-storm force winds extend up to 250 miles to the north and 100 miles to the south. [5] Hal Gerrish, a forecaster with the National Hurricane Center said [6] it is one of the larger systems seen in the Caribbean for a long time. [7] Warnings were posted for the Cayman Islands, Haiti and Cuba [8] but discontinued for the Dominican Republic. [9] Jamaicans are expecting to bear the brunt of Gilbert as its eye moves lengthwise across the island.
