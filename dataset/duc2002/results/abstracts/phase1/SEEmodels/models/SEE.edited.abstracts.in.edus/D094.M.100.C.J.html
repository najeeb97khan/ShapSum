[1] Japan's Emperor Hirohito, posthumously named Showa, [2] left a $13 million taxable estate. [3] He died of cancer on Jan. 7. [4] Hirohito is remembered for surrendering in WW II. [5] Though many world leaders mourned his passing, [6] South Korea and others viewed him as a symbol of Japanese crimes against Asian people. [7] Generally portrayed as a peaceful man, a January BBC documentary claims [8] that Truman decided not to prosecute Hirohito for war crimes, [9] and that Japan's wartime prime minister was pressured into changing his testimony [10] to avoid incriminating the emperor. [11] An LA newspaper published a letter to the editor after Hirohito's death also questioning his innocence.
