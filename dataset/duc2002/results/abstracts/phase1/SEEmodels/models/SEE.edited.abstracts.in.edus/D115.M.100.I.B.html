[1] The deadliest earthquake in this region since 1950, [2] hit near the Nepal-India border killing at least 750 people [3] and injuring thousands. [4] The magnitude 6.5 quake, with its epicenter about 100 miles southeast of Katmandu, Nepal, was felt as far away as New Delhi and Calcutta. [5] The hardest hit areas were Bihar state in India and the Dharan district of Nepal. [6] Aftershocks kept people terrified for days. [7] Earthquake damage and monsoon rains have paralyzed rail and road transportation thus hindering rescue efforts and isolating some remote areas. [8] In Nepal police bulldozed [9] more than 50 houses considered too unsafe to be habitable.
