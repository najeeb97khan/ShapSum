[1] The recent tragic sinking of the ferry Estonia, with the loss of 800-plus lives, [2] has concentrated attention on making ferries safer. [3] Over the past two decades [4] the United Nations Maritime Organization has devoted much thought [5] to ship safety, [6] but now in September 1994 it is an imperative. [7] Any review of ferry safety will focus on ferry design, enforcement of existing regulations, and crew training. [8] Little attention is paid when a vessel with a third-world crew goes down in a distant ocean, [9] but the loss of a relatively modern passenger ferry in European waters concerns the public.
