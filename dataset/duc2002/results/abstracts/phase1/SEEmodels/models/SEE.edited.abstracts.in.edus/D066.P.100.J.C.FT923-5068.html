[1] Sam Walton died in April 1992. [2] Shortly before his death he wrote about his life in a book entitled, [3] "Made in America: My Story". It is the story of a happily married and well-liked man [4] who lived frugally in an obscure corner of Arkansas. [5] Readers may find the book boring and dreary. [6] It is full of common sense, [7] and charming and humorous anecdotes. [8] It does not directly address his company's non-union policies, [9] and the impact of his 40 billion dollar giant on the mom-and-pop stores in the small towns [10] where he located his stores.
