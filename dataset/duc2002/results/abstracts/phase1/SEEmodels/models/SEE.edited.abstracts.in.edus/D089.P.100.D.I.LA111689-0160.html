[1] A tornado slammed into a shopping district, residential area, and school in Huntsville, Alabama, on Wednesday. [2] At least 14 were killed, [3] nearly 300 injured, and many others still trapped. [4] Alabama governor, Guy Hunt, has dispatched National Guard troops [5] and said rescue teams will search through the night. [6] Witnesses said the area was reduced to rubble by the tornado [7] that hit between 4 and 4:30 p.m. Electricity and telephones are disrupted and only one television station is still broadcasting. [8] Doctors are having difficulty getting to the hospital [9] because of blocked roads. [10] Most of the state had been under a tornado watch since early afternoon.
