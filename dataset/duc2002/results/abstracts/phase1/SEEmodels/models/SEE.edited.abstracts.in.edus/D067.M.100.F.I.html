[1] Damage from Hurricane Andrew is estimated at $20 billion, with $8 billion covered by insurance. [2] American insurers will take the brunt of the claims, [3] but General Accident, the leading British insurer, expects claims [4] to rise as high as $40 million. [5] Andrew tore through southern Florida, [6] leaving 15 dead, power outages, [7] and polluted water in his wake. [8] Homestead and the nearby air force base were flattened. [9] In Dade County, 250,000 people are homeless. [10] Andrew next smashed into Louisiana, [11] killing two. [12] It missed the important oil refineries, [13] but damaged crops and the oyster and alligator businesses. [14] The dollar sustained a similar assault.
