[1] Deployment of the Hubble Space Telescope (HST), originally scheduled for 1983, [2] was delayed by technical problems and the Challenger accident in 1986 [3] and did not take place until April 1990. [4] The HST had two mirrors, one 94.5 inches in diameter and the other 12 inches. [5] Electrical power was provided by two wing-like solar arrays. [6] It was named after Edwin P. Hubble [7] who discovered in the 1920s that the universe is constantly expanding. [8] Scientists regarded the telescope as the greatest advance in astronomy since Galileo, [9] providing 10 times better resolution and 25 times more sensitivity than ground-based observatories. [10] A successful launch of space shuttle Discovery with the HST aboard took place at 8:34 a.m. April 24, 1990 and Discovery ascended into orbit 380 miles above Earth. [11] On April 25 the HST was placed in orbit on its own with Discovery trailing behind to provide astronaut assistance if needed. [12] The HST's aluminum aperture door was successfully opened on April 27 exposing the telescope's 94.5- inch mirror to starlight. [13] By April 28 there were still problems with the electronic and communications systems, [14] but procedures were in place to take care of these, [15] the HST was in orbit, [16] and the Discovery astronauts prepared for return to earth on April 29, 1990.
