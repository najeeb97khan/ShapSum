[1] Emperor Hirohito of Japan has been bedridden for a month with an intestinal hemorrhage. [2] He has received nearly 22 pints of blood [3] since his illness worsened and the transfusions put a strain on his heart and kidneys. [4] Recently his blood pressure and pulse dipped sharply, [5] but then returned to normal even as the internal bleeding continued. [6] He is being fed intravenously [7] and while complaining of fatigue does not appear to be in pain. [8] He visited with his two sons [9] and was described as fully conscious and alert. [10] Princess Michiko, Hirohito's daughter-in-law, scaled down celebrations for her 54th birthday.
