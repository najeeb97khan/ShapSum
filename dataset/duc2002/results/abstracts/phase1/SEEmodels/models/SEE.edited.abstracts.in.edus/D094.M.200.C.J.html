[1] Emperor Hirohito, who died on January 7 of cancer after several months of internal bleeding, [2] left a taxable estate of approximately $13 million. [3] Posthumously named Showa, or Enlightened Peace, the name of his reign, [4] Hirohito will be remembered for his most courageous decision in his 62-year reign: [5] Japan must surrender and end the Pacific conflict in WW II. [6] Though many world leaders mourned his death, painful WW II memories sparked criticism of the monarch. [7] South Korea sent condolences, [8] but a government party statement added, [9] "We reserve further comment, considering the unhappy past." [10] A Taiwanese newspaper noted [11] that "while Hirohito apologized to the United States and Europe...", [12] he did not direct a single word to China..." [13] Generally portrayed as having had no significant role in the war, [14] a BBC documentary by Edward Behr challenged this view, [15] alleging that Hirohito had advance knowledge of the Pearl Harbor attack. [16] The show also claims [17] that President Truman decided not to prosecute Hirohito for war crimes, [18] and Japan's wartime prime minister, Gen.Tojo, was pressured into changing his testimony to avoid incriminating the emperor. [19] A letter to the editor of an LA newspaper after Hirohito's death also expressed doubt [20] that he was blameless.
