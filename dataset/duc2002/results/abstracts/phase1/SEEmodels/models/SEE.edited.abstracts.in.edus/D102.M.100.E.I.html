[1] The Queen of TV comedy and entrepreneur, Lucille Ball, [2] died on April 26, 1989 at 77. [3] Presidents, comedians and actors, neighbors, residents of her hometown, television critics, surviving co-stars, and just plain fans paid tributes to her. [4] More than 2,000 attended three memorial services. [5] She was a chorus girl, model, [6] and in 75 movies, [7] but is best remembered for her physical and verbal comedy and superb timing evident on television. [8] She and her husband, Desi Arnaz, revolutionized television comedy with "I Love Lucy," [9] which was filmed before a live audience, [10] becoming a rerun gold mine, [11] still seen world-wide.
