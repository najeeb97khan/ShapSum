[1] John Lennon spirit is still very much alive to adoring fans worldwide ten years after his death. [2] His 50th birthday was observed with a global celebration of his peace anthem "Imagine" [3] which was played for an estimated 1 billion people in 130 countries. [4] The song was played following a brief ceremony at the United Nations. [5] Fans also gathered in Liverpool, Moscow, Los Angles and New York [6] to pay tribute to the former Beatle. [7] One biographer described Lennon [8] as an anorexic bisexual addled by drugs [9] who raged his way from Liverpool to New York, [10] but friends and family denounced [11] that version of Lennon's life. [12] Lennon himself acknowledged his drug use and sexual promiscuity, [13] although he claimed [14] that his worst days were behind him. [15] His wife, Yoko Ono admitted using heroin for a time. [16] He had also been portrayed as a dour, shoeless youth with an unfit mother, [17] a description refuted by his half-sister in her book. [18] Since his death, his wife has kept his legacy alive, [19] issuing new albums of his work and selling memorabilia, [20] featuring his image. [21] Lennon was gunned down by a deranged fan outside his Manhattan apartment December 8, 1980 at the age of 40.
