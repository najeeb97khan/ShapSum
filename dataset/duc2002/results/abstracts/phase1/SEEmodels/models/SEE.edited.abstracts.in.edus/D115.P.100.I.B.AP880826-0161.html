[1] An earthquake [2] that measured 6.5 on the Richter scale [3] devastated parts of Nepal and India earlier this week, [4] killing more than 980 people. [5] In Bhaktapur, Nepal, [6] mourners called for revenge [7] after political rivals beat a former legislator to death [8] while he was distributing relief supplies to earthquake victims. [9] Karna Hyanju, once a Communist Party sympathizer, was beaten [10] and then dragged through the streets for nearly two hours [11] before police intervened. [12] He was accosted by a group of men [13] who accused him of giving relief supplies only to his own supporters. [14] Several people have been arrested in the killing, including the mayor.
