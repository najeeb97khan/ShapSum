[1] The earthquake that devastated northern Iran on June 20th registered a magnitude of 7.7, [2] making it the strongest ever recorded in that county. [3] Initial estimate of those killed was 25,000 but in the days following, that was scaled upward to 36,000. [4] Iran is in an active seismic region so earthquakes, even major ones, are not unknown. [5] Iran is used to massive tragedies: [6] natural disasters, war, and revolution. [7] President Hashemi Rafsanjani declared a three-day mourning period and the government said [8] it would welcome aid from all countries except Israel and South Africa. [9] A spokesman for the People's Mojahedin accused the government [10] of underreporting the number of deaths [11] and pinned responsibility for those deaths on the Islamic government forcing people to live in shoddy housing. [12] The government blamed the US for loss of life by earlier plundering Iran's oil [13] thus depriving the country of the means [14] to develop modern construction methods. [15] Relief supplies poured into Iran from all over the world including the US. [16] US contributions, including those from the government, were funneled through private relief organizations. [17] The Iranian community of Orange County was active in seeking contributions. [18] Aid was vital for Iran [19] since thousands were injured and 400,000 homeless.
