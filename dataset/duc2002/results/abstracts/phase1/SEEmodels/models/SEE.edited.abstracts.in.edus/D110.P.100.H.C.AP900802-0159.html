[1] An emergency session of the foreign ministers of the Arab League was convened in Cairo at Kuwait's request soon after Iraq's military forces overran the small Persian Gulf nation. [2] Kuwait asked the League [3] to condemn the attack. [4] Only Lebanon joined [5] Kuwait in publicly condemning the action, the first time in modern history [6] one Arab nation has taken over the government of another. [7] Kuwait invoked the 1950 Joint Defense Pact that calls on League members [8] to come to the aid of any member [9] that is a victim of military action. [10] The first session adjourned without taking any action.
