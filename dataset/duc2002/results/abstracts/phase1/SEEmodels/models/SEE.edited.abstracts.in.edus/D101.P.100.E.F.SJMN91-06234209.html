[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev prepared to return to Moscow from the Crimea [2] and reclaim power [3] after the coup against him collapsed and its leaders fled. [4] Crowds cheered outside the Russian parliament building, [5] which served as headquarters for resistance to the coup led by Boris Yeltsin. [6] The national legislature demanded Gorbachev's reinstatement [7] and nullified the coup leaders' emergency decrees. [8] The three-day coup was one of the most tumultuous events in recent history and its failure could be a fatal blow to efforts [9] to thwart reform drives by Gorbachev and Yeltsin. [10] Huge anti-Coup demonstrations were held in Moscow, Leningrad, and other cities.
