[1] Andrei Sakharov, 68, a Nobel Peace Prize winner and a human rights activist, [2] died in Moscow on 14 December 1989. [3] Sakharov was a physicist [4] who attained hero status for his work on the Soviet hydrogen bomb. [5] He later made himself a nuisance to the Kremlin with his continual calls for improvement in the rights of the Soviet people. [6] In 1973 the International League for Human Rights gave him an award, [7] but he was not able to accept it until his first visit to New York City in 1989. [8] His humanitarianism won him the 1975 Noble Peace Prize. [9] His gadfly activities throughout the 1970s exasperated the Kremlin. [10] In January 1980, when he criticized the invasion of Afghanistan, [11] President Brezhnev exiled him to the closed city of Gorky. [12] President Gorbachev freed Sakharov in December 1986. [13] He and Yelena Bonner, his wile and fellow activist, settled in Moscow. [14] He praised Gorbachev for the new direction in foreign affairs, [15] but loudly criticized the slow pace of economic reform. [16] In April 1989 Sakharov was elected to membership in the Soviet Congress of People's Deputies. [17] Sakharov's obituary, signed by Gorbachev and published in Pravda, and the outpouring of remorse constituted a public apology for his exile.
