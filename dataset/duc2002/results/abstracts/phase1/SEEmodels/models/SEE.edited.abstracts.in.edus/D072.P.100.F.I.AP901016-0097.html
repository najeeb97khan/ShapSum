[1] Leonard Bernstein, the first internationally acclaimed American maestro, was buried privately today beside his wife at Greenwood Cemetery in Brooklyn. [2] Flags at the Lincoln Center, the home of the New York Philharmonic, were lowered in his honor. [3] The Philharmonic plans an all-Bernstein program through October 23. [4] A memorial concert is also planned at Carnegie Hall on November 14. [5] A gymnastic conductor, he also composed classical and popular music, [6] and introduced thousands to classical music on his television series. [7] Musical director Zubin Mehta and Larry Kert, [8] who first played Tony in Bernstein's "West Side Story", [9] praised him today.
