[1] Clark AFB, Philippines, was evacuated today after gases, ash and rock [2] spewed from Mount Pinatubo located 10 miles from the U.S. base. [3] Philippine Institute of Vulcanology and Seismology Director Raymundo Punongbayan warned [4] that bigger eruptions would follow. [5] Subic Bay naval base will house the nearly 15,000 evacuees. [6] Fifteen hundred troops remained at Clark to provide security. [7] Since Pinatubo began erupting last week, [8] Philippine officials have evacuated more than 12,000 people in three provinces. [9] Pinatubo is in the "Ring of Fire" [10] that circles the Pacific. [11] Mount Unzen in Japan, also in the ring, erupted a week ago, [12] killing 38 people.
