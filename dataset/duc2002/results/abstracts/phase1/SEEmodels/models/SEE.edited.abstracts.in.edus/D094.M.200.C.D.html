[1] Emperor Hirohito of Japan died in 1989 at the age of 87. [2] He ascended to the throne in 1926 [3] and presided over one of Japan's most tumultuous eras. [4] The last of the World War II generation of leaders, Hirohito was a controversial figure, even in death. [5] While many western leaders praised him, [6] many Asian leaders, [7] whose countries suffered under the Japanese military during World War II were cool. [8] While most historians believe [9] that Hirohito was a powerless figurehead, [10] others believe that he had to have known of the massacre of thousands of Chinese in Nanking and of the plan [11] to bomb Pearl Harbor. [12] Some believe that the U.S. covered up his complicity in war crimes after the war [13] to avoid unrest in Japan. [14] After the U.S. dropped the atomic bomb on Japan, [15] Hirohito announced Japan's surrender in a radio address to his nation. [16] After the war, [17] Hirohito was just a symbol [18] but the imperial system has remained important to the Japanese people. [19] Hirohito had traveled in Europe [20] and had a preference for Western food and clothes. [21] With few real responsibilities, [22] he spent much time in the study of marine biology. [23] He died of cancer after a long illness [24] and is succeeded by his son Akihito.
