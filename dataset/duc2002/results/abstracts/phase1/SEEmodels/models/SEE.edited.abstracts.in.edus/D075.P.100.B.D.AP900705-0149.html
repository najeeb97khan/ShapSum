[1] A natural substance [2] called transforming growth factor (TGF) [3] beta appears to be able to limit damage to cardiac cells following a heart attack. [4] A study at the Jefferson Medical College in Philadelphia showed [5] that laboratory rats induced to have heart attacks, [6] suffered 50 percent less cell damage after injections of TGF beta than rats [7] that did not receive TGF beta. [8] The TGF beta seems to block the action of other substances, such as tumor necrosis factor, [9] that can cause blood vessels to narrow following a heart attack. [10] Any experimental treatment of human heart attack victims with TGF beta is at least a year away.
