[1] President Bush's nominee for ambassador to Mexico is 28-year Foreign Service veteran John D. Negroponte, [2] who as ambassador to Honduras from 1981-1985, [3] helped direct CIA aid to Nicaraguan rebels. [4] Negroponte, 49, [5] later concluded secret US aid was a mistake. [6] Mexican leftists criticize the appointment [7] but it is expected to be accepted. [8] Rejection would be an unusual diplomatic slap. [9] Negroponte was most recently a deputy to Reagan's last national security adviser, Lt. Gen. Colin Powell; [10] and was an aide to adviser Henry Kissinger during the Vietnam war. [11] He has worked on negotiations with Mexico on environmental and fishing issues [12] and is known as being good at working the bureaucracy.
