[1] The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) orbited with its lens cover open Friday, [2] but its electronics were shut down due to a malfunction. [3] HST's 10-foot aluminum aperture door was opened exposing its finely polished 94.5-inch mirror to starlight for the first time, [4] but problems with its high-speed antennas will delay the release of its first test image which had been planned for Tuesday. [5] Scientific data will follow in a moth or two. [6] From its orbit high above the earth's distorting atmosphere HST will enable astronomers over its 15-year lifetime [7] to look back 14 billion years [8] and possibly determine the age of the universe.
