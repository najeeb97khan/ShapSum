[1] Coal miners from Romania's Jiu Valley, [2] who spearheaded violent anti-reform rampages through Bucharest in 1990 and 1991, [3] are once again out on strike, [4] pressing the government for higher wages. [5] So far the government, unlike its predecessor has not caved in but has begun action [6] to have the strike declared illegal. [7] Romanians, most of who earn far less than the striking miners, [8] appear solidly opposed to the strike. [9] The miners want more compensation for the difficult conditions [10] in which they work and live. [11] They are also following their charismatic leader, Miron Cosma, [12] who led them on previously successful strikes.
