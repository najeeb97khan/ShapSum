[1] Vice-presidential candidate Dan Quayle, 41, was seen as a political novice [2] and relegated to small towns and safe GOP areas. [3] After controversies subsided he freed himself from handlers [4] and became more accessible [5] but soon dropped off front pages. [6] He is loyal to Bush and willing to be a team player [7] but has few administration intimates. [8] He is criticized as bringing no reputation or staff to the campaign, [9] being untested, [10] and being unqualified to assume the presidency. [11] He is described as immature but not stupid, thrust into a situation for which he was unprepared. [12] Quayle as VP choice is compared to Nixon, chosen to appease conservatives, and political nonentity Spiro Agnew.
