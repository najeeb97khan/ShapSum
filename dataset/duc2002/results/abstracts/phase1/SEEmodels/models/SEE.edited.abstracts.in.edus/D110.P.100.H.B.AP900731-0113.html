[1] An Iraq-Kuwait crisis erupted July 17 [2] when Iraqi President Saddam Hussein accused Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates [3] of costing his country $14 billion in lost revenue [4] because of their overproduction of OPEC oil limits. [5] He further demanded $2.4 billion for oil allegedly stolen from a common field and the cancellation of $15 billion in war loans [6] that Kuwait gave to Iraq. [7] Through mediation by Saudi Arabia and Egypt, Kuwaiti and Iraqi officials [8] met in Jiddah [9] to negotiate issues. [10] Meanwhile, Iraq massed 100,000 troops and 300 tanks on Kuwait's border. [11] Iraq has historically laid claim to all of Kuwait.
