[1] Robert Maxwell was described as larger than life and the Citizen Kane of his time. [2] Born in Czechoslovakia, [3] Maxwell escape the Holocaust [4] and emigrated to Britain in 1940. [5] He put together and personally ran a publishing empire [6] which included Macmillan publishing house in New York; [7] Britain's Daily Mirror, The Daily Record, and the European and British Printing Company; [8] and Communications Corp., Britain's largest printing company. [9] Details of Maxwell's death are sketchy [10] and the cause of his death is not known [11] although it is speculated that he slipped and fell overboard. [12] Maxwell's death came as he was embroiled in an international financial controversy.
