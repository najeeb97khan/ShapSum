[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev returned to power as the coup against him collapsed, [2] unraveling swiftly as junta was unable to cope with thousands of people [3] who created barricades around the Russian Parliament, Russian President Yeltsin's stronghold. [4] The national legislature demanded Gorbachev's reinstatement and the Communist Party, seeking to salvage its image, denounced the takeover. [5] The real issue for the coup failure was not Gorbachev [6] but the new political culture of democracy he initiated. [7] At first Gorbachev and Yeltsin appeared as equals [8] but Yeltsin, who challenged the Communist Party [9] and stayed close to the Russian people, trumped Gorbachev.
