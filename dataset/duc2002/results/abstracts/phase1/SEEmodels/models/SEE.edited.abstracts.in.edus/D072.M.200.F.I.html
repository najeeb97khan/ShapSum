[1] Leonard Bernstein -pianist, composer, conductor, teacher, TV personality, liberal, and bon vivant-died at 72 on October 14, just four days [2] after announcing his retirement from conducting. [3] He had been in failing health since last spring, [4] suffering from a variety of lung diseases, [5] and cut short a Japanese tour in July. [6] He was buried on October 16. [7] "Lenny", the first American conductor to attain world fame, [8] was the well-educated son of Russian Jewish immigrants. [9] A member of the first class at Tanglewood, he achieved instant stardom at 25 as an emergency fill-in to conduct the New York Philharmonic on nation-wide radio. [10] He conducted this orchestra over 2000 times. [11] He composed both classical and popular music and introduced thousands to classical music on television. [12] He made more than 200 classical recordings, many now being examined for re-release. [13] Plaudits have come from everywhere and the New York Philharmonic presented four all- Bernstein nights of classical selections. [14] In Los Angles, three orchestras and two radio stations honored him. [15] On November 14, his career was commemorated at Carnegie Hall and Broadway said goodbye on December 13. [16] He had savored the celebrations of his 70th birthday, [17] which featured all-star fundraising concerts at the Tanglewood Music Center and Carnegie Hall.
