[1] Federal aid was approved Friday for tornado-ravaged Huntsville, Alabama. [2] Governor Guy Hunt viewed [3] the destruction, toured hospitals, [4] and visited with grieving families. [5] Seventeen were killed, [6] 400 injured, [7] and 80 are still in hospitals. [8] State emergency officials estimate damages in excess of $100 million. [9] About $1 million will be needed for grants to pay for emergency clothing and temporary housing. [10] The Federal Emergency Management Agency hopes to begin taking relief applications on Sunday. [11] Food has arrived in the city, [12] but there no longer is a place to store it. [13] Flags will be flown at half- staff through Tuesday in memory of the victims.
