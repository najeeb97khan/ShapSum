[1] On Easter Sunday, three Cabinet ministers supported John Major in the wake of speculation [2] that he could be forced out this autumn. [3] Both Secretary of State for Education John Patten and Michael Howard, the Home Secretary, dismissed talk of a leadership contest before the next general election. [4] Lord Wakeham, Leader of the House of Lords, called on Torries [5] to be loyal to Mr. Major. [6] One poll shows [7] that Michael Heseltine is the favorite [8] to replace Mr. Major [9] if he is forced out, [10] but that Michael Portillo will vigorously campaign to secure Eurosceptic policies from the eventual winner.
