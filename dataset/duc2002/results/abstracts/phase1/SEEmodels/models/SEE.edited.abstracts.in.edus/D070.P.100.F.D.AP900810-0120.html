[1] Former East German leader Erich Honecker and Erich Mielke, former head of the secret police, were interrogated by federal prosecutors about their role in killings of people [2] who tried to flee the country. [3] More than 200 people were shot to death [4] or killed by land mines as they tried to escape East Germany's Communist rule. [5] Honecker, 77 and in failing health, has already been charged with corruption and abuse of power. [6] The murder charge could put Honecker behind bars for life, [7] but his poor health makes prosecution questionable. [8] He was toppled from power [9] in October 1989 following pro-democracy protests.
