[1] Strikes in the Soviet Union by thousands of coal miners and other workers have shut down more than 200 mines and factories in the country's two top coal-producing regions: [2] the Donetsk region of the Ukraine and Siberia's Kuzretsk Basin. [3] The miners are demanding more local control over their industry, higher wages, and better living conditions. [4] Government negotiations have resolved most of the demands, including those on pensions and overtime, [5] but some longer-term questions will take up to a month to settle and miners are refusing any delay. [6] A law on ending strikes and labor conflicts is being rushed through the Supreme Soviet.
