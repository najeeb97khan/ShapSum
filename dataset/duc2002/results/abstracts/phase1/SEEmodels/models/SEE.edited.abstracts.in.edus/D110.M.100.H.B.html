[1] Iraq, [2] after accusing Kuwait of multiple, harmful wrongdoings, invaded and overran Kuwait. [3] It claimed to have responded to a request by a Kuwaiti revolutionary group. [4] A skeptical U.S. and U.N. rejected this justification, condemned the action, [5] demanded withdrawal, [6] and threatened sanctions. [7] The beleaguered Kuwaiti government requested U.S. military intervention. [8] Divided Arab nations failed to resolve the crisis. [9] After further Iraqi intransigence, strong U.N. economic and military sanctions were imposed, [10] including a ban on Iraqi oil and imports. [11] The price of oil soared. [12] Iraq responded by rounding up foreigners in Kuwait, [13] sending some to Baghdad, [14] and alerting its citizens to a possible U.S. attack.
