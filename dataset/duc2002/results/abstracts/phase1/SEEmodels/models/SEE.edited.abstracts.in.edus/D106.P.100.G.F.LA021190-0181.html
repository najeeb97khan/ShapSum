[1] Nelson Mandela joined the African National Congress in 1944 at age 26 [2] and became active in black protests and civil disobedience [3] to fight apartheid. [4] He was often arrested by the South African government [5] and finally received a life sentence in 1964 for sabotage and conspiracy. [6] In 1985, then President Botha offered to release him [7] if he renounced violence. [8] Mandela refused. [9] Mandela was celebrated outside South Africa on his 70th birthday [10] but South Africa banned local celebrations. [11] While in prison, Mandela contracted tuberculosis [12] but was treated and recovered. [13] President Frederik de Klerk released Mandela from prison in February 1990.
