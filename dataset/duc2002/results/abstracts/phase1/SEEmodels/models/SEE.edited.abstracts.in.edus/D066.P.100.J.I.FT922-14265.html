[1] Sam Walton is arguably the most important retailer of his generation. [2] The owner of a general store in the backwaters of Arkansas in 1950 became the world's largest retailer, with annual sales of almost $44 billion. [3] Success can be attributed to Walton's personality and his willingness to challenge retailing orthodoxy. [4] He was a frugal, modest, hard- working man, [5] who made pop-in visits [6] and delivered pep talks. [7] Employee satisfaction was a vital ingredient, with bonus payments and profit-sharing plans. [8] Also essential is Wal-Mart's year around high-volume, cut-price selling. [9] Walton proved discount stores could be successful in rural towns [10] and drove out hundreds of small main-street stores.
