[1] It appears [2] that Olympic gold has lost much of its commercial luster. [3] Following the 1984 Olympics, pixie gymnast Mary Lou Retton signed up for $5 million in commercial advertisements. [4] No one from the 1988 Games will follow in her steps. [5] Marketers say [6] these medalists will pull in $100,000 to $500,000 a year. [7] Why? Reasons abound. [8] The games occurred halfway around the world, [9] TV coverage was rated poor, no heroes [10] - the Soviets trounced U.S. competitors and drug scandals cast a pall over the Games. [11] Also, many advertisers now prefer athletes [12] who are in the public eye longer, [13] like football players or basketball's Michael Jordan.
