[1] The Academy Awards are criticized for their unfairness [2] when films get nominated but their directors don't, perhaps out of professional jealousy. [3] For instance, director Steven Spielberg has never been nominated for an individual achievement [4] even though his films are the biggest moneymakers in history. [5] The Irving Thalberg award for consistently high quality of production has come in handy for acknowledging giants who were overlooked and for salving the academy's conscience. [6] Spielberg received the Thalberg award in 1987. [7] In 1986 the Directors Guild named him best director. [8] Films that became classics have been overlooked, too. [9] "Citizen Kane" won only for its script.
