[1] Hurricane Gilbert, a category 5, one of the strongest storms in history battered the Dominican Republic, Jamaica and the Cayman Islands. [2] It was the worst disaster in Jamaica's modern history [3] and will take some time [4] to assess its full damage. [5] The storm measured 26.13 inches of barometric pressure, the lowest ever measured in the Western Hemisphere. [6] Oil companies rushed to evacuate about 10,000 oilrigs in the Caribbean. [7] With phones knocked out, [8] ham radio operators in Jamaica reported [9] that people who were trying to enter the Wyndam Hotel on Jamaica's Montego Bay at the height of the storm were swept into the ocean.
