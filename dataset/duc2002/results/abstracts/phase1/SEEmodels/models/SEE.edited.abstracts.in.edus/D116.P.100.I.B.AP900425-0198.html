[1] The $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope left the shuttle Discovery's cargo bay on Wednesday, nearly two hours and one orbit late, for orbit 380 statute miles above the earth's distorting atmosphere. [2] It took three attempts [3] to deploy the telescope's second solar array panel, [4] thus causing the delayed release from Discovery to operational orbit. [5] Suited astronauts were standing by [6] to manually unfurl the solar panels if necessary. [7] Next, after first testing the telescope's maneuverability, [8] the 10-foot aperture door will be opened on Friday, [9] exposing the finely polished 94.5-inch mirror to starlight. [10] NASA expects its first images from space on Tuesday.
