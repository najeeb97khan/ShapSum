[1] Coal miners from Romania's Jiu Valley [2] who led anti-reform rampages through Bucharest in 1990 and 1991 are again on strike. [3] The government has not caved in and has begun legal action [4] to have the strike declared illegal. [5] The miners' position seems untenable. [6] Romanians are opposed to the miners' wage demand, [7] the mines are not considered a vital source of energy, [8] and the miners are in danger of pricing their coal out of the market. [9] The miners want more money [10] to compensate for difficult living and working conditions. [11] They also are following Miron Cosma, their charismatic leader [12] who secured their earlier wage increases.
