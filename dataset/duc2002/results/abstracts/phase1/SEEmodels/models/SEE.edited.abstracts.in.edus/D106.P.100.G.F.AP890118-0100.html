[1] An activist and family friend of Nelson Mandela said [2] that four African National Congress leaders [3] that had been convicted along with Mandela in 1964 were allowed to visit Mandela just before Christmas. [4] The four who were all held at Victor Verster Prison were taken to Pollsmoor Prison [5] and spent about six hours with Mandela. [6] The Prisons Service declined to confirm or deny the visit. [7] Mandela had been moved to a suburban-style home on the prison grounds in early December [8] after recovering from tuberculosis. [9] Mandela's wife, Winnie, had visited him the previous month [10] and said that he was "practically in solitary confinement".
