[1] The exact distance of a marathon is 26 miles and 385 yards. [2] It is often expressed as 26.2 miles. [3] Marathons are very popular, [4] and major cities of the world organize marathons [5] that draw thousands of participants. [6] London, Berlin, Rome, Paris, Stockholm, Vienna, Budapest, Brussels, Munich, Bangkok and Tel Aviv are some of the cities that organize annual marathons. [7] Tour companies even offer "marathon tours" to distant marathons. [8] Most of the thousands who run do so only for self-satisfaction, [9] but others compete for monetary prizes offered by most marathons. [10] The Marine Corps marathon in Washington, D.C. is one [11] that does not offer prize money. [12] Marathons are usually run in the early morning hours [13] to take advantage of cooler temperatures and cleaner air. [14] The organizers of the 1992 Barcelona Olympics were criticized for contesting the marathon in the late afternoon heat. [15] The London Marathon is the world's largest. [16] The Boston Marathon is the most storied. [17] It goes back almost a century, [18] and in 1992, 83 year-old John Kelley was planning to run in his 60th Boston Marathon. The Los Angeles Marathon is renown as the best-marketed marathon. An Diego and San Francisco pride themselves for providing the most marathon-friendly weather.
