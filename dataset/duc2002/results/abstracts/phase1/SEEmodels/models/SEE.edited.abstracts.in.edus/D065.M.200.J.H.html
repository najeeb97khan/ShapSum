[1] Indiana Senator Dan Quayle, 41, was the surprise choice of Republican George Bush for presidential running mate in 1988. [2] Quayle was compared to John F. Kennedy because of their similar ages and congressional service, and to Robert Redford in appearance. [3] Quayle was criticized as a novice and his National Guard service attacked. [4] His campaign gaffes made news, [5] but he was loyal to Bush and a team player. [6] After controversies subsided he kept a low profile. [7] A year after the election he agreed to be on "Meet the Press", [8] shortly after Bush announced he would be on the ticket in 1992, [9] and was uncontroversial. Quayle grew up in Huntington, Indiana, population 17,000, [10] where his grandfather owned the newspaper. [11] He graduated from DePauw University in 1969, [12] spent six months in National Guard training, then in 1971 [13] investigated consumer problems in the Indiana Attorney General's office [14] and became an aide to the governor. [15] While attending Indiana University Law School he met and married his wife Marilyn. [16] They jointly practiced law in Huntington [17] before Quayle spent 12 years in the US Congress. [18] Quayle has three children [19] and is known as a nice guy with good values. [20] In 1991, Huntington hosted an exhibit in his honor, which he didn't attend because of a busy schedule.
