[1] The Bush administration, eager to have an ambassador in Nicaragua now [2] that Violeta Chamorro has taken over after 11 years of Sandinista rule, [3] is expected to name career diplomat Harry Shlaudeman to the post. [4] It had planned to send Melissa Wells [5] but feared a prolonged confirmation fight [6] because she was a previous target of conservatives. [7] Shlaudeman, 64, previously ambassador to Argentina, Peru, Brazil, and Venezuela, is expected to win easy Senate confirmation. [8] He agreed to the job only after being personally asked by Bush. [9] The Sandinistas expelled eight US diplomats in July 1988 for interfering in the country's internal affairs, [10] and the US expelled eight Nicaraguan envoys in return.
