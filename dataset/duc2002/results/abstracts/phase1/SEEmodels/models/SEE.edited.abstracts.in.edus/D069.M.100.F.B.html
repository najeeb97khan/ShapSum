[1] Political unrest and a collapsing economy prompted tens of thousands of skilled East German workers [2] to rush through recently opened borders to West Germany. [3] Western leaders saw [4] that a reunified Germany was possible. [5] The East bloc communists, [6] initially opposed to the idea, softened by urging reunification as a longer-term goal, to be reached in gradual stages. [7] Serious concerns about the future relations of NATO and the Warsaw Pact, the impact [8] a strong, united German state would have on the evolving European integration, trepidation by neighboring states and the fears of Jews world-wide had to be addressed. [9] Reunification occurred. [10] It was inevitable.
