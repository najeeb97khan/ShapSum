[1] After devastating Guadeloupe, Montserrat, Antigua, the Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico on Sunday and Monday with winds up to 150 mph, [2] killing at least 20 people [3] and leaving perhaps 100,000 homeless, Hurricane Hugo slowed down yesterday to winds of 105-125 mph, only to pick up speed again and crash into Charleston, S.C. at midnight with winds of 135 mph. [4] In addition to flooding, fires erupted from natural gas leaks in Charleston and ten people were killed in the Carolinas. [5] Today the hurricane moved inland with steadily diminishing winds [6] and was downgraded to a tropical storm at 6 A.M.
