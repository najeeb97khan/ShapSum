[1] Former East German leader Erich Honecker may be moved to a monastery [2] to protect him from enraged citizens. [3] Since being ousted in October, [4] Honecker and other party officials have been investigated for corruption and living in luxury at cost to the state. [5] Honecker is under house arrest in government housing outside East Berlin, [6] which is being converted to a medical rehabilitation center. [7] Lutheran and Catholic churches have offered protection and refuge fearing [8] he would be attacked and lynched if placed in smaller quarters [9] as demanded by East German groups calling for his punishment. [10] Currently, Honecker is seriously ill following gall-bladder surgery.
