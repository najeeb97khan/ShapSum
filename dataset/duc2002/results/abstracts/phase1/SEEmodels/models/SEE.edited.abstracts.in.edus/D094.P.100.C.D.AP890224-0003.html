[1] To the rest of the world, he was known as Emperor Hirohito, [2] but in Japan his name was rarely used. [3] Japanese found the foreign practice [4] of using his given name impolite and referred to him simply as His majesty, the Emperor. [5] Japanese histories will record him as Emperor Showa, the name [6] he chose when he became emperor in 1926. [7] His son, Akihito succeeded Emperor Hirohito and has chosen the name Heisei. [8] Japan numbers the years according to the emperor's tenure on the thrown. [9] Thus the first year of Hirohito's reign was Showa 1. [10] Until he died, [11] 1989 was Showa 64, [12] now it is Heisei 1.
