[1] Democratic reforms elsewhere in the Eastern Bloc have magnified the emerging opposition to uncompromising Communist rule in East Germany. [2] The mass emigration of thousands [3] of disaffected East Germans has impelled West Germany's Parliamentary Speaker [4] to place the subject of reunification on his committee's agenda. [5] Recent polls show [6] that between 85% and 90% of all West Germans favor a reunified Germany, [7] but officials there are circumspect of the concerns of Washington and Moscow. [8] However, the U.S. Ambassador to Bonn predicted [9] that reunion could occur relatively soon. [10] Because East Germany is the Soviet's most important Warsaw Pact satellite, [11] Moscow may not be so agreeable.
