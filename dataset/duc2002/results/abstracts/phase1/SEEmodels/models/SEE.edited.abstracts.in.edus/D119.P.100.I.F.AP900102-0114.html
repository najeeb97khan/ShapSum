[1] President Bush named career diplomat Deane Hinton as ambassador to Panama as a recess appointment [2] since Congress is not in session. [3] Hinton, currently ambassador to Costa Rica, replaces Arthur Davis [4] who had been recalled in protest [5] of what the administration considered the stealing of the Panamanian elections by General Manuel Noriega. [6] Davis was later returned to Panama [7] after US forces invaded [8] Panama and Guillermo Endara was installed as president. [9] Hinton has also been ambassador to El Salvador and Pakistan. [10] Senate Majority Leader George Mitchell called Hinton highly qualified [11] because of his "wide-ranging experience and expertise in Central America".
