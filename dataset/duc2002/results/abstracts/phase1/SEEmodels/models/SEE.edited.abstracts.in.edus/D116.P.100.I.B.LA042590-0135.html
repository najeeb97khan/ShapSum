[1] The space shuttle Discovery soared to a record high orbit of 380 miles [2] to deploy the $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope. [3] Sadly, many of the scientists who worked so hard for this moment missed the launch from earth [4] because it was aborted the first time due to problems. [5] Possibly the finest optical instrument ever created, when activated, [6] it should produce images 10 times sharper than the largest earthbound telescopes. [7] Once free from the shuttle, [8] the Hubble has six hours [9] in which to deploy its solar power arrays, [10] otherwise sensitive internal equipment could be damaged. [11] Twelve years after conception, it will soon be operational.
