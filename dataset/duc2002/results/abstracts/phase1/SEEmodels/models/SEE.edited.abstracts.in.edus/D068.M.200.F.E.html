[1] Checkpoint Charlie, the most famous symbol of the Cold War was lifted into history today. [2] At an invitation only ceremony attended by the foreign ministers of the four World War II allies, [3] a U.S. army band played an old Berlin song [4] as the checkpoint was hoisted onto a waiting flatbed truck, to be taken to a museum. [5] Speaking at the ceremony, Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze, the first Soviet foreign minister to visit Berlin, [6] called for the removal of Berlin's status as an allied controlled city and the withdrawal of troops six months after the two German states unify. [7] Secretary of State James Baker, [8] citing Checkpoint Charlie's status as a Cold War symbol, [9] expressed the hope [10] that its removal would bury the conflicts it created. [11] The decision to remove Checkpoint Charlie came as large sections of the wall were being dismantled [12] and the opening of the border between the two Berlins made the checkpoint superfluous. [13] Its removal was timed to coincide with a meeting of German and Allied foreign ministers in East Berlin [14] to discuss the status of Berlin and a united Germany. [15] Among the invited guests was West Germany's Chancellor Willy Brandt who was mayor of Berlin in the 1960s.
