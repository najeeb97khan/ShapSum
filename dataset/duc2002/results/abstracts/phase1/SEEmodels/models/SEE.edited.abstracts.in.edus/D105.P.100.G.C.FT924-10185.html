[1] Over the weekend Russia dispatched 3,000 special troops [2] to halt ethnic fighting in the troubled Caucasus Mountains. [3] Inguish rebels in North Ossetia were reported to have taken hostage 80 Interior Ministry soldiers based in the region. [4] The Inguish, a regional ethnic group, are demanding return of lands [5] that Stalin expropriated [6] and declared part of North Ossetia. [7] This was the second recent troop deployment. [8] Last week soldiers were sent to help crush a coup attempt in Tajikistan. [9] Several other ethnic conflicts are continuing in republics of the former Soviet Union [10] that are located in the Caucasus Mountains.
