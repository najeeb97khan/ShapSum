[1] Port workers in Szczecin, Poland and southwestern coal miners declared [2] sympathy strikes with miners demanding the legalization of Solidarity according to opposition activists. [3] A local prosecutor declared the strike illegal. [4] In support of 3000 colleagues striking for a second day at a mine in Jastrzebie, [5] workers at a nearby mine refused to take up their tools [6] and launched their own strike. [7] The strikes mark the eighth anniversary of the birth of Solidarity, [8] which Communist authorities [9] crushed in a 1981 military crackdown. [10] Miners are demanding the legalization of Solidarity, [11] pay raises [12] and reinstatement of workers fired for union activities.
