[1] Iraqi President Saddam Hussein's occupation of Kuwait reflects his need to escape from economic and social problems arising from the eight-year war with Iran. [2] Saddam says he fought the war to protect Sunni Arabs from the fundamentalist Shiite Persian revolution in Iran [3] and should not be expected to repay war debts to fellow Sunni Arabs. [4] By focusing on claimed misdeeds of Kuwait he hopes to divert attention from the economic and social chaos reigning at home [5] while gaining the wealth and territory represented by Kuwait. [6] This is the first time that an Arab state has deposed the government of a fellow Arab League member.
