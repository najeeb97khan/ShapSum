[1] A tornado carved a three-mile stretch of destruction in Huntsville, AL. [2] It killed 17 people, injured 463, [3] and left 1,000 homeless. [4] It was one of a series [5] that touched down Wednesday in an arc [6] spanning the Deep South to the Midwest causing at least 19 injuries and far-flung damage. [7] Seven thunderstorms are crossing the Northeast, and tornado watches have been put out to states in the area. [8] The tornado that struck Huntsville was Alabama's deadliest [9] since a 1975 twister killed 22 people in Birmingham. [10] No serious damage was reported from NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center and the Army's Redstone Arsenal.
