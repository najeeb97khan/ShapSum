[1] Virginia Thomas, the white wife of black Supreme Court nominee Clarence Thomas, is a brainy lawyer with an impressive career. [2] Critics see her as a woman with strong opinions on issues [3] that are bound to come before the court. [4] Women's-rights activists cite her opposition to comparable-worth legislation and some religious groups dislike her anti-cult activities. [5] Some blacks feel the Thomases' marriage shows his rejection of the black community. [6] But in the eyes of the Thomases they have stood for true equality [7] and rejected programs that actually harm women and minorities by lowering standards. [8] They advocate and live their belief in a colorblind society.
