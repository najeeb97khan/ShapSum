[1] John Lennon decided [2] that it was time to make music again after devoting several years to his son [3] while Yoko Ono ran the family business. [4] Lennon cut two more albums before his death in December, 1980. [5] Since then, Ono has overseen the release of several albums of John's music and the syndication of a radio program featuring previously unpublished songs. [6] Many believe that Ono has made millions promoting these ventures [7] and she has been called "The Merry Widow". Ono is [8] circumspect about where the money goes except to say [9] that some goes to the Spirit Foundation, the charitable foundation [10] that she and John founded.
