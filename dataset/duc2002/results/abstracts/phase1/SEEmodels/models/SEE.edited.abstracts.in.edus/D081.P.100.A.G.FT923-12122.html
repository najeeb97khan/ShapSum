[1] Workers at KGHM in Lubin, Poland's copper producing combine went on strike yesterday [2] as coal-mining unions staged token stoppages in a third of the country's pits in a major challenge to the government's economic policies. [3] The KGHM strike came despite a management offer to increase wages by 20%. [4] Coal mining unions are also demanding wage increases as well as a clear restructuring strategy for their industry. [5] The copper industry continues to show profit [6] but coal mining is running at a loss. [7] This has led to temporary lay-offs, [8] which hit miners' incomes [9] and led to the present bout of unrest.
