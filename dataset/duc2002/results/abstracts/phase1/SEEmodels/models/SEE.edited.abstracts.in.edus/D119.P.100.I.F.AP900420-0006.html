[1] President Bush is expected to name Melissa Wells, currently ambassador to Mozambique, [2] to be ambassador to Nicaragua. [3] The administration apparently believes that a female ambassador can relate better to the new president Violeta Chamorro. [4] Several male career diplomats with experience in Latin American affairs were passed over in the selection of Ms Wells. [5] Ms Wells was born in Estonia [6] and has no Latin American experience [7] but does speak Spanish. [8] The US and Nicaragua have not had ambassadors in each other's country since July 1988 [9] when President Daniel Ortega expelled [10] the US ambassador and President Reagan expelled the Nicaraguan ambassador.
