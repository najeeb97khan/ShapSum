[1] Authorities charges a ferry captain with negligent homicide after the boat, [2] overcrowded with New Year's travelers sank off Guatemala' east coast. [3] Sixty-seven people drowned, [4] 14 were missing [5] and 46 survived. [6] The ferry ran out of fuel mid-way in its journey [7] and sank [8] after a naval patrol boat tried to tow it to port. [9] The ferry captain was slow to respond to requests by the skipper of the naval patrol boat [10] to take control of the ferry's wheel once it was under tow. [11] The ferry captain radioed [12] that he had taken the wheel [13] just as the boat began to capsize.
