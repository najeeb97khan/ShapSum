[1] Hurricane Hugo, packing winds of up to 135 mph, [2] raged into Charleston Thursday night. [3] Dubbed a "killer hurricane" by Mayor Joseph Riley Jr., [4] it is the first major hurricane [5] to hit the 300-year-old city since 1959. [6] The worst disaster [7] to strike the city in recent decades [8] was a tornado in 1938 [9] that killed 32 people [10] and injured hundreds. [11] The worst natural disaster [12] to beset the city ever occurred on Aug. 32, 1886, [13] when an earthquake measuring 7.7 on the Richter scale struck, [14] claiming 110 lives [15] and destroying 102 buildings. [16] Millions of tourists annually visit the historic city and its nearby beaches.
