[1] A strong earthquake hit Nepal and parts of eastern India on Sunday, [2] triggering landslides and collapsing buildings. [3] First reports said [4] 100 people may have died. [5] Nepal's Department of Geology said [6] the earthquake measured 5.7 on the Richter scale. [7] The quake, [8] lasting about 40 seconds, [9] was centered near Udayapur, a town near the Indian- Nepalese border and 100 miles southeast of Katmandu, Nepal's capital. [10] The quake was also felt in the Indian capital of New Delhi and Calcutta, [11] but there were no reports of casualties. [12] In New Delhi, the Seismological Observatory said [13] the quake measured 6.5 on the Richter scale.
