[1] The 30-ton ferry Justo Rufino Barrios II, which sank off Guatemala's east coast Sunday, [2] was authorized to carry 90 passengers [3] but had 120. [4] It ran out of fuel midway on a 16-mile run across Amatique Bay from Livingston to Puerto Barrios. [5] The skipper of a patrol boat towing it to port requested that 22-year-old ferry captain Juan Pablo Ezquibel take control of the ferry's wheel, [6] but Esquibel did so only when the boat began to capsize, [7] after passengers ran to one side [8] when the boat leaned while riding a small wave. [9] Sixty-seven people drowned and 14 are missing. [10] Esquibel was arrested for negligent homicide.
