[1] On January 16, 1989, Patrick Edward Purdy fired an AK-47 automatic assault rifle into a crowded playground at Cleveland Elementary School in Stockton, California, [2] killing five children [3] and wounding 30, [4] then killing himself. [5] All the dead were children of Southeast Asian refugees, [6] as were 60% of the school's students. [7] Translators and Buddhist priests assisted families, [8] and returning students received counseling. [9] A South Carolina school where a similar attack occurred sent encouragement. [10] Governor Deukmejian spoke at one memorial service. [11] Anti-racism statements were made. [12] Purdy, 24, attended Cleveland Elementary through third grade.
