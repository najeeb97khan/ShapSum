[1] The cities of Charleston, SC and San Francisco have similarities and differences [2] when they were the scenes of two back-to-back disasters: [3] Hurricane Hugo and the Bay Area earthquake. [4] Charleston had warning [5] but San Francisco did not. [6] Charleston's neighborhoods were littered with debris, [7] 80% of the buildings were damaged, [8] and power was out. [9] San Francisco's streets were passable within a day and power was restored overnight. [10] Charleston, however, in contrast to San Francisco, could quickly assess structural damage [11] and it lost no major economic area such as the Bay Bridge. [12] Both have experienced delays in disaster relief from the federal government.
