[1] The coveted Booker Prize for the year's best work of fiction was awarded to Peter Carey Tuesday evening. [2] The Booker, now worth 26,250 dollars, is administered by Book Trust, an educational charity promoting books, [3] and is sponsored by Booker, an international food and farming company. [4] Carey won for his novel "Oscar and Lucinda" [5] that tells of a romance between an English gambler and an Australian heiress [6] who meet on a steamship's maiden voyage to Australia in the middle of the 19th century. [7] Carey, an Australian, was the only non-Briton in the group of six on the short-list.
