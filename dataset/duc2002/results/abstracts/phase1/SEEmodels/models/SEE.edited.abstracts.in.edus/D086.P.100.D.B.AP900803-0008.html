[1] The events between Iraq and Kuwait from July 17 through August 2 resulted in war. [2] Saddam Hussein accused Kuwait of driving down oil prices by overproduction thereby costing Iraq billions in lost revenues, [3] of stealing Iraqi oil from a common field along their border, and of conspiring with the U.S. and Israel to weaken Iraq. [4] Kuwait suggested [5] that Iraq's belligerence was to force Kuwait to forgive billions of Iraqi war debt. [6] Kuwait accepted an OPEC plan [7] to halt overproduction of oil. [8] Iraq then amassed troops on Kuwait's border, [9] pulled out of talks being held in Saudi Arabia with Kuwait [10] to resolve issues [11] and attacked.
