[1] Hurricane Hugo raged into Charleston Thursday night packing 135-mph winds. [2] Few residents had experienced such a dangerous, killer hurricane, the first major storm to strike the city since Hurricane Gracie came ashore 70 miles south in 1959. [3] In 1938, a tornado tore through the city killing 32, [4] injuring hundreds, [5] and causing $2 million in property damage. [6] The worst natural disaster to hit Charleston occurred Aug 31, 1886, [7] when an earthquake measuring 7.7 on the Richter scale claimed 110 lives, [8] destroyed 102 buildings, [9] and tossed most of the city's chimneys into the streets. [10] The city has survived two wars and any number of smaller hurricanes, [11] which residents call "September gales."
