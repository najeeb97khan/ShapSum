[1] John Major, Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher's Chancellor of the Exchequer and her chosen heir apparent, was elected Conservative Party leader today [2] and will become prime minister. [3] Major won 185 votes. [4] Both his opponents, former Defense Secretary Heseltine and Foreign Secretary Hurd, with131 votes and 56 votes, respectively, [5] conceded defeat. [6] Queen Elizabeth II will summon Major [7] and ask him to form a cabinet after Thatcher surrenders her seals of office at Buckingham Palace on Wednesday. [8] Major, 47, a former welfare recipient, was elected to Parliament in1979, [9] was appointed foreign secretary in July 1989, [10] and Chancellor of the Exchequer three months later.
