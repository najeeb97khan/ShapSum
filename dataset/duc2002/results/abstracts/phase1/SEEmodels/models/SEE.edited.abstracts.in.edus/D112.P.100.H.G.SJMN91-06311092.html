[1] Robert Maxwell, the flamboyant millionaire [2] who built a global publishing empire, [3] was found dead yesterday in waters off the Canary Islands [4] where he had been vacationing on his yacht. [5] Maxwell, 68, who earlier this year had bought the New York Daily News, [6] vanished overnight from his yacht. [7] The Czechoslovak-born Maxwell, who escaped the Holocaust [8] and immigrated to Britain in 1940, [9] personally ran his own publishing empire. [10] His sons Kevin and Ian took over the business Tuesday. [11] His death prompted immediate concern in British financial circles about the future of his debt-laden empire and the future of the New York Daily News.
