[1] William Wegman's two Weimaraners serve [2] as both companions and artist models to the artist. [3] The breed is affectionate [4] and can hold a pose. [5] The mother-daughter dogs live with Wegman in his home, a former Orthodox synagogue on Manhattan's Lower East Side. [6] The 47-year-old artist began his association with Weimaraners in 1970 [7] when he acquired a puppy, Man Ray, for $35 dollars. [8] He quickly learned that the breed makes perfect subjects. [9] When he died at the age of 11, [10] Man Ray was world famous. [11] One of Wegman's current dogs, Fay Ray, is now a familiar face both with artists and among children [12] who watch Sesame Street.
