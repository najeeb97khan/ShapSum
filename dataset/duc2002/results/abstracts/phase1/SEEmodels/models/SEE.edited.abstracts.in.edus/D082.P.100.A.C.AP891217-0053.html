[1] Sunday was the first day of memorial ceremonies for Andrei Sakharov. [2] As many as 70,000 mourners viewed his body in the Youth Palace. [3] Sub-zero temperatures prevailed, [4] but the mourners waited in a line [5] that stretched a mile from the Palace. [6] It was a vast outpouring of grief for a man once ostracized [7] and sent into exile. [8] It was as if the populace was apologizing for being late in recognizing his contributions. [9] He will be buried on Monday. [10] Yelena Bonner, his widow and fellow activist, said [11] that she hopes his writings will finally be made widely available in his homeland.
