[1] The earthquake that struck northern Iran on Thursday killing about 25,000 people registered 7.7 on the Richter scale. [2] Many of the victims lived in fragile homes built of a ceramic-type brick or adobe that collapses easily [3] and the quake occurred just after midnight, [4] so most people were inside asleep when it hit. [5] It occurred in an area of Iran where most of the buildings are on a flood plain of loosely deposited soil [6] that shifts and allows structures to collapse. [7] The quake of 7.7 magnitude was the largest ever recorded in that area [8] where two major plates of the earth's crust meet.
