[1] At dog shows throughout the world, proud owners compete their charges for the awards and glory that await the champions. [2] Prince, a fluffy, little Pomeranian, [3] after winning at the prestigious Westminster Kennel Club Show in Madison Square Garden was honored at a lunch held at a Sardi's, a prominent New York City restaurant. [4] However, not all "famous" dogs are show dogs. [5] Sharing the limelight at Sardi's was Nero, New York City's top police dog. [6] Nero helped the police make hundreds of arrests [7] and apprehend more than 50 felons. [8] And one need only look at the renown attained by photographer William Wegman's Weimaraners. [9] Artists worldwide know his dogs, [10] as do children who watch "Sesame Street". [11] But it's the show dogs [12] that crisscross the country [13] to strut their stuff, [14] sometimes as many as the 2,500 dogs representing [15] 138 breeds [16] that competed at the All Breed Dog Show and Obedience trials in Los Angeles. [17] Some may possibly go to Tel Aviv, Israel for its annual show. [18] Bill McFadden, a professional dog handler, works about 120 shows a year throughout the U.S., [19] showing 20 to 25 dogs at each show. [20] Among those he shows is Kerrageen's Hotspur, the top award winning Kerry blue terrier of all time.
