[1] A cloud of ash-laden steam rose up to 11,500 feet above Mount Pinatubo today. [2] The Philippine volcano erupted Saturday after six centuries of dormancy. [3] According to Peter Lipman, [4] a Menlo Park U.S. Geological Survey volcanologist, a major eruption could occur causing death and destruction from hellish avalanches of hot ash. [5] Lipman is in contact with five USGS scientists [6] who remained at Clark AFB [7] which was evacuated along with thousands of Filipinos living near the volcano. [8] Thus far, ash has blown away from the base, [9] but the summer monsoon weather pattern will reverse the pattern.
