[1] Recovery efforts continue in Huntsville, Alabama, [2] where a tornado carved a swath of destruction, [3] killing 17, [4] injuring 463 [5] and leaving 1,000 homeless. [6] This tornado was one in a series [7] that struck in at least seven Midwestern and Southern states. [8] The storm system is now moving through the Mid-Atlantic states toward New England. [9] The tornado struck Huntsville with virtually no warning [10] as the evening rush hour approached. [11] Although an elementary school was hit, [12] most of the dead were in apartments, stores, or cars. [13] Downed power lines and blocked roads continue to afflict the area. [14] The nearby Redstone Arsenal and NASA's Marshall facility were not damaged.
