[1] Soviet human rights activist Andrei D. Sakharov was elected in April to the new Soviet Congress of People's Deputies, two years [2] after being released from seven years of internal exile. [3] On Sunday, he praised the Soviet's new course in world affairs, demonstrated by withdrawal from Afghanistan, [4] but criticized internal measures restricting freedom of speech.
