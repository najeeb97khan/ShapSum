[1] Greek divers located the remains of a wooden sailing vessel on 3 May 1988. [2] The vessel was found near Chios, a Greek island in the eastern Aegean Sea, [3] where a Greek naval vessel sank a Turkish warship on 7 June 1882. [4] The Turkish vessel was identified as the Bourloti Seimaz, or translated as "Explosives Invincible". It was returning from raiding and killing thousands of Greeks on Chios. [5] More than 1,000, including 400 Greeks, [6] were aboard the Turkish ship. [7] The ship was crammed with loot [8] taken from Chios. [9] The Greek divers hope to interest American and Turkish experts [10] to help in recovering the vessel.
