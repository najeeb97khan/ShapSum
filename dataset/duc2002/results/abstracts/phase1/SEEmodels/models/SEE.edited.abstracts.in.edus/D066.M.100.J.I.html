[1] Sam Walton, who died in April 1992, [2] revolutionized discount retailing. [3] Born in Oklahoma, [4] he became the richest man in America. [5] He took discount shopping to rural America founding Wal-Mart Discount City, [6] which became Wal-Mart, the world's largest retailer by 1992. [7] Sam was frugal and homespun and used pop-in visits [8] to test for happy customers and employees. [9] To preserve his personal legacy, he opened a Wal-Mart visitors' center [10] and wrote his autobiography, [11] filling it with "good ole" anecdotes. [12] A 1994 book highlighted his ability [13] to copy and adapt, the chain's responsive management, and the chain's impact on Mom-and-Pop stores and anti-union position.
