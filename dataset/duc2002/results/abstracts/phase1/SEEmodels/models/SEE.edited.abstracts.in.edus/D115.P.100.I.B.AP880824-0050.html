[1] Nepalese police have bulldozed more than 50 brick and mud houses [2] that have been made unstable by an earthquake [3] that struck near the India-Nepal border [4] and killed at least 749 people. [5] Police said they will continue demolishing houses until satisfied [6] that those remaining are safe to live in. [7] Most homeless have moved in with neighbors [8] or found shelter in tents [9] while waiting to hear from the government about compensation or new homes. [10] Prime Minister Shrestha [11] while visiting Nepal's hardest hit town, Dharan, on Tuesday said, [12] "This is the biggest calamity to strike Nepal." [13] The quake registered 6.5 on the Richter scale.
