[1] President Corazon Aquino used military firepower [2] to end the military led rebellion on Mindanao. [3] The Oct. 4-6 uprising never spread to other islands. [4] Aquino has survived two major coup attempts and several military mutinies. [5] The current rebellion is the latest of several military mutinies. [6] In August Aquino reversed a long-held policy [7] and said [8] she was willing to negotiate cease-fires with Communist guerillas and former soldiers [9] who have staged coup attempts. [10] In December 1989 a confrontation between pro-coup supporters and Aquino supporters occurred at the Philippine consulate in Los Angeles. [11] Aquino's government has also had its political controversies. [12] Her opposition to the nomination of Secretary-designate Abad raised doubts about her commitment to social reform. [13] In 1989, her Vice President refused to condemn the insurrection that was then unfolding. [14] Aquino rose to power in 1986 after dictator Ferdinand Marcos, [15] accused of stealing billions of government funds, [16] was ousted. [17] June1988 news stories quoted sources saying [18] Aquino would allow Marcos to return to the Philippines to stand trial, [19] but a year later she stated [20] the critically ill Marcos could not return to die, [21] nor could he be buried in the Philippines. [22] Aquino traveled to West German in 1989 seeking economic assistance.
