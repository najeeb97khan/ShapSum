[1] Senate confirmation hearings for President Bush's nominees for ambassadorships have shown mixed results for the administration. [2] Some nominees like Shirley Temple Black the designated ambassador to Czechoslovakia, [3] sailed through the process. [4] The nominees for Spain, Switzerland, Australia, and New Zealand faced strong questioning during Senate committee hearings [5] but were eventually slimly approved. [6] Democrats and retired diplomats believed [7] that three of the nominees had no real qualifications [8] but were picked because of large money contributions to the Republican Party. [9] Democrats also had concern about the nominee [10] to be ambassador to Italy. [11] Senator Jesse Helms and other conservatives objected to one nominee [12] because he was a Democrat [13] and were thought likely to have such strong objections to another [14] that her name was withdrawn. [15] That woman was initially picked to be ambassador to Nicaragua [16] since that country was to install a woman as president [17] but the potential objections led to a seasoned Latin American expert being named. [18] The administration picked Thomas Pickering, the ambassador to the UN [19] to be ambassador to India. [20] This selection of a well-known diplomat underscores the administration concern regarding the Indian subcontinent [21] where India and Pakistan, both now nuclear capable, [22] continue to feud over Kashmir.
