[1] A powerful cold front moved through the eastern half of the United States on November 15 and 16, [2] spawning tornados, violent thunderstorms, and heavy rainfall. [3] Thirteen states reported deaths, injuries, or damage. [4] Hardest hit was Huntsville, Alabama, [5] where a tornado leveled a shopping district, apartment development, and elementary school. [6] Seventeen were killed, [7] 463 injured, [8] and 1000 left homeless. [9] Damages were estimated at $100 million. [10] President Bush declared Huntsville a disaster area. [11] Marilyn Quayle and Alabama's governor and senators toured the area on November 18. [12] This storm front killed nine others, including seven school children in Newburgh, New York.
