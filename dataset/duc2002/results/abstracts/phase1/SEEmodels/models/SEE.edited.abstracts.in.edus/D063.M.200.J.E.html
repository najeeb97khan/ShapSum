[1] A barracks at the Royal Marine Music School in Deal was leveled in an explosion on Friday, [2] which killed 10 persons and injured 22 others, 12 seriously. [3] Responsibility for the blast was claimed by the Irish Republican Army, [4] which said the attack was in response to a recent speech made by Prime Minister Thatcher to the Ulster Defense Regiment. [5] Calling the speech an "act of war," [6] the IRA said [7] such attacks would continue until the British withdrew their forces from Northern Ireland [8] and allow a unification of the protestant Northern Ireland with the Roman Catholic Republic of Ireland. [9] The explosion, [10] which could be heard two miles away, [11] came at 8:30 in the morning, [12] just as the musicians were taking a coffee break. [13] Destroying the barracks, [14] it also severely damaged a number of neighborhood homes, [15] causing minor injuries among their residents. [16] Neighbors of the barracks as well as opposition politicians blamed the use of private security firms, [17] which, they say, [18] caused the security at the base to be lax. [19] Defending the use of the firms, [20] Defense Secretary Tome King issued an alert for all military bases in Britain. [21] The attack is the latest in a series of deadly attacks against British bases in the last 20 years.
