[1] The Korean-flagged fishing vessel Chin Yuan Hsing was scuttled in waters off Argentina on 21 May 1994. [2] Adverse weather conditions prevailed [3] when the Argentine corvette Spiro spotted [4] the Korean ship fishing illegally in restricted Argentine waters. [5] The Korean vessel did not respond to the corvette's warning. [6] Instead, the Korean crew sank their ship, [7] and transferred to another Korean ship sailing nearby. [8] In accordance with a UK-Argentine agreement, the Argentine Navy turned the surveillance of the second Korean vessel over to the British [9] when it approached within 15 nautical miles of the Malvinas (Falkland) Islands.
