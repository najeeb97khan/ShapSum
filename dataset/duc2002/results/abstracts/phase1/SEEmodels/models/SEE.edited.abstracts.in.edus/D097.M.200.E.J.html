[1] American Insurance Service Group says Hurricane Hugo, [2] which ravaged the Caribbean [3] and the Carolinas last week, is responsible for record hurricane losses. [4] State Farm Insurance expects claims of approximately $500 million in the Carolinas. [5] Nationwide Insurance is projecting claims near $100 million. [6] Though the Hugo-related losses are substantial, [7] most companies have sufficient funds to cover them. [8] Some companies, however, will reduce third-quarter earnings. [9] Hugo slammed into Charleston Thursday night with top winds of 135 mph. [10] Upgraded to a Category 4 hurricane hours before it struck the historic city, [11] it caused widespread damage throughout the Carolinas. [12] Only two lives were lost in Charleston, however, and only 17 total in South Carolina. [13] Officials credit the low number of fatalities to residents [14] who monitored the hurricane [15] and evacuated. [16] Computer models at the National Hurricane Center used satellite information [17] to track Hugo's path into Charleston. [18] Before the storm's arrival in South Carolina, the governor declared [19] a state of emergency and National Guardsmen were ordered to assist a mandatory evacuation of coastal barrier islands and shorefront. [20] Thousands fled inland [21] or went to shelters. [22] Kingston, New York, assisted by Charleston during the Revolutionary War, [23] is repaying the kindness [24] by sending money [25] to help the city recover.
