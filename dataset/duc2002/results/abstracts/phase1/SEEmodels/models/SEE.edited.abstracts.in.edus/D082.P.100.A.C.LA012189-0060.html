[1] Soviet physicists, in a rebuff to the Soviet Union's scientific establishment, [2] voted on Friday to nominate Andrei Sakharov to be a candidate in the March parliamentary elections. [3] The overwhelming vote, [4] taken in secrecy, [5] came only two days after the Governing Board of the Academy of Sciences rejected Sakharov's candidacy. [6] The vote was more an expression of respect for Sakharov than a specific endorsement of his political views. [7] Sakharov used the occasion to outline a detailed program that would transform the Soviet Union. [8] Complicated voting procedures still make it uncertain that Sakharov's name will appear on the ballot.
