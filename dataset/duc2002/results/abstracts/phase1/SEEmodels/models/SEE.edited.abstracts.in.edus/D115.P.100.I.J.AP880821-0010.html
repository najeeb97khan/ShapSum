[1] An earthquake measuring 6.5 on the Richter scale struck the India-Nepal border region Sunday, [2] triggering landslides and destroying houses. [3] In India, least 100 people were killed in Bihar and at least 1000 more injured in house collapses in Bihar and surrounding states. [4] In Nepal, authorities said at least 137 were killed [5] and more than 500 were injured. [6] Officials of both countries expect the death toll to rise [7] after they receive reports from the harder hit remote areas. [8] Both countries were sending emergency medical teams to the area. [9] Rescue operations are being hampered by the monsoon rains [10] which began in late June.
