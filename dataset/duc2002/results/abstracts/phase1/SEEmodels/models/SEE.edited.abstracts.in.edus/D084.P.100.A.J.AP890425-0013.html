[1] Relatives of the 47 victims killed in the No.2 gun explosion aboard the Iowa last Wednesday were among the 6000 people [2] who attended a memorial at Norfolk Naval Air Station on Monday. [3] President Bush, a WWII Navy pilot, and the ship's commander, Capt. Fred P. Moosally, led the service. [4] Moosally praised the No. 2 gun turret crew as the best on the ship. [5] At the end of the service, President and Mrs. Bush greeted [6] and consoled the families of the lost men. [7] As many as 500 Iowa crewmen, [8] delayed by traffic, [9] were unable to attend [10] because of presidential security measures.
