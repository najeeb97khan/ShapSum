[1] The low number of deaths from powerful Hurricane Hugo can be credited to residents' healthy respect for forecasters' warnings. [2] The storm caused billions in damage [3] and claimed 17 lives in South Carolina. [4] Only two died in the Charleston area, [5] which bore the brunt of Hugo's 135mph winds. [6] 18,000 thousand endangered residents went into shelters and tens of thousands evacuated inland, [7] heading up I26 toward Columbia. [8] The last big storm to hit South Carolina was Hurricane David in 1979. [9] Several big storms have threatened since then but veered away. [10] Officials worried that Charlestonians wouldn't heed warnings about Hugo since there had been so many false alarms.
