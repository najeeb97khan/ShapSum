[1] British media entrepreneur Robert Maxwell, publisher of Britain's Daily Mirror, bought the New York Daily News in 1990. [2] He then planned to raise $448 million through the sale of shares in his Mirror Group Newspaper Ltd. of Britain. [3] He bought Mirror Group in 1984 [4] and turned it into one of Britain's most profitable newspaper groups. [5] A year after the Daily News purchase, he was found dead in the waters off the Canary Island [6] where he was vacationing on his yacht. [7] Investigators say Maxwell siphoned money from his companies in an attempt to deal with crushing debts. [8] Some $630 million was taken from his Mirror Group pension fund without authority. [9] His sons Kevin and Ian, who took over the business, were investigated [10] and found guilty for their knowledge of illegal schemes using two Swiss trusts, a private UK trust company and a US investment bank [11] to support the Maxwell Communications Company (MCC). [12] In a flurry of foreign exchange deals, the MCC would buy currency in one financial center promising, but often failing, to make payment in another center; [13] thus getting an unauthorized loan. [14] Some repayments were made by looting pension funds assets.
