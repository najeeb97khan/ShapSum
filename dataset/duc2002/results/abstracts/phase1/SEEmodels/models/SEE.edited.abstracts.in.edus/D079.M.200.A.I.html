[1] Hurricane Gilbert has been downgraded to a tropical storm [2] and is bringing heavy rain, flooding, and mudslides to northeastern Mexico. [3] Ten policemen were swept away in Monterrey and buses full of evacuees were lost in Matamoros. [4] Gilbert's feared path onto the Texas Gulf Coast did not materialize, [5] although a number of tornadoes were spawned on Friday and Saturday. [6] In Mexico, relief operations are continuing in order to bring food, water, and medical supplies to survivors. [7] Gilbert formed in the eastern Caribbean [8] and for the last week rampaged through the Caribbean, [9] bringing destruction to the Dominican Republic, Jamaica, and the Cayman Islands. [10] Damages in Jamaica, [11] where six were killed, [12] are estimated at $8 billion. [13] On Tuesday Gilbert was upgraded to a Category 5 storm, the highest category and only the third recorded. [14] Winds were clocked at 175 mph and the lowest pressure [15] ever recorded in the Western Hemisphere was measured at its center. [16] Wednesday Gilbert slammed into the Yucatan Peninsula with 160 mph winds, [17] severely damaging the resort areas in Cancun and Cozumel, [18] severing electricity and communications over a wide area, [19] and causing widespread flooding. [20] Gilbert has killed at least 109 people [21] and left at least 800,000 homeless.
