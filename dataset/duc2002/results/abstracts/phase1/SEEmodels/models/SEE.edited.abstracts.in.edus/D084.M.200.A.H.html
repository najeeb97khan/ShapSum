[1] An explosion in the battleship Iowa's No.2 turret on Wednesday, April 19, 1989, during a training exercise NE of Puerto Rico, killed 47. [2] Naval investigators concluded the explosion occurred while gunpowder was in transit from a powder hoist. [3] Crew in upper turret chambers died instantly in the explosion and fire [4] while those at lower levels suffocated [5] as the blast sucked out oxygen. [6] Eleven escaped from powder magazines at the turret's base. [7] On Sunday, 3000 family and friends welcomed the Iowa home to Norfolk. [8] Sailors with black arm bands lined the Iowa's rails and two surviving guncrew members stood on the blackened No.2 turret. [9] On Monday, President Bush spoke to 8000 mourners at a memorial service and Iowa commander Capt. Fred Moosally praised the victims. [10] A moratorium was placed on firing the 16-inch guns, [11] which are criticized as outdated and dangerous, [12] using gunpowder packed in silk bags that are rammed into a gun after the shell and ignited by electric spark. [13] Newer guns' powder is packed in metal containers. [14] Missiles have integrated propellants. [15] The four WWII-era battleships came out of mothballs in 1982. [16] Critics say they have outlived their purpose [17] but the Navy disagrees and is keeping them in service. [18] The Senate Armed Services Committee will hold hearings on the explosion.
