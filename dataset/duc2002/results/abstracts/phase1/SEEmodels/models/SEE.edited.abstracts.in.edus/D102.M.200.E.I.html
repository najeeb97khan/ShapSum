[1] The Queen of TV comedy, Lucille Ball, [2] died on April 26, 1989, from a ruptured abdominal aorta. [3] President Bush, former President Reagan, the president of CBS, American comedians and actors, neighbors, residents of her hometown, television critics, and just plain fans paid tributes to her. [4] Her burial was private, [5] but more than 2,000 attended three memorial services held on May 8. [6] Bob Hope, a frequent co-star and friend, created a TV special in her honor. [7] Two of her surviving TV co-stars, Gail Gordon and Keith Thibodeaux, remembered her fondly. She is best remembered for her physical and verbal comedy and superb timing. [8] She was born in 1911 in Jamestown, N.Y., [9] and, after staring in high school plays, [10] became a model and chorus girl, [11] moving in 1933 to Hollywood. [12] There, she was in over 75 movies, usually playing the second banana. [13] In 1940 she married Desi Arnaz, [14] and together they revolutionized television comedy with "I Love Lucy" [15] which started in 1951. [16] Filmed with three cameras before a live audience, [17] it became a rerun gold mine [18] and has been seen in 80 countries. [19] After her divorce, [20] she remarried [21] and continued to be a successful television entrepreneur and star of television and screen.
