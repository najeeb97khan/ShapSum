[1] At the National Hurricane Center in Coral Gables, Florida, forecasters working 12-hour days monitored Hurricane Hugo using computers and satellites. [2] They calculated where it would strike [3] and got emergency operations going in those areas. [4] Director Bob Sheets appeared on TV all week [5] and credited the ever-present but bothersome media with getting the word out. [6] A hurricane warning, involving emergency preparations and evacuation plans, costs $50 million. [7] Before the warning system was established, a Hugo- sized storm would have killed thousands. [8] So far there were no reports of coastal-area deaths. [9] After Hugo's landfall, forecasters relaxed [10] but still followed three other Atlantic weather systems and one in the Pacific.
