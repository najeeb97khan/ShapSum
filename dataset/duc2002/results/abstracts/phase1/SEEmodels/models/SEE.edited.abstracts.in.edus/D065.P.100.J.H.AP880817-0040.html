[1] Indiana Republican Senator Dan Quayle, 41, was chosen by George Bush to be his presidential running mate. [2] Quayle, from Huntington, Indiana, [3] graduated from a small liberal arts college, DePauw University, in 1970 [4] and went to work in the Indiana Attorney General's office. [5] He met his wife Marilyn, 39, when they were law students at Indiana University in Indianapolis. [6] They married in 1972 while still in school. [7] They have three children, 14-year-old son Tucker, 12-year-old son Benjamin, and 9-year-old daughter Corinne. [8] Quayle is said to be the same nice guy he was in law school. [9] His sister Martha Saddler lives in Huntington.
