[1] On Sunday searchers in the rural area of eastern Ohio found a body in debris along Wegee Creek [2] and extracted two from the Ohio River, [3] bringing the death toll from flash floods [4] that swept through the area last Thursday to19. [5] Eighteen people are still missing. [6] As many as 70 houses were destroyed. [7] Investigators Sunday blamed the flash floods on an unusually heavy rainstorm [8] that overwhelmed the designed flow capacities of three bridges over the Wegee and Pipe creeks. [9] Rainwater running down hillsides forced debris into the creeks, [10] forming dams at each bridge. [11] The "dams" broke under the water pressure.
