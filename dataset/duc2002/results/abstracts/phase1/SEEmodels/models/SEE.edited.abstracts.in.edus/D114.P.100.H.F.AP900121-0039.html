[1] Fighting between police and Moslem separatists in Srinagar, [2] the summer capital of the Jammu-Kashmir state killed at least 21 [3] and wounded 100. [4] Police had imposed a 24-hour curfew [5] to quell the violence resulting from the militants, [6] led by the Jammu-Kashmir Liberation Front, [7] holding anti-India demonstrations. [8] However, crowds defied the curfew. [9] Heavy rains caused fighting to taper off [10] but the situation remains tense and dangerous. [11] Both India and Pakistan claim parts of Kashmir in a dispute [12] that started after the state was partitioned in 1947. [13] Most of the population of Kashmir is Moslem [14] while Jammu is predominately Hindu.
