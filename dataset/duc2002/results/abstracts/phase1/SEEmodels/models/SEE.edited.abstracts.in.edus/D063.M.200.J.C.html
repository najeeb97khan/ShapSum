[1] An explosion at 8:28 A.M. on 22 September destroyed a military barracks in Deal, a town southeast of London in County Kent. [2] The barracks was home to the Royal Marines School of Music. [3] Ten military bandsmen were killed, and as many as 22 were injured, eight seriously. [4] Neighbors expressed shock at the strength of the blast. [5] No serious injuries to civilians were reported, [6] but neighboring homes suffered heavy damage. [7] The IRA claimed [8] responsibility for the attack, [9] linking it what they called a warlike speech delivered by Mrs. Thatcher during a recent visit to Northern Ireland. [10] Investigators said [11] the damage was illustrative of that caused by a bomb. [12] The IRA has a record [13] of attacking military installations, [14] and this latest attack was the more damaging than any in more than seven years. [15] The Prime Ministers of Britain and Ireland have denounced the attack. [16] Police are seeking three men with Irish accents who lived near the barracks for a short time. [17] Private security firms serve the barracks at Deal and 29 other bases in Britain. [18] Families of the victims and opposition party leaders have criticized the arrangement, [19] blaming the attack on lax security because of the private guards.
