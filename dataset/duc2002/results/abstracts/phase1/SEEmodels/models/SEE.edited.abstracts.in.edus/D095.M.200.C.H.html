[1] On Tuesday, January 16, 1989, Patrick Edward Purdy entered the grounds of Cleveland Elementary School in Stockton, [2] California and fired an AK-47 automatic assault rifle into a crowded playground. [3] He killed five children [4] and wounded 29 others and a teacher, [5] then killed himself. [6] All the dead and 19 of the wounded were children of Southeast Asian refugees, [7] as were 60% of the school's 970 students. [8] Translators and Buddhist priests assisted families, [9] who received financial aid from the county victim assistance program. [10] Students returning to school received counseling. [11] A South Carolina school where a similar attack occurred sent encouragement. [12] Governor Deukmejian spoke at one memorial service [13] and was considering a ban on assault weapon sales. [14] Anti-racism statements were made [15] but authorities denied a racial motive in the attack. [16] Purdy, AKA West, 24, blond and blue-eyed, attended Cleveland Elementary through third grade. [17] He dropped out of high school [18] and took welding classes, [19] then drifted around the country searching for work. [20] He had many LA area arrests for minor offenses, [21] served jail time, [22] and attempted suicide. [23] He had military and white supremacist leanings. [24] He was described as a loner, child alcoholic, dangerous, and mildly retarded. [25] His father was struck by a car and killed in 1981. [26] His mother remained unlocated.
