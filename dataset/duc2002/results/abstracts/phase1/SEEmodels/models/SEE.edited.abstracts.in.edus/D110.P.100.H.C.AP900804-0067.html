[1] Saudi Arabia's reaction to the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait has been subdued. [2] The presence of Iraqi forces near the border concerns the Saudis [3] who do not want to provoke Iraq into attacking Saudi oilfields only 150 miles south of the border. [4] Oil executives cite this as reason [5] for believing Saudi Arabia will not close the pipeline [6] that carries exported Iraqi oil across Saudi Arabia. [7] Saudi Arabia did join the other five members of the Gulf Cooperation Council in low-key condemnation of the invasion. [8] The Council maintains a military force of only 3,000 [9] that is no match for Iraq.
