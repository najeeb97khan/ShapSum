[1] Fighting has broken out sporadically in the Indian state of Jammu-Kashmir since January 20. [2] At least 1,034 people had been killed by late August. [3] The Indian government is battling Muslim insurgents seeking the formation of an independent, neutral, nation. [4] India (predominately Hindu) [5] and Pakistan (predominately Islamic) [6] have fought two wars over Kashmir [7] since the nations were established in 1947. [8] Jammu-Kashmir is 62 percent Muslim. [9] Insurgent tactics have included bombings, kidnappings, killings of suspected police informers, [10] ambushes of police vehicles, and attacks on police installations. [11] Large demonstrations have been held in Jammu-Kashmir and on the Pakistani border. [12] Indian tactics have include retaliatory fire, [13] arresting and killing insurgent leaders, 24-hour curfews, [14] disrupting and firing on demonstrations, [15] and interfering with funerals of dead insurgents. [16] The state is now governed directly from New Delhi. [17] In August, India increased border forces and operations [18] and killed 80 infiltrators, [19] claiming the insurgents are being trained and armed in Pakistan. [21] Pakistan denies these charges [22] but says it does support the Muslims' crusade for "self-determination." [23] The Prime Minister of Pakistan has sought the support of other Islamic nations. [24] The two nations have exchanged fire on several occasions, [25] but both say there is no cause for concern.
