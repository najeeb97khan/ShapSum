[1] The mysterious sinking of the Lucona in the Indian Ocean ignited a scandal [2] that led to high level political resignations twelve years later. [3] Six sailors died in the sinking, [4] which was believed deliberate and the wrongdoing of Udo Proksch. [5] The ship's cargo was listed as material for a uranium processing plant [6] and was insured for $16 million dollars. [7] The insurance company refused to pay, [8] alleging that the cargo was really scrap metal. [9] Proksch, wanted by Austrian authorities, has fled. [10] Parliament President Leopold Gratz and Interior Minister Karl Blecha, both friends of Proksch have resigned [11] because they allegedly helped Proksch.
