[1] In the recent San Francisco earthquake, why did some luxury four-story apartments or office buildings collapse while high-rises escaped virtually unscathed? [2] Accordingly, some buildings collapsed because of two things: [3] very poor soil and very poor structural design. [4] Some were built over landfill and others were erected earlier [5] when there were no earthquake codes. [6] Four-story buildings with garages underneath have no walls on one side. [7] Connections break and the front falls. [8] Skyscrapers usually survive because architects factor in wind gusts and earth movement. [9] In smaller buildings, contractors make sure [10] that vertical forces -such as snow and ice- cannot force a collapse.
