[1] On Friday, Nepal updated its death toll from Sunday's Himalayan earthquake to 704 [2] as reports arrived from remote area. [3] In India, the official death toll rose to 277, [4] 200 of which were in Bihar, [5] but the press and others claim [6] as many as 1500 may have perished. [7] Bihar is also threatened by flooding from swollen rivers. [8] The quake, measuring 6.5 on the Richter scale, [9] struck at 4:54 a.m. (local). [10] According to Nepal's Department of Geology, [11] it was centered near Udayapur, Nepal. [12] Monsoon rains, [13] overflowing rivers, [14] submerged bridges and roads and aftershocks hampered relief efforts and the search for victims in throughout the quake area. [15] In Dharan, Nepal, over 131 bodies were found, most in collapsed houses [16] that had used mud, [17] loosened by rain, between the bricks. [18] Karna Hyanju, a former Nepalese legislator, however, did not die in the quake. [19] He was killed after being accused of distributing relief supplies only to his supporters. [20] All but three of Nepal's 157 Peace Corps volunteers have been found safe. There are no Peace Corp workers in India. [21] The U.S. is sending financial aid to both counties. [22] Britain, U.N. agencies and the Red Cross sent medical teams to Nepal.
