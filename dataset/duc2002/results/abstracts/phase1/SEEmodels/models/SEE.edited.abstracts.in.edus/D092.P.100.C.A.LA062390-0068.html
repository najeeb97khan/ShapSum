[1] After years of animosity, the U.S. government has come to the aid of Iran, [2] channeling $300,000 in earthquake relief supplies through the American Red Cross. [3] The U.S. military stands ready to provide logistical support for any aid requested by Iran, [4] but it is unlikely that Iran would accept or the White House suggest a military airlift. [5] The quake hit northern Iran early Thursday, [6] leaving 29,000 dead by the latest count, [7] but the toll is expected to rise. [8] About 400,000 are homeless. [9] Iranian President Hashemi Rafsanjani has appealed for international aid and the American Red Cross has mobilized its local offices to raise funds for earthquake relief.
