[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev said [2] the failed power grab by hard-liners could prove to be an opportunity to further his democratic reforms. [3] He condemned the attempted takeover [4] as being against "the people, against democracy". He named a new acting KGB chief, new interior and defense ministers and said [5] he would do everything to "drive out the reactionary forces from the Communist Party". Gorbachev said [6] that he and Yeltsin were brought together by the situation [7] and he publicly praised Yeltsin [8] who had attained folk-hero status [9] by rallying hundreds of thousands of his supporters [10] to resist the coup.
