[1] The ferry Salem Express, returning from Jiddah, [2] sank off a Red Sea port Saturday night when stormy seas tossed it into a coral reef. [3] Four hundred sixty-two are missing. [4] Some survivors say life-preserver-clad crew pushed passengers aside [5] and took the life boats. [6] The engineer denies [7] this, saying only 10 crew surviving out of 71 indicates no widespread negligence. [8] He himself helped save passengers. [9] The ferry sank within 15 minutes. [10] Water entered through a hole in the ship's side and through boarding doors. [11] All but 10 of the 649 passengers were Egyptian, [12] many Muslims returning from pilgrimages to Mecca or from working in Saudi Arabia.
