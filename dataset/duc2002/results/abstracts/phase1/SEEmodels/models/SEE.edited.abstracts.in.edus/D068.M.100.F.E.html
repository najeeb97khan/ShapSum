[1] Checkpoint Charlie was lifted into history today during a ceremony including the foreign ministers of the four World War II allies. [2] Secretary of State Baker, [3] citing Checkpoint Charlie as a symbol of the Cold War, hoped its removal would bury the conflicts it created. [4] Soviet Foreign Minister Shevardnadze, the first Soviet foreign minister to visit Berlin, [5] called for a removal of Berlin's status as an allied controlled city. [6] Checkpoint Charlie became superfluous when the East Germany overthrew its communist-controlled government and the border between East and West Germany was opened. [7] Although the ceremony was closed to the public, residents of the street watched from their windows.
