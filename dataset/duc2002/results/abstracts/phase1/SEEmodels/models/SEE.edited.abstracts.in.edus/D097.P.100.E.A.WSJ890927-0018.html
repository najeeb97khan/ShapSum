[1] Losses to insurers from Hurricane Hugo far surpass the $752.5 million for Hurricane Frederick in 1979, [2] but analysts and company officials predict no increase in premium rates before mid-1990. [3] Mr. Ruoff of Fred S. James figures that even if losses reach $5 billion [4] insurers won't feel enough "financial pain" to make them boost premium rates right away. [5] In the last three years hurricane and storm seasons have been rather mild so insurers haven't had to dip into reserves. [6] Most insurers also protect themselves by buying reinsurance, [7] and even State Farm, which doesn't buy reinsurance, believes its reserves are large enough to absorb its losses.
