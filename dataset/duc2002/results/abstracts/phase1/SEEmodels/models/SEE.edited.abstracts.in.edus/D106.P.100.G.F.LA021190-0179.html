[1] World leaders welcomed South Africa's announcement [2] that Nelson Mandela would be released from prison. [3] Many of the world's nations had cut off commercial contact with South Africa [4] because of its policy of apartheid. [5] Anti-apartheid foes warned against lessening pressure against South Africa even as they celebrated the announcement. [6] International support for anti-apartheid victims should continue. [7] A different line was taken by Britain's Margaret Thatcher [8] who said "it no longer makes sense [9] to discourage new investment in South Africa". Mandela was hailed for "his indomitable courage". The Commonwealth Secretary General said [10] that South Africa must guarantee Mandela's safety until change there becomes irreversible.
