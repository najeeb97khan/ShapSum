[1] Hugo, a category 4 hurricane, struck South Carolina Thursday and officials hoped precautions were sufficient. [2] As Hugo approached, roads and resort towns from Hilton Head to Myrtle Beach were nearly empty as coastal residents fled inland [3] and crowded into shelters. [4] Hurricane-force winds first hit land at 9:15pm. [5] Three hours later the 10-mile eye of the storm passed just east of Charleston, with top winds of 135 mph. [6] 119-mph winds in Charleston brought down power lines, [7] snapped trees, [8] exploded utility transformers, [9] and littered the street with tin roofs. [10] Schools and businesses were closed. [11] Hugo was expected to turn north, [12] cause severe flooding, [13] and bring tornadoes.
