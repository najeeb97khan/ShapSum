[1] Flash floods [2] raged through small towns along the Ohio River in eastern Ohio on Thursday. [3] Death toll from the flood was put at ten initially, [4] but was raised to 21 as more bodies were found. [5] The number may still go higher since little hope [6] remains for the fourteen still unaccounted for. [7] The floods hit two creeks near Shadyside, Ohio, about 10 miles south of Wheeling, West Virginia. [8] Five and a half inches of rain fell in slightly more than three hours. [9] The flood created a wall of water 15 to 20 feet high as it tore through the area. [10] The force of the raging water demolished a crowded tavern in Shadyside. [11] A nine-year-old girl [12] who was swept into the creek and on into the Ohio River miraculously survived a seven mile ordeal by clinging to logs. [13] Ohio Governor Celeste declared a state of emergency [14] after surveying the area. [15] The National Guard was brought in to patrol [16] and help rebuild roads. [17] Dog teams from four states were used in searching for survivors and bodies. [18] Few homeowners had flood insurance [19] to aid in rebuilding lost or damaged homes. [20] The Federal Emergency Management Agency planned to accept low-interest loan [21] and grant applications.
