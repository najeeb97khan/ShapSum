[1] In what they said was a response to Prime Minister Thatcher's "declaration of war" in a speech to the Ulster Defense Force, [2] the Irish Republican Army claimed responsibility for an explosion [3] which leveled a three-story barracks in Deal, killing 10 and injuring 22. [4] The barracks, which belonged to the Royal Marines Music School, [5] was the latest in a series of IRA bombings of military facilities. [6] Security forces believe at least two IRA "active service units" are operating in Britain and Europe. [7] Two members of these groups, Patrick Sheehy, known as the Jackal, and John Conaghty are being sought in connection with earlier attacks.
