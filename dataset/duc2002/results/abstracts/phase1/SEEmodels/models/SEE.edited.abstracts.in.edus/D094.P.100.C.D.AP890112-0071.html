[1] A British Broadcasting documentary claims [2] that U.S. prosecutors pressured Japan's wartime prime minister into changing testimony at his trial to avoid incriminating Emperor Hirohito in war crimes. [3] General MacArthur feared [4] that if Hirohito were indicted, [5] it would touch off a guerrilla war and the documentary claims the decision not to prosecute was Truman's. [6] The documentary challenges Hirohito's image as a peaceful man, [7] saying he must have know about the massacre of Chinese at Nanking [8] and knew more than a month in advance of Japan's plan [9] to attack Pearl Harbor. [10] Hirohito died in 1989 at the age of 87.
