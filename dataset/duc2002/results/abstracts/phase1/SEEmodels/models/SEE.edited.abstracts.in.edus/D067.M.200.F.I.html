[1] Damage from Hurricane Andrew is estimated at $20 billion, with only $8 billion covered by insurance. [2] The last serious hurricane caused $4.2 billion in insured loses in 1989. [3] American insurers most affected by claims from Andrew are the State Farm Group, Allstate, Hartford Insurance, Aetna, and Travellers. [4] General Accident, the leading British insurer, expects their claims [5] to rise as high as $40 million, [6] which means the company may be able to pass losses on to external reinsurers. [7] Andrew hit the Bahamas, [8] killing two, [9] then on August 24 tore through southern Florida, [10] where 15 were killed [11] and power outages and polluted water plagued the residents for several days. [12] The city of Homestead and the nearby air force base were flattened. [13] In Dade County, 250,000 people are without homes. [14] After gathering strength over the Gulf of Mexico, Andrew next smashed into Louisiana, [15] killing two [16] and injuring dozens. [17] It did miss the important oil refineries, [18] but damaged crops and the oyster and alligator businesses. [19] Andrew was downgraded to a tropical storm [20] as it moved inland. [21] While Andrew ravaged the land, [22] the dollar sustained a similar assault. [23] Bond yields rose [24] as the Dow Jones and the dollar fell. [25] Insurance stock also fell, [26] but home construction stock had big gains.
