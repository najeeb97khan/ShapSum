[1] Following tensions caused by Iraqi accusations [2] that Kuwait stole Iraqi oil [3] and exceeded OPEC production quotas to drive down prices, [4] 100,000 Iraqi troops entered Kuwait at dawn, [5] seizing the palace and government buildings. [6] Iraq bombarded Kuwaiti ports and military airports [7] and moved toward oil facilities. [8] 200 Kuwaitis were killed [9] or wounded, including the emir's brother. [10] Leaders fled to Saudi Arabia. [11] Iraq claimed it invaded at the request of revolutionaries [12] who staged a coup [13] and established "the provisional government of free Kuwait", [14] which was dissolving Parliament and would hold future free elections. [15] Kuwait denied this. [16] The al-Sabah family has ruled Kuwait for 250 years. [17] Kuwait requested US military intervention.
