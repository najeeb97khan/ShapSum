[1] Australian novelist Peter Carey was awarded the Booker Prize for fiction Tuesday for his mid- 19th century Australian love story, "Oscar and Lucinda". A panel of five judges, [2] who unanimously announced the award of the $26,250 prize, [3] made their selection from 102 books published in Britain in the last 12 months [4] and which they read in their homes. [5] Carey was the only non-Briton among the exceptionally strong competitors in the final six. [6] Book Trust, an educational charity promoting books, [7] administers the Booker Prize that was first awarded in 1969. [8] It is sponsored by Booker, an international food and farming business.
