[1] The US had its worst Winter Olympic showing in 52 years the 1988 Games. [2] The only gold medal winners were Brian Boitano in figure skating and Bonnie Blair in speed skating. [3] Debi Thomas, the US hope in figure skating had to settle for a bronze [4] after East Germany's Katarina Witt took gold and Canadian Elizabeth Manley the silver. [5] The Seoul Summer Games were a much brighter arena for the US with multiple gold medal winners, [6] but the Soviets were the overall winners. [7] Drug usage disqualified several athletes, most notably Canadian sprinter Ben Johnson. [8] Because the US showing did not equal their domination in the Los Angeles Games four years earlier [9] (boycotted by the Soviets) [10] and because of unimaginative TV coverage, lucrative endorsement contracts did not appear to be forthcoming for the winners. [11] The late Jesse Owens, hero of the 1936 Berlin Games was honored with a Congressional Gold Medal, [12] which was given to his widow by President Bush.
