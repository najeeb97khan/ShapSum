[1] The earthquake, which devastated Iran killed at least 25,000 people [2] and injured thousands more. [3] Most of the victims were asleep [4] when the quake struck [5] and died when their fragile homes collapsed on them. [6] The quake registered a magnitude 7.7 [7] which is considered a major quake [8] and is the largest ever recorded in Iran, [9] although twelve quakes greater than magnitude 7 have occurred there in the past 30 years. [10] Most of the homes were built on a flood plain of loosely deposited soil [11] that shifts in an earthquake [12] and allows buildings to collapse. [13] A US Geological Survey geophysicist compared the quake to Armenian quake two years earlier.
