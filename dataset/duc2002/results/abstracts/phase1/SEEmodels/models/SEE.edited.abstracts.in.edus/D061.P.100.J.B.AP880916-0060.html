[1] Scientists do not know what fuels a budding hurricane [2] as development begins. [3] It is a mysterious convergence of winds, moisture and temperature near the equator. [4] About one out of six of the 50 to 60 low-pressure troughs moving west out of Africa each year develop into a higher level storm. [5] A tropical trough, or wave, becomes a depression [6] when its winds start swirling. [7] When sustained winds reach 39mph, [8] the system becomes a named tropical storm. [9] It reaches hurricane status [10] when sustained winds hit 74mph. [11] Scientists believe [12] that hurricanes' primary role is to transport heat from the lower to the upper atmosphere.
