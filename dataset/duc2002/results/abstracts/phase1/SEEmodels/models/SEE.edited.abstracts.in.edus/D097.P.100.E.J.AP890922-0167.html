[1] National Hurricane Center forecasters used computer models [2] to track Hugo's path into Charleston, [3] giving people time to evacuate the South Carolina coast. [4] A greater problem was predicting the intensity of the storm, [5] which was upgraded to a Category 4 hurricane just hours [6] before it hit Charleston. [7] The increase in wind speed, [8] which was detected by Air Force reconnaissance, [9] surprised hurricane specialists. [10] Satellite information is fed to supercomputers at the National Meteorological Center in Suitland, Md. [11] which send data to the hurricane center's tracking model. [12] MIT meteorology professor Kerry Emmanuel criticizes the system [13] because satellites don't provide sufficient wind and temperature data.
