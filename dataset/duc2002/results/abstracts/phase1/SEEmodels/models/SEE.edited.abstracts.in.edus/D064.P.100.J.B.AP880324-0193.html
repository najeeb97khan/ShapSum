[1] Communism suffered its first Big Mac attack Thursday [2] as McDonald's opened a restaurant in Belgrade, Yugoslavia. [3] This, its first outlet in a communist country, has 350 seats, [4] employs 110 people [5] and is capable of serving 2,500 meals per hour. [6] Police kept an eye on eager customers [7] who waited in line for hours to get in. [8] The McDonald's hamburgers, fries and shakes will compete with a local favorite fast-food dish, the pljeskavica, ground pork and onions on a bun. [9] In a change to Yugoslav eating customs, [10] McDonalds will not serve alcohol [11] or allow smoking, [12] nor allow customers to linger long after their meal is finished.
