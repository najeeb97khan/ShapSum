[1] Last night U.S. cities along the Gulf of Mexico from Alabama to eastern Texas were on storm watch for Hurricane Andrew which early yesterday [2] caused at least eight deaths and heavy property damage in southern Florida. [3] Florida Power estimates that 1.2 million of its customers are without power. [4] There was concern last night that the hurricane might hit New Orleans [5] which is especially vulnerable because of its low-lying location. [6] It could also threaten offshore oil production facilities along the Gulf coast of Louisiana and Texas. [7] A British insurance adjuster estimates [8] that total losses, concentrated among U. S. Insurers, [9] could exceed $15 billion.
