[1] Mount Pinatubo erupted Sunday, the first eruption in over 600 years, [2] shooting out gases, rock and ash. [3] Eruptions continued today, [4] leaving the mountain enshrouded in thick gray clouds [5] that rose to 12,000 feet. [6] It had recently shown signs of activity, [7] and Philippine scientists now say a catastrophe is possible. [8] Rain last night raised the additional fear of mudslides. [9] Clark Air Force Base, one of the largest U.S. installations overseas, is ten miles from the mountain. [10] Most airmen, dependents and civilian employees were evacuated to the U.S. Naval Base at Subic Bay, 50 miles from Clark.
