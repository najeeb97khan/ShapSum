[1] The Chairman of the Simon Wisenthal Center, Rabbi Marvin Hier, [2] wrote to West German Chancellor Kohl and East German Premier Modrow. [3] The Center, [4] based in Los Angeles, [5] was founded by Hier [6] to keep alive the memory of the Nazi persecution of Jews in Germany and Nazi-occupied countries. [7] Hier asked to meet with Kohl [8] when he is in Washington, and with Modrow in Germany next month. [9] He says [10] that the victims of Nazism have great and legitimate fear of German reunification, [11] and listed actions that should be taken to allay that fear.
