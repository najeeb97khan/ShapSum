[1] An earthquake hit northwestern Iran on June 21, 1990 killing at least 50,000 people [2] and leaving 400,000 homeless. [3] The death toll made this the fourth deadliest earthquake worldwide in fifty years. [4] The quake measured 7.7 on the Richter scale [5] and exacted particularly heavy casualties since it occurred when most people were inside asleep and hit a coastal area of loosely deposited soil that shifts, [6] causing the adobe homes in the area to collapse. [7] The Iranian President appealed for international aid from all countries except Israel and South Africa. [8] Countries around the world, both friendly and hostile to Iran's Islamic government, responded quickly. [9] The aid from the United States, including funds and supplies raised by Iranians in California , [10] was channeled through the Red Cross and Red Crescent. [11] This was a rare example of U.S.-Iranian cooperation after the hostage crisis of 1979-80. [12] Countries as diverse as Egypt, China, India, Malaysia and the Soviet Union sent help. [13] Even Salman Rushdie, the Indian-born author of "Satanic Verses," [14] condemned to death by the Iranian ayatollahs, [15] contributed $8,600. [16] All was not sweetness and light, however. [17] In Iran an anti-American newspaper blamed the high death toll on the United States and in Washington Iranian opposition leaders blamed it on the Iranian government.
