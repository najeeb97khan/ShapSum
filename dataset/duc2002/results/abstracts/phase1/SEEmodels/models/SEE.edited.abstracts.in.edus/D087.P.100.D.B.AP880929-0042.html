[1] Florence Griffith Joyner, 28, of Los Angeles, [2] known for her long fingernails and colorful bodysuits, [3] set an Olympic record Sunday in winning the 100-meter sprint. [4] In Thursday's 200-meter race, she dashed to her second gold in 21.34 seconds, [5] shattering the world mark [6] she had set 90 minutes earlier in her semifinal heat. [7] She is scheduled to run the 400-meter relay on Saturday [8] and may compete in the 1600-meter relay that day. [9] Griffith Joyner has a chance [10] to match the 1984 quadruple gold medal performance of U.S. sprinter and long jumper Carl Lewis, and by Fanny Blanker-Koen of the Netherlands in 1948.
