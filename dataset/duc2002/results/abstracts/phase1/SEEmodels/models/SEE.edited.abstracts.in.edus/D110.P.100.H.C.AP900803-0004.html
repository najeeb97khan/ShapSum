[1] Iraqi President Saddam Hussein saw the invasion of Kuwait as an opportunity [2] to solve his financial problems [3] and fulfill his territorial ambitions. [4] Iraq's eight-year war with Iran left Iraq with a 70 billion dollar debt [5] that was a drag on the economy. [6] Saddam was incensed [7] that Kuwait demanded repayment of wartime loans. [8] Hussein also accused Kuwait of stealing 2.4 billion dollars worth of oil from a disputed border area, [9] and costing Iraq oil revenue by ignoring production quotas [10] established by OPEC. [11] By controlling Kuwait, Iraq could also fulfill its longstanding wish to for more gulf coastline.
