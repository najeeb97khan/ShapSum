[1] With 27 cash registers and seating for 900, the world's largest McDonald's opened in Moscow on Wednesday [2] to mixed reviews for the Big Mac and high praise for its strawberry milkshakes, called cocktails by the Russians. [3] Choosing from 25,000 applicants, McDonald's trained 630 workers in Western marketing techniques. [4] Clerks are trained to say "please" and "thank-you" [5] as well as observing new standards of cleanliness. [6] The ingredients used at the restaurant are provided by Soviet farms, [7] but McDonald's provided farmers with special training and disease-resistant seeds for the process. [8] At 3.75 rubles (around five U.S. dollars) for a Big Mac, [9] feeding a family at McDonald's could cost the average Soviet worker two-day's wages.
