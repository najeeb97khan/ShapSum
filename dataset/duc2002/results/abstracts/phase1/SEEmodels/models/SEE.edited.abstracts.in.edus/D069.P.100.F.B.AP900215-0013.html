[1] American historians agree [2] that fears of a resurgent militaristic German State after reunification are baseless. [3] Most believe that a politically and economically collapsing East Germany represents a greater threat to world stability. [4] However, there is not agreement on how a unified Germany will affect its European neighbors and Soviet bloc countries. [5] A dominant German economy could slow or even reverse what has been emerging European economic integration. [6] Furthermore, [7] while most West Europeans have gotten over nationalistic mistrusts, [8] East Europeans living in closed societies have not. [9] One historian urges caution. [10] Reunification could prove explosive [11] if mishandled. [12] West Germans are aware of their neighbors' misgivings.
