[1] The Indian government imposed direct federal rule in Kashmir [2] where their security forces are battling to put down rebellion by Moslem separatists. [3] This action follows the killing of at least 29 Moslem militants by Indian security forces. [4] Meanwhile, in the Pakistani capital Islamabad, Pakistan and India began negotiations [5] to repair deteriorating conditions between the two countries. [6] India claims, and Pakistan denies, [7] that Kashmiri militants cross into Pakistan for arms and training. [8] The announcement of federal rule was made in the belief [9] that the state was too unstable for elected government. [10] Since India cracked down on the militants, [11] 792 people have been killed.
