[1] In early March 1993 the Bombay Stock Exchange [2] (BSE) reacted to a postponement of tax reform. [3] By March 10 the BSE index sunk to a 13-month low at 2,287. [4] On March 12 a coordinated bomb attack against targets in Bombay including the BSE killed at least 255 people [5] and injured 1,200 threatening a resumption of Hindu-Moslem rioting which had occurred earlier. [6] The BSE reopened on March 15, [7] but on March 16 an explosion in Calcutta killed at least 45. [8] Despite the tragedies and threat of war or rioting, the BSE index had climbed up as far as 2,409.23 by March 17.
