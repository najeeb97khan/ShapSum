[1] Russia and Georgia deployed peacekeeping troops to stop ethnic fighting in the disputed region of South Ossetia, [2] which is part of Georgia, in the first peacekeeping operation since the collapse of the Soviet Union. [3] The disintegration of the Soviet Union has prompted waves of bloodletting in ethnic flashpoints from Romania to the Afghan border. [4] Russia and Georgia were cautiously optimistic [5] that Ossetia could be the first region of the former Soviet Union [6] where armed conflict could be resolved with the help of "political measures and actions". The peacekeepers' mission is to form a physical barrier between the two sides, opening the way for future talks.
