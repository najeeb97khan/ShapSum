[1] The dispute between Iraq and Kuwait began [2] when Iraq accused Kuwait of flooding the oil market and stealing $2.4 billion worth of oil. [3] On July 20, Kuwaiti officials responded that Iraq wanted them to forgive war debts. [4] On July 24, the United States announced a "short- notice exercise" in the Persian Gulf and Kuwait reinstated its alert. [5] Saddam Hussein summoned the U.S. ambassador for talks. [6] OPEC ministers then agreed to halt overproduction and the two nations met to negotiate, [7] although 100,000 Iraqi troops, 79,700 more than the Kuwait army, were on the border. [8] Talks collapsed [9] and Iraq overran Kuwait before dawn on August 2.
