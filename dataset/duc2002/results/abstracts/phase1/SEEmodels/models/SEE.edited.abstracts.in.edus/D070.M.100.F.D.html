[1] Erich Honecker, former head of East Germany, [2] died on 29 May 1994 in Santiago Chile. [3] He had been living there since his release from prison in 1993. [4] Honecker and his regime were toppled in October 1989, by pro-democracy demonstrators, leading to the eventual unification [5] of East and West Germany. [6] He, along with several members of his Politburo, were arrested and charges with treason, corruption, and abuse of power. [7] Honecker was also charged with manslaughter, [8] stemming from his orders, [9] which resulted in the deaths of East Germans attempting to flee to the West. [10] Honecker ruled East Germany for 18 years [11] by using extreme repression.
