[1] Soviet physicists at Moscow's prestigious Lebedev Institute voted 1543 to 22 in secret ballot Friday to nominate 1975 Nobel Peace Prize winner Andrei D. Sakharov to parliament, two days [2] after the Academy of Sciences rejected him and other reformers as candidates. [3] Whether 67-year-old Sakharov's name will appear on the March 26 ballot is uncertain. [4] Sakharov outlined his reform plan for freedom of movement and association, a multiparty system, a publicly-controlled KGB, and the convergence of communism and capitalism. [5] Sakharov publicly supports Gorbachev [6] but is concerned with slow, uneven reforms. [7] He acted as unofficial envoy to the Transcaucasus last month, [8] and called for the release of imprisoned Karabakh Committee members.
