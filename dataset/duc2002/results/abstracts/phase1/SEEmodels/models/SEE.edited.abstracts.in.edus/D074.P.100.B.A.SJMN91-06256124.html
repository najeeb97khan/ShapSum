[1] As Senate hearings on Clarence Thomas's nomination to the Supreme Court continue, [2] there appears to be no groundswell of opposition to his confirmation [3] although Democratic members of the Senate Judiciary Committee have criticized the candidate's refusal to answer some questions and his reply [4] when asked his view on abortion: "Whether or not I have a view is irrelevant." [5] The committee's 14 members seem to break down into three groups: [6] 5 Republicans solidly for Thomas; 6 Democrats in opposition; [7] and three swing votes, one Republican and two Democrats. [8] Thomas is expected to continue testifying through Friday and the committee will hear from other witnesses next week.
