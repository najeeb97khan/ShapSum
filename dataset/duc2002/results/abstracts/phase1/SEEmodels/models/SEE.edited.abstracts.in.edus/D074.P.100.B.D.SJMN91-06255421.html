[1] Virginia Thomas, wife of Supreme Court nominee Clarence Thomas, [2] who shares her husband's conservative viewpoint, [3] has her own critics. [4] Some women's rights activists are upset by her lobbying against comparable worth legislation and religious-rights groups are troubled by her anti-cult activities following her involvement with Lifespring, a motivational group. [5] The fact that she is white has drawn criticism from some blacks [6] who see her as evidence [7] that Thomas has rejected his roots. [8] Virginia has represented the conservative viewpoint in jobs as staffer for a Republican congressman, spokesperson at the U.S. Chamber of Commerce and deputy assistant secretary at the Department of Labor.
