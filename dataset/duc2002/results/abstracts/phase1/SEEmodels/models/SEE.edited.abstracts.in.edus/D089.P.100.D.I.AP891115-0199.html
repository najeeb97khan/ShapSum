[1] Storms roared across the middle Mississippi Valley and the Southeast on Wednesday, [2] bringing tornadoes, high winds, and baseball-sized hail. [3] These storms were created as a strong cold front moved eastward from Missouri and Arkansas. [4] Tornados pounded Huntsville, Alabama. [5] Tornados also were identified [6] or suspected in southern Illinois, northern Georgia, South Carolina, and Tennessee. [7] Severe thunderstorms hit in Arkansas, Ohio, and Kentucky. [8] Peru, Indiana reported the heaviest rainfall. [9] Other storms, with locally heavy rain, were scattered along the northern Atlantic Coast, [10] which had been experiencing unseasonably warm weather. [11] Snow continued in the West and upper Midwest.
