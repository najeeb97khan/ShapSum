[1] In March 1778, after the British torched Kingston, then the New York capital, South Carolina Gov. Livingston sent New York Gov. George Clinton 1,000 New York pounds with a letter saying, "This money has been received for the charitable purpose [2] of alleviating the distresses of the now-indigent inhabitants of the town of Kingston." [3] Kingston Historian Edwin Ford remembered the letter [4] when Hurricane Hugo struck the Carolinas last week, and on Wednesday a local Red Cross chapter began collecting donations. [5] New York State is also airlifting relief supplies to the Caribbean and Puerto Rico [6] which were also hard hit by Hugo.
