[1] More than 4,000 workers at the Manifest Lipcowy coalmine in Jastrzebie southwest of Warsaw went on strike today [2] to demand legalization of Solidarity and higher wages. [3] Solidarity was formed around Gdansk eight years before when factory and shipyard workers united to form the only independent trade federation in the Soviet bloc. [4] It was crushed in 1981. [5] Manifest Lipcowy is the second mine in the industrial region of Silesia to experience unrest. [6] Workers at the Rudna copper mine near Wroclaw staged a protest rally Monday [7] to demand improved safety conditions and the return to work of Solidarity activists barred Saturday from working.
