[1] World leaders expressed condolences at the death of Japan's 87-year-old Emperor Hirohito, [2] who surrendered his country at the end of World War II, [3] but went on to preside over its economic rise. [4] Several Asian countries were decidedly cool however, [5] noting the suffering of their people at the hands of the Japanese army during the war. [6] The Korean president referred to Japan and Korea's unhappy past. [7] Taiwan newspapers remarked [8] that Chinese people suffered the most from Japan's invasion. [9] President Reagan referred to his reign [10] as spanning a tumultuous and yet constructive era. [11] The Philippine foreign secretary praised Hirohito as a stabilizing factor in post-war Japan.
