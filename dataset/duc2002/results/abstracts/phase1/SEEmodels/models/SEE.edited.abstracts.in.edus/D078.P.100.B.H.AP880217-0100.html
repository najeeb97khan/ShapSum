[1] The Oscar, created 60 years ago by MGM art director Cedric Gibbons, weighs 8 pounds [2] and stands 13 inches tall. [3] It is made of Britannia metal [4] and plated with copper, nickel, and gold. [5] From 1942-44 it was made of plaster. [6] It is manufactured by Chicago's R.S. Owens Co. 1,816 have been produced so far. [7] The only engraving mistake was "Dick Tracy" instead of "Spencer Tracy" in 1938. [8] The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences reserves the first right to buy an Oscar from anyone. [9] "All About Eve" is the most-nominated film (14), [10] Ben Hur the most awarded (11), and Walt Disney the most- awarded person (32).
