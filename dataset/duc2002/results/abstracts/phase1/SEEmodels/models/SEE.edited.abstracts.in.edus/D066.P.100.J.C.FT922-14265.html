[1] Sam Walton's phenomenal success [2] as a retailer stemmed from his personality, [3] and his willingness to defy accepted retailing wisdom. [4] By 1991 Wal-Mart was the world's largest retailer, as measured by revenues. [5] His success made him very wealthy, [6] but he did not take on the trappings of the opulent. [7] Employee satisfaction was very important to him, [8] and he instituted policies to keep them happy. [9] He put his stores in small rural towns despite warnings that they would fail. [10] They were a great success [11] but often caused the failure of many small businesses.
