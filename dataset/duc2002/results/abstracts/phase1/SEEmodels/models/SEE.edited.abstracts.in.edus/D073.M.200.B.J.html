[1] Since roaring to life about two weeks ago, explosions at Mount Pinatubo, [2] located 10 miles from Clark AFB in the Philippines, [3] have become increasingly severe. [4] The first eruptions shot gas, [5] steam and ash 12,000 feet above the mountain. [6] Today's eruptions spewed matter over 12 miles high, [7] and a possible lava flow was seen for the first time. [8] Clark, evacuated nearly a week ago, [9] is covered in ash, [10] as is Subic Bay naval base, [11] which received the 16,000 Clark evacuees. [12] U.S. officials, [13] while not commenting on whether nuclear weapons are at the abandoned base, [14] denied the possibility of radioactive contamination [15] if molten rock hits the weapons depots. [16] At least four deaths and 24 people injured have been recorded [17] since Pinatubo began erupting after six centuries of dormancy. [18] About 84,000 have been evacuated. [19] Heavy rains today have raised fears of catastrophic mudflows. [20] Pinatubo is in the "Ring of Fire" that circles the Pacific. [21] Mount Unzen in Japan, also in the ring, recently erupted, killing 38. [22] President Aquino toured refugee centers [23] and authorized the release of $1.42 million for relief efforts. [24] According to Foreign Secretary Manglapus, [25] the eruptions should not effect the stalled talks with the U.S. on new Clark and Subic lease agreements.
