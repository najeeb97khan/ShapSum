[1] Charleston and San Francisco are united by suffering two of the century's costliest, back- to-back natural disasters, Hurricane Hugo and the Bay Area earthquake. [2] Both endured disruption of normal life, destroyed buildings, billions in damages, loss of life, and frustrating delays in getting federal assistance. [3] The two disasters were different [4] because Hugo gave Charleston warning. [5] Its damage was everywhere, over quickly, and immediately evident, [6] whereas earthquakes leave hidden damage and have aftershocks. [7] Charleston recovered slowly [8] and disrupted communications left it isolated. [9] San Francisco's earthquake was reported immediately [10] and the city bounced back quickly. [11] Charleston lost no major lifeline like the Bay Bridge.
