[1] Deaths from heart and blood vessel disease dropped 24% over the past decade. [2] But it remains by far the nation's largest killer. [3] Experts attribute the decline to a combination of healthier living habits, including fewer cigarettes, better food [4] and improved medical treatment. [5] Statistics show [6] that more than one in four Americans suffer from some form of cardiovascular disease and half of these will eventually die from it. [7] Since 60% of heart attacks occur [8] before the victim reaches the hospital, [9] a major goal is getting people to go to the hospital at the first sign of a heart attack.
