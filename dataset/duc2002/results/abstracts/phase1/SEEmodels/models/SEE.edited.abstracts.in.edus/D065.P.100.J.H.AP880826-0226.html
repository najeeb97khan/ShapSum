[1] Republican vice-presidential candidate Dan Quayle, a 1969 DePauw University graduate, [2] underwent six months of Indiana National Guard training then was hired as a research assistant by the Indiana Attorney General's office, [3] in an interview arranged by an employee of his grandfather. [4] Quayle helped handle consumer complaints in the spring of 1971, [5] and was chief investigator of the new Consumer Protection Division from July to mid-September, [6] when he became administrative assistant to the governor [7] while attending Indiana University Law School. [8] 1980 campaign descriptions of Quayle as a "Vietnam-era veteran" were dropped after objections by combat veterans. [9] "Active National Guard duty" referred to basic training and 15 days of camp each summer.
