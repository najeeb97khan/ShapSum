[1] Andrei D. Sakharov, 66, won the 1975 Nobel Peace Prize. [2] He was exiled to Gorky for seven years [3] after criticizing the Soviet drive into Afghanistan, returning to Moscow with his wife Yelena Bonner in December 1986. [4] Sakharov belongs to the Academy of Sciences [5] and helped create the Soviet hydrogen bomb. [6] He vocally supports Gorbachev's reform campaign [7] but calls for political prisoner liberation and human rights improvements. [8] Past Soviet reports called the couple traitors, [9] accusing Bonner of marrying Sakharov for his money, [10] and saying they lived in luxury amid scandals. [11] Now press says they live simply. [12] Sakharov always does the dishes. [13] His apartment is a mecca for Soviets seeking his aid.
