[1] Discovery's astronauts sent the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) floating free from the shuttle today [2] and it began orbiting Earth every 97.3 minutes at an altitude of 380 miles. [3] Ground controllers had difficulty unfurling two 39-foot solar panels which provide the power to recharge the telescope's batteries [4] but succeeded on a third try. [5] Earlier this morning the Discovery commander maneuvered the shuttle into its deployment position to put the HST's solar arrays in the proper position to quickly start collecting energy. [6] The HST is 43 feet long, [7] weighs 24,250 pounds [8] and is about the size of a railroad tank car.
