[1] The Bush administration fearing that the nomination of Melissa Wells to be ambassador to Nicaragua might face a prolonged Senate confirmation fight has decided to name Harry Shlaudeman for the post. [2] Ms Wells is ambassador to Mozambique, [3] but Senator Jesse Helms and his colleagues had blocked [4] that appointment for 11 months as an expression of opposition to the Reagan administration's friendship toward Mozambique's Marxist government [5] and might delay action on Wells again. [6] Shlaudeman is considered the premier State Department specialist on Latin America [7] and is expected to win easy Senate confirmation. [8] He has served as ambassador to Argentina, Peru, Brazil, and Venezuela.
