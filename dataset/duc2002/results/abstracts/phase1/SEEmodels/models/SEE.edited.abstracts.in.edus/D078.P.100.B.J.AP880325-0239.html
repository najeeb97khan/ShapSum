[1] In the 60-year history of the Academy Awards, [2] there are numerous examples of Hollywood's highest achievers being overlooked. [3] The latest casualties are directors James L. Brooks, [4] whose movie "Broadcast News" won seven major nominations, including best picture, and Steven Spielberg whose movie "Empire of the Sun" received six nominations. [5] Though Brooks has a Best Director trophy [6] ("Terms of Endearment" (1984)), [7] Spielberg has never been recognized with the coveted award. [8] Last year the Academy appeared to make amends by presenting him with the Irving Thalberg award, an honorary award [9] that was also given to other overlooked giants such as Hitchcock and DeMille.
