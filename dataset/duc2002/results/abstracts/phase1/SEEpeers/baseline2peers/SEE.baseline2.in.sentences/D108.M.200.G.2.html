[1] Hitting Baghdad where it hurts, more countries have joined a U.N.-sanctioned boycott of oil from Iraq and Kuwait. [2] On Tuesday, countries ranging from powerful Japan to neutral Switzerland to tiny Cyprus cut off petroleum imports in an effort to convince the Baghdad government to withdraw its troops that invaded Kuwait on Thursday. [3] Turkey announced it was shut down the twin Iraqi oil pipelines that run through it to the Mediterranean _ carrying half of Baghdad's crude output _ and cutting off trade with Iraq. [4] Baghdad's only other major pipelines exporting crude run through Saudi Arabia, where the flow has been drastically reduced. [5] The U.N. [6] Security Council on Monday voted to impose broad economic sanctions against Iraq for invading small oil-rich Kuwait. [7] The measure forbids loans to Iraq or Kuwait and prohibits any activities, including the transfer of money, that would help the two countries export their goods. [8] Sanctions adopted by the European Economic Community on Saturday froze Iraqi assets and suspended the purchase of oil from the two Arab countries. [9] More than 90 percent of imports from Iraq to the 12-nation EEC are petroleum products. [10] The EEC also suspended arms sales to Iraq.
