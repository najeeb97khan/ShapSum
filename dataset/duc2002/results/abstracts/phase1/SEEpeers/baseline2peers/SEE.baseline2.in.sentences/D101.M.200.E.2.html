[1] IT IS the stuff of a Byzantine Cold War novel, enticing enough to grab the attention of even the most benign conspiracy theorist: Did Mikhail Gorbachev orchestrate the coup that almost overthrew him? [2] The blueprint for imposing a state of emergency was drawn by Gorbachev himself last winter. [3] He pushed through the legislation that included suspension of political activity and of freedom of the press. [4] He hand-picked the heads of the coercive institutions and other key officials who took part in the coup . [5] Gorbachev chose to ignore warnings about the right's impending attempt to seize power. [6] Some warnings came from Gorbachev's liberal advisers, such as Alexander Yakovlev, shortly before the coup. [7] But, incredibly, the clearest signals came from the hard-liners themselves. [8] A leading conservative journalist, Alexander Prokhanov, known as the "nightingale of the General Staff," published a transcript of his conversation with the head of the General Staff Academy, the commander-in-chief of the Soviet Navy and Gorbachev's deputy at the Defense Council, Oleg Baklanov. [9] With remarkable candor, Baklanov called for a military takeover as an appropriate step to avert reforms that would result in a fundamental transformation of the union. [10] Needless to say, Baklanov delivered on that warning and joined the Gang of Eight.
