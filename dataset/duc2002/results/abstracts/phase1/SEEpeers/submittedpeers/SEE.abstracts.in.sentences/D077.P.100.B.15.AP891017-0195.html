[2] The Richter scale is a measure of ground motion as recorded on seismographs. [3] Thus a reading of 7.5 reflects an earthquake 10 times stronger than one of 6.5. [4] An earthquake of 3.5 on the Richter scale can cause slight damage in the local area, 4 moderate damage, 5 considerable damage, 6 severe damage. [5] A 7 reading is a "major" earthquake, capable of widespread heavy damage; 8 is a "great" quake, capable of tremendous damage.
