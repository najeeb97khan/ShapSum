[1] >From behind bars, Nelson Mandela has dominated the fight for black rights in South Africa. [2] Sixty-nine black protesters are killed by police in Sharpeville; a state of emergency is declared and the ANC is outlawed. [3] Attends Pan-African Freedom Movement Conference in Ethiopia; travels to London to meet with leftists. [4] In August he is charged with incitement and leaving the country illegally; gets five-year jail term. [5] With Mandela in jail, police raid ANC offices, seizing documents outlining guerrilla campaign. [6] A rock concert in London is held in his honor. [7] But South Africa bans all local celebrations.
