[1] Here is a brief chronology in the dispute between Iraq and Kuwait over oil production and their common border. July 17: Iraqi President Saddam Hussein accuses Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates of flooding the oil market and driving prices down . He said that the move cost Iraq $14 billion in lost oil revenue . July 24: The U.S. Kuwait goes on state of alert . July 26: OPEC ministers in Geneva agree to halt overproduction in hopes of pushing up the price of crude oil. [2] July 31: Kuwaiti, Iraqi delegations meet in Jidda, Saudi Arabia, to negotiate oil and border dispute .
