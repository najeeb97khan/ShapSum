[1] With the winds of Hurricane Gilbert clocked at 175 miles per hour, U.S. weather officials called Gilbert the most intense hurricane ever recorded in the Western Hemisphere. [2] Gilbert was barreling toward Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula. [3] Gilbert is one of only three Category 5 storms in the hemisphere since weather officials began keeping detailed records. [4] The others were the 1935 Labor Day hurricane and Hurricane Camille in 1969. [5] Category 5 storms have winds greater than 155 mph, barometric pressure of less than 27.17 inches and a storm surge higher than 18 feet.
