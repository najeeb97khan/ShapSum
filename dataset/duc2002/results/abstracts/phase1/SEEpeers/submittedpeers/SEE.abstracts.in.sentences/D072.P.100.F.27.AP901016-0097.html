[1] Leonard Bernstein, whose wide-ranging talents and flamboyant personality helped make him the first American maestro to conquer music's international podium, was buried today alongside his wife. [2] His wife, Felicia Montealegre, died in 1978. [3] The Philharmonic will perform an all-Bernstein program beginning Thursday and continuing through Oct. 23. [4] Famed for his gymnastics at the podium, Bernstein composed symphonies as well as movie scores and Broadway shows such as ``West Side Story,'' ``On the Town'' and ``Candide''. [5] His Emmy-winning ``Young People's Concerts'' on television introduced thousands to classical music.
