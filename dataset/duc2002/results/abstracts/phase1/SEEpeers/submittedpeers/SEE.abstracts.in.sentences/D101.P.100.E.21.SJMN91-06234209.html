[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev was prepared to head back to Moscow today to reclaim power after the hard-line coup against him collapsed and its leaders reportedly fled. [2] Gorbachev was set to board a plane in the Crimea, where he was vacationing at the time of Monday's coup, for the two-hour flight to Moscow, said a deputy to Russian Defense Minister Konstantin Kobets. [3] Earlier reports that the plane had already taken off were incorrect, officials said. [4] The Soviet leader, in a statement for radio and television, said he was fully in command of the situation and that the coup leaders would bear "full responsibility" for their actions.
