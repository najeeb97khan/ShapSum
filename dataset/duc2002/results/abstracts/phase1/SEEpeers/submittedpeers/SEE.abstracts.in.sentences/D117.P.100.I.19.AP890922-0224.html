[1] Six novels have been nominated for the Booker Prize, Britain's most prestigious fiction award, and bookmakers say the favorite is "The Remains of the Day" by Japanese author Kazuo Ishiguro. [2] The award, to be announced 10/26/1989, carries a $31,600 prize funded by the Booker McConnell food company. [3] The other books nominated are: _ "Cat's Eye," by Canadian author Margaret Atwood. [4] _ "The Book of Evidence," by John Banville. [5] _ "Jigsaw," by Sybille Bedford. [6] _ "A Disaffection," Scottish novelist James Kelman's story. [7] _ "Restoration," British author Rose Tremain's story.
