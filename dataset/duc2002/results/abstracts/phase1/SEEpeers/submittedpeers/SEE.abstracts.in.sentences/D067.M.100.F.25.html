[1] HURRICANE AFFECTS FLORIDA, LOUISIANA AND NEW ORLEANS [2] Squads of workers fanned out across storm-battered Louisiana on Aug. 27, 1992 to begin a massive rebuilding effort after Hurricane Andrew had flattened whole districts, killing two people and injuring dozens more, agencies report from Florida and New Orleans. [3] While hurricane Andrew was wreaking havoc across large parts of southern Florida and Louisiana the week of Aug. 29, 1992, a grade two dollar crisis (on an one-to-four scale, four being most severe) was blowing itself out at the end of a tumultuous few days for US and world financial markets.
