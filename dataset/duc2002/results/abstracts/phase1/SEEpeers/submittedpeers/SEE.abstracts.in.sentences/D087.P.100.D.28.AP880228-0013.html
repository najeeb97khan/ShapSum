[1] Debi Thomas' dream of Olympic gold turned into disappointment Saturday as East Germany's Katarina Witt won her second straight Olympic championship and Canadian Elizabeth Manley took home the silver before a crowd of cheering countrymen. [2] While the top two skaters in the world staged a shootout to music from Bizet's ``Carmen,'' Manley was so sensational in the freestyle that she finished first with seven judges. [3] Combined with a fourth in the compulsory figures and a third-place finish in the short program earlier in the week, the performance put Manley in second place. [4] Witt, a three-time world champion from East Germany, became the first repeat singles champion since Dick Button took Olympic gold in 1948 and '52.
