[1] THE LATE Mr Robert Maxwell liked to think of himself as a 'strong ' chess player and there was always a chess set in his suite in his London headquarters. [2] 'There is no evidence to suggest that Maxwell actually was a strong player or that he played in any tournaments,' said Mr Malcolm Pein, international chess master and director of Chess and Bridge, which yesterday took over the business and assets of Maxwell Macmillan Chess and Bridge. [3] And when Pergamon was sold to Elsevier last year Maxwell kept the chess and bridge publishing businesses.
