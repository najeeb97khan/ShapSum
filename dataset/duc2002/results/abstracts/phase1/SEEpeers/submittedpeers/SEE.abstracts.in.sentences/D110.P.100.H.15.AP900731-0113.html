[1] Diplomats reported that Iraq had massed 100,000 troops on Kuwait's border. [2] Gulf-based diplomatic sources said the Iraqi force, triple the number earlier reported, included about 300 Soviet-made tanks, and dwarfed the entire Kuwaiti army of 20,000 troops. [3] The diplomats, who spoke on condition of anonymity, said the troops have been concentrated along the border since last week, when Iraq began threatening Kuwait. [4] The diplomats said the number of troops was at first difficult to determine. [5] Kuwaiti leaders had insisted that Iraq pull back its troops before conciliation talks could began, but later dropped that demand.
