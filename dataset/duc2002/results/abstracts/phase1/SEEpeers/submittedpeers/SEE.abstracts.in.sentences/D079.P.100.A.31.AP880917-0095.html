[1] Hurricane Gilbert weakened to a tropical storm as it blustered northwestward today but it threw tornadoes and sheets of rain at thousands of shuttered evacuees along the Texas-Mexico border. [2] Gilbert, responsible for billions of dollars in damage during a weeklong rampage across the Caribbean and Gulf of Mexico, barreled ashore Friday afternoon in Tamaulipas state, a sparsely populated area of Mexico about 120 miles south of Brownsville. [3] Thousands of people evacuated in areas of northeastern Mexico, including the entire beach area of Matamoros, a city of 280,000 just across the border. [4] ``I wouldn't want to go home and then have my family hurt by a tornado,'' said Praxedis Lindsey, 25, of Brownsville, who was at a shelter with his pregnant wife and son.
