[1] Chinese troops stormed Tiananmen Square 05/28/1989 Sunday, shooting and beating their way through crowds of pro-democracy protesters in clashes that witnesses and doctors said killed at least 32 people. [2] Hundreds were wounded. [3] By 3:30a.m. [4] , soldiers and riot police had surrounded Tiananmen, boxing off several thousand students and supporters concentrated around a monument to revolutionary heroes in the center. [5] Sporadic gunfire could still be heard on the outskirts of the square as troops shot at crowds of thousands. [6] The assault came two weeks after Premier Li Peng declared martial law in Beijing and sent troops to the city.
