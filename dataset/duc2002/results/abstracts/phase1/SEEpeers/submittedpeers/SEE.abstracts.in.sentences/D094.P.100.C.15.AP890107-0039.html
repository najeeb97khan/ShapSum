[1] World leaders from President Reagan to Malaysia's king mourned today's death of Emperor Hirohito, who surrendered a broken nation to the Allies in World War II but presided over its rise to economic pre-eminence. [2] Reaction to Hirohito's death at age 87, however, was decidedly cool in several Asian countries, including South Korea, which endured years of harsh colonial rule by Japan. [3] The main opposition party led by Kim Dae-jung said, "We have complex feelings at the death of Hirohito in view of Japan's 20-year rule over Korea during his reign".
