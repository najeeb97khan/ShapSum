[1] The fates made it a night of Leonard Bernstein. [2] So four nights after Bernstein's death at age 72, Beethoven's ``Violin Concerto'' became Bernstein's ``Serenade for Solo Violin, String Orchestra, Harp and Percussion'' (1954); Shostakovich's ``Symphony No. 4'' became Bernstein's ``Symphony No. 1 (Jeremiah),'' written in 1942. [3] Then came the eclectic ``Serenade,'' which Bernstein's program notes says is supposed to be based on Plato's ``Symposium''. [4] During her chanting of the prophet's ``Lamentations,'' mezzo-soprano Wendy White had to wipe a tear from her eye. [5] After he finishes, the men's chorus bellows: ``Why do the nations rage''?
