[1] COALMINERS from Romania's Jiu Valley, who spearheaded violent anti-reform rampages through Bucharest in 1990 and 1991, are once again out on strike, pressing the government for higher wages. [2] But so far the government, unlike its predecessors, has not caved in. [3] It has instead begun legal action to have the strike, which today entered its second week, declared illegal. [4] In response to the question as to whether he intends to lead his men, who appear to support him whole-heartedly, to Bucharest again, he says: 'With miners, anything is possible'. [5] It remains to be seen whether those involved in the incidents of 1990 and 1991 now feel confident enough to ignore him.
