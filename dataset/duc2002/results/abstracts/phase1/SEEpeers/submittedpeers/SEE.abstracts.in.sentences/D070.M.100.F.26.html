[1] (01/29/1990) Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker, who is expected to be indicted for high treason, was arrested on 01/29/1990 morning upon release from a hospital and taken to prison, the official news agency ADN said. [2] (01/29/1990) Honecker, the aging hard-line Stalinist leader who ruled East Germany for 18 years until his ouster on Oct. 18, had previously been declared too ill to withstand imprisonment. [3] (01/29/1990) Eleven members of Honecker's ousted Politburo already are in prison awaiting trial. [4] (08/25/1990) Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker will not stand trial in East Germany as long as the formerly Communist country exists, a West German newspaper reported.
