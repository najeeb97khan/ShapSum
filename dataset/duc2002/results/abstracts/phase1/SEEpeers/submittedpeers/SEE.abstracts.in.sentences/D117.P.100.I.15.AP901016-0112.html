[1] Critic and novelist A.S. Byatt on Tuesday won the Booker Prize, Britain's most prestigious literary award, for her tale of two young scholars investigating the lives of a pair of imaginary Victorian poets. [2] The five judges deliberated two hours before awarding the $39,000 prize to Antonia Byatt for "Possession," one of six finalists in the 21-year-old competition. [4] The Booker Prize is sponsored by Booker, an international food and agriculture business, and administered by The Book Trust. [5] It is Britain's top literary award.
