[1] STORM AFFECTS CANCUN, JAMAICA AND YUCATAN PENINSULA [2] Hurricane Gilbert, one of the strongest storms ever, slammed into the Yucatan Peninsula on Sept. 13, 1988 and leveled thatched homes, tore off roofs, uprooted trees and cut off the Caribbean resorts of Cancun and Cozumel. [3] Prime Minister Edward Seaga of Jamaica said on Sept. 13, 1988 the storm destroyed an estimated 100,000 of Jamaica's 500,000 homes when it throttled the island on Sept. 11, 1988. [4] Earlier on Sept. 13, 1988 Gilbert was classified as a Category 5 storm, the strongest ad deadliest type of hurricane.
