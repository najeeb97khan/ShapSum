[1] Mourners sobbed and shook their heads in disbelief Sunday, 12/16/1989, as tens of thousands viewed the flower-draped body of Andrei D. Sakharov in a huge outpouring of grief for the man described as his country's conscience. [2] Based on official sources, an estimated 45,000 people filed through. [3] Sakharov died Thursday, 12/14/1989, alone in his study at the age of 68, apparently of a heart attack. [4] So many people wanted to see Sakharov's body lying in state that authorities extended the viewing period by four hours and moved the coffin so two rows of mourners could file past.
