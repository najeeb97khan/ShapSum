[1] Hurricane Hugo struck South Carolina with renewed fury Thursday, 09/21/1989, after thousands of coastal residents in southeastern states grabbed what they could carry and fled inland on jammed highways. [2] In Charleston, the hurricane downed power lines, snapped trees and caused utility transformers to explode. [3] By midnight, 119 mph winds were reported in Charleston. [4] Hugo was expected to turn gradually to the north, causing severe flooding as it moved up the East Coast. [5] The storm posed a 19 percent chance of hitting New York City and a 16 percent chance of hitting Boston before Sunday, 09/23/1989, night.
