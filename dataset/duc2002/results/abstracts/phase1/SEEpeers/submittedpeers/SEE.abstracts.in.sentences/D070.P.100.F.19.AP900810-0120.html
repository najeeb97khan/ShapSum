[1] East Germany authorities on Friday, 08/10/1990, interrogated former Communist leader Erich Honecker about his role in the killings of people who tried to flee the country. [2] Honecker and Erich Mielke, head of East Germany's once-feared secret police agency, were interrogated by federal prosecutors. [3] More than 200 people were shot to death or killed by land mines as they tried to escape from East Germany's Communist rule. [4] The murder charge could put Honecker behind bars for life, but his poor health makes prosecution questionable. [5] He is staying in a Soviet hospital outside Berlin.
