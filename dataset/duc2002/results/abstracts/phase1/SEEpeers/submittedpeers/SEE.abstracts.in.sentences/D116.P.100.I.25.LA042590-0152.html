[1] DEPLOYMENTS AND PANELS [2] The Discovery astronauts triumphantly launched the $1.5-billion Hubble Space Telescope one orbit late on Apr. 25, 1990 after last-ditch commands from Earth freed a stuck solar panel, narrowly averting a daring emergency spacewalk. Astronauts Kathryn Sullivan and Bruce McCandless had put on spacesuits and entered an air lock in order to be ready to walk outside and pull out the array in case mechanical deployment of the solar panels failed . The deployment of the two 39-foot panels allowed sun power to flow into the telescope's batteries, relieving worries that they would use up their eight-hour charge .
