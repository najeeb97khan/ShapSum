[1] ADMINISTRATION IN NICARAGUA [2] President Bush is expected to name a female career diplomat as U.S. ambassador to Nicaragua as that country prepares for a new era under President-elect Violeta Chamorro, according to informed sources. [3] The sources said Thursday it is virtually certain the appointment will go to Melissa Wells, 57, currently ambassador to Mozambique. [4] They said the administration believed a female ambassador could relate better than a man to Mrs. Chamorro, who takes office on Apr. 17, 1990. [5] Wells, the administration passed over several male career diplomats with long experience in that region, said the sources, who asked not to be identified.
