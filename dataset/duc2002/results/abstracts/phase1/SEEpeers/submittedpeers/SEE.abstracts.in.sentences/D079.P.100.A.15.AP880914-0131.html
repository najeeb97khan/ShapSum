[1] Hurricane Gilbert battered the resorts of the Yucatan Peninsula today with 160 mph winds and torrential rains. [2] Robert Sheets, director of the National Hurricane Center in Florida, said early today he expects Gilbert to make landfall on the Texas-Louisiana coast in 48 to 60 hours. [3] The hurricane center said Gilbert was the most intense storm on record in terms of barometric pressure. [4] Gilbert is a Category 5 storm, the strongest and deadliest type of hurricane. [5] Only two Category 5 hurricanes have hit the United States _ the 1935 storm that killed 408 people in Florida and Hurricane Camille that devastated the Mississippi coast in 1969 and killed 256 people.
