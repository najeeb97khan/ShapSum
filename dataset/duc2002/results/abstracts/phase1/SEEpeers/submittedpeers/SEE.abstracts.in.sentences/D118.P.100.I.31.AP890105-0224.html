[1] Retired Sen. John Tower, the defense secretary-designate, underwent surgery Thursday to remove a colon polyp, a hospital spokesman said. [2] ``Senator Tower is resting comfortably and is listed in fair condition,'' hospital spokesman Steve Habgood said Thursday night. [3] Tower, 63, a U.S. senator from Texas for 24 years, was admitted to Baylor University Medical Center on Wednesday after undergoing a colonscopy examination last week that revealed the polyp, Habgood said. [4] After leaving the Senate in 1985, Tower went into business as a defense consultant. [5] He also served as an arms negotiator for President Reagan and he headed the White House's Iran-Contra investigative panel. [6] President-elect Bush designated Tower as his defense secretary on Dec. 16.
