[1] Saudi Arabia has been subdued in its reaction to Iraq's invasion of Kuwait. [2] The only tangible help Saudi Arabia gave the Kuwaiti government in the wake of the invasion was to shelter members of the ruling family, including the emir, Sheik Jaber al-Ahmed al-Sabah, and the crown prince. [3] The Saudis also allowed the Kuwaiti royal family to operate a radio. [4] But Friday, 08/03/1990, the radio went off the air. [5] The Saudis did condemned the invasion as part of a joint statement issued by the Gulf Cooperation Council, in which it is allied with Kuwait, the U.A.E., Qatar, Oman and Bahrain.
