[1] McDonald's hamburgers, fries and golden arches came to China on Monday when the fast-food chain opened its first restaurant in a nation famed for its distinctive cuisine. [2] Hundreds of Chinese waited for hours outside the restaurant in Shenzhen, an economic boom town near Hong Kong, for their first taste of a McDonald's hamburger, fries or shake. [3] A factory worker, who waited several hours before she was able to purchase her hamburger, said she liked it because of its ``sweet taste'' - the result of tomato ketchup, which is not commonly used in traditional Chinese cooking.
