[1] Australian novelist Peter Carey was awarded the coveted Booker Prize for fiction Tuesday night for his love story, ``Oscar and Lucinda''. [2] The judges made their selection from 102 books published in Britain in the past 12 months and which they read in their homes. [3] Carey, who lives in Sydney with his wife and son, said in a brief speech that like the other five finalists he had been asked to attend with a short speech in his pocket in case he won. [4] Carey called the award a ``great honor'' and he thanked the prize sponsors for ``provoking so much passionate discussion about literature _ perhaps there will be more tomorrow''.
