[1] The U.N. [2] Security Council today, 08/06/1990, overwhelmingly approved sweeping trade and military sanctions against Iraq, to punish Baghdad for invading Kuwait. [3] The vote was 13-0, which adopted the sanctions resolution proposed by the United States. [4] The sanctions were intended to force Iraq to withdraw its troops from Kuwait and the resolution bans "the import into their territories of all commodities and products originating in Iraq or Kuwait and exported there from after the date of this resolution". [5] It was the third time in its 45-year existence that the world body had voted sanctions against a member state.
