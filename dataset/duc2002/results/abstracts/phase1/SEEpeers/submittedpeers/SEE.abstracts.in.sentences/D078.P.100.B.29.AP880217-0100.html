[1] The motion picture industry's most coveted award, Oscar, was created 60 years ago and 1,816 of the statuettes have been produced so far. [2] Weighing 8 pounds and standing 13 inches tall, Oscar was created by Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer studios art director Cedric Gibbons, who went on to win 11 of the trophies. [3] From 1942 to 1944, the trophy was made of plaster, but winners were later presented with the real thing. [4] The Academy holds all the rights on the statue and ``reserves the right to buy back an Oscar before someone takes it to a pawn shop,'' said Academy spokesman Bob Werden. [5] The most-nominated film was ``All About Eve'' in 1950.
