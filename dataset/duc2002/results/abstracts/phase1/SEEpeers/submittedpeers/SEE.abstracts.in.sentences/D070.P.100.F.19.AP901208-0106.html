[1] A ranking member of Chancellor Helmut Kohl's party demanded the Soviet Union hand over Erich Honecker, who is wanted by German authorities on manslaughter charges, a newspaper reported on Saturday, 12/08/1990. [2] A Berlin court has issued an arrest warrant for Honecker, 78, on the manslaughter charges. [3] He is accused of issuing shoot-to-kill orders to East German guards at the now-fallen Berlin Wall. [4] Honecker has denied all charges against him, including that he was personally responsible for the estimated 200 people who died trying to escape his repressive government. [5] He has taken refuge at a Soviet military hospital in Beelitz.
