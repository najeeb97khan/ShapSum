[1] To the rest of the world, he was known as Emperor Hirohito, but in Japan his given name was seldom used. [2] In Japanese he was simply called Tenno Heika, meaning His Majesty The Emperor. [3] The foreign habit of using his name rankled some Japanese, who found it impolite, and unnecessary since to them there is only one emperor. [4] Some Japanese complained that after his death it even became inaccurate to call him Hirohito because he has been given the posthumous name Showa.
