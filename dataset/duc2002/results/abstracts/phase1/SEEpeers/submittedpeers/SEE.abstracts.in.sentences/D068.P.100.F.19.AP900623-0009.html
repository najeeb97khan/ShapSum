[1] Checkpoint Charlie, the famed Allied border crossing on the west side of the Berlin Wall, was lifted into the sky by a giant crane Friday, 06/22/1990, placed gently onto a flatbed truck and consigned to history. [2] It was emptied, locked and taken away in an elaborate ceremony attended by six foreign ministers, a brace of generals, a brass brand and hundreds of reporters. [3] The public was not invited to the ceremony on Friedrich Street, the avenue blocked by Checkpoint Charlie and its once - feared East German counterpart nearby. [4] The checkpoint went up with the Berlin Wall in 1961.
