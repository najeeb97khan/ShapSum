[1] Watching the new movie, ``Imagine: John Lennon,'' was very painful for the late Beatle's wife, Yoko Ono. [2] Though it fails to delve too deeply into the darker side of Lennon and the Beatles (drug use, abusive tempers, manager Brian Epstein's death), it does capture the music and the times in which Lennon lived. [3] When Albert Goldman's ``The Lives of John Lennon'' was published in September, depicting the former Beatle as riddled with vices, faults and neuroses, Ono called it ``totally fiction''. [4] Lennon's half sister, Julia Baird, also has written a new book, ``John Lennon, My Brother''.
