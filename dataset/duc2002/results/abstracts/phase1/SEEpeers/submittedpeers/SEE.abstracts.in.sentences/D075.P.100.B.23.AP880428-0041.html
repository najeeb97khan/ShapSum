[1] New treatments that stop a heart attack in its tracks may help victims to get out of the hospital more quickly than ever before, perhaps even three days after their attacks, a study published today concludes. [2] Doctors from the University of Michigan Medical Center studied the effects of sending a few carefully selected heart patients home early. [3] Topol said the early discharges were made possible by two recent medical advances _ tissue plasminogen activator, or TPA, and angioplasty. [4] When given quickly after a heart attack, TPA can dissolve clots in the heart's arteries and minimize damage.
