[1] Overcrowding and safety violations caused an aging ferry to capsize in the Ganges River, killing as many as 400 people, a government report said. [2] The ferry accident was said to be India's worst ever. [3] Strong currents hampered efforts today to pull the two-decker from the rising monsoon-swollen river. [4] A senior government official, discussing the confidential report on condition of anonymity, said authorities believed at least 300 people on the lower deck died when the ferry overturned Saturday morning. [5] About 200 people were on the upper deck, he said. [6] Only about a dozen bodies have been found, and most of the missing were believed trapped inside the 35-year-old ferry.
