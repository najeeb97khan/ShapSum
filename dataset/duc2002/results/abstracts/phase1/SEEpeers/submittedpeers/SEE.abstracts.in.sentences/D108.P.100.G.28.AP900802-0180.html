[1] THE INVASION: Iraq's troops, led by about 350 tanks, crossed the border at dawn Thursday, and seized the Kuwaiti palace and government buildings 40 miles away. [2] Early Friday, the invaders controlled Kuwait city, the capital. [3] THE FORCES: Iraq, a country of more than 17 million, has the Arab world's most battle-trained army, and had massed more than 100,000 soldiers on the Kuwaiti border. [4] Kuwait, an oil-rich city-state, has 1.9 million residents 60 percent foreigners and an army of 20,300 soldiers. [5] CAUSE OF CONFLICT: President Saddam Hussein of Iraq accused Kuwait of stealing oil from its territory and forcing down oil prices through overproduction.
