[1] From behind bars, Nelson Mandela has dominated the fight for black rights in South Africa. [2] The following are some of the key events in his life. Mandela joins the African National Congress at age 26, later becoming president of the group's Youth League. [3] In a short time, the ANC becomes the force behind black protests. [4] Three months later he is taken to a prison farm in Paarl and placed in a house usually reserved for prison employees . His statement makes clear his position has not changed in 28 years . On Feb. 11, the nation's best-known black leader becomes a free man at last .
