[1] From behind bars, Nelson Mandela has dominated the fight for black rights in South Africa. [2] The following are some of the key events in his life. Mandela joins the African National Congress at age 26, later becoming president of the group's Youth League. [3] When the National Party comes to power in 1948, the ANC begins planning a campaign of civil disobedience to fight the party's apartheid policy . President Pieter W. Botha offers to free Mandela if he renounces violence; Mandela refuses until the government takes steps to dismantle apartheid and grants full political rights to blacks .
