[1] President Mikhail S. Gorbachev met Tuesday, 01/30/1990, with East German Premier Hans Modrow and appeared to be more open toward eventual German reunification. [2] Asked by an East German correspondent about his attitude toward reunification, Gorbachev said the question was not unexpected and that ``no one can cast doubt'' on the reasonableness of asking it. [3] Gorbachev's position was similar to that adopted by Communists in East Berlin on Tuesday. [4] For the first time, the Communists acknowledged German unity as a long-term goal but said it must be imbedded in the process of overcoming Europe's overall divisions.
