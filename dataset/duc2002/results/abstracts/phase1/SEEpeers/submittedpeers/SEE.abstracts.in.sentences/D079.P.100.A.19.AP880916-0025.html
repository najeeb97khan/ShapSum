[1] Thousands of coastal residents from Mexico to Louisiana fled to higher ground Thursday 09/15/1988 as fierce Hurricane Gilbert sent the first of its storms against Texas after thrashing the Yucatan Peninsula. [2] The weather service issued a hurricane warning for Mexico's northern coast and the southern half of the 370-mile Texas coast from Brownsville to Port O'Connor. [3] A hurricane watch remained in effect for the remainder of the Texas coast, from Port O'Connor north to Port Arthur near the Louisiana border. [4] The weather service said warnings might be extended northward, depending on the path of the 450-mile wide storm.
