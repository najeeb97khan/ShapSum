[1] STORM AFFECTS HUGO, GUADELOUPE AND ST. CROIX [2] Sept. 12 Storm reaches the 39 mph tropical storm threshold and is named Hugo. [3] The storm brushes past the islands, with minimal damage, moving on a course parallel to the islands. [4] Sept. 14 Winds increase to 115 mph; storm is 650 miles east of Lesser Antilles. [5] Sept. 22 The hurricane, with steadily diminishing winds, continues to move inland and is downgraded to a tropical storm. [6] Storm, designated a Category 4 hurricane, is considered "extremely dangerous". [7] Storm is about 1,000 miles east of the Lesser Antilles island chain.
