[1] President Bush is expected to name a female career diplomat as U.S. ambassador to Nicaragua as that country prepares for a new era under President-elect Violeta Chamorro, according to informed sources. [2] The sources said Thursday it is virtually certain the appointment will go to Melissa Wells, 57, currently ambassador to Mozambique. [3] Vice President Dan Quayle and a large congressional delegation are expected to represent Bush at her inaugural. [4] The administration has asked Congress for $300 million in assistance to Nicaragua, part of which would be used for resettling Contra rebels.
