[1] The engineer of a ferry that sank off this Red Sea port, leaving more than 400 people missing, today denied accusations of widespread negligence on the part of the ship's crew. [2] Mahmoud Moharram, engineer of the Salem Express, acknowledged misconduct by some crew members but said the survival of only about 10 of the 71-member crew indicated negligence was not widespread. Investigators were looking into complaints from some survivors that the crew abandoned the ferry without helping passengers and that some crew members pushed aside passengers to get to lifeboats. [3] Only seven bodies have been recovered since the ferry went down, leaving 462 people missing .
