[1] Australian novelist Peter Carey was awarded the coveted Booker Prize for fiction Tuesday night for his love story, "Oscar and Lucinda". [2] A panel of five judges unanimously announced the award of the $26,250 prize after an 80-minute deliberation during a banquet at London's ancient Guildhall. [3] Carey, who lives in Sydney with his wife and son, said in a brief speech that like the other five finalists he had been asked to attend with a short speech in his pocket in case he won. [4] He was unsuccessful in the prize competition in 1985 when his novel, "Illywhacker," was among the final six.
