[1] Here is a look at key events in the student pro-democracy movement that led to Sunday's assault by soldiers on protesters in Tiananmen Square: April 15 - Former Communist Party chief Hu Yaobang dies. [2] May 27 - Beijing student leaders propose students end their occupation of Tiananmen Square. [3] June 1 - The Beijing Municipal Government warns foreign journalists that under martial law they must receive approval for all press coverage. [4] June 3-4 - Tens of thousands of troops make several attempts to move on the square but are driven back by crowds of hundreds of thousands of people supporting the students.
