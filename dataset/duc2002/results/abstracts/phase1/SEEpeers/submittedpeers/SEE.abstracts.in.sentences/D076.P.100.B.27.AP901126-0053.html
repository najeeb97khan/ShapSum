[1] Margaret Thatcher set the example of what a woman could achieve in British society, but her critics say she did little else to help women along. [2] One woman wrote to The Independent newspaper last week that her 7-year-old daughter wondered what title Mrs. [3] Thatcher's successor would hold, since ``he couldn't be called prime minister, that's a girl's name''. [4] Mrs. Thatcher, who stepped down on Thursday, urged women to get involved in politics, but did little to pull them up the ladder. [5] Though Mrs. Thatcher never made a priority of women's issues, she did introduce some helpful legislation: tax concessions for workplace nurseries; independent taxation for married women; and legislation to compel absent fathers to pay child benefits.
