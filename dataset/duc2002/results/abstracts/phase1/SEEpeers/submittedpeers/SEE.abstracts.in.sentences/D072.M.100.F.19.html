[1] Louis Bernstein was born 08/25/1918, in Lawrence, Mass. [2] The parents preferred the name "Leonard" and called the boy that. [3] His first major composition, a symphony titled "Jeremiah," was introduced in 1942, and his first ballet, "Fancy Free," and related first musical, "On the Town," both debuted in 1944. [4] His best and best-remembered work, "West Side Story," debuted in 1957. [5] Leonard Bernstein, the Renaissance man of music who excelled as pianist, composer, conductor and teacher and was, as well, the flamboyant ringmaster of his own nonstop circus, died Sunday 10/14/1990 in his apartment.
