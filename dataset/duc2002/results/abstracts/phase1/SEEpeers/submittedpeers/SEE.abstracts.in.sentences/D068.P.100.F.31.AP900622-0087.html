[1] The Soviet Union said today that a united Germany can join NATO after a five-year transition period during which all Soviet and U.S. troops would leave the country. [2] Shevardnadze made the proposal as he met with U.S. [3] Secretary of State James A. Baker III and their counterparts from Britain, France and the two German states. [4] It also said that within six months of unification, all Western troops should leave West Berlin, which remains technically under military occupation by the United States, France and Britian. [5] There are about 380,000 Soviet troops in East Germany, while a quarter-million U.S. troops are stationed in West Germany along with tens of thousands of British and French soldiers.
