[1] Here is a brief chronology in the dispute between Iraq and Kuwait over oil production and their common border. [2] July 17: Iraqi President Saddam Hussein accuses Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates of flooding the oil market and driving prices down. [3] July 18: Iraq accuses Kuwait of stealing $2.4 billion worth of oil from wells along disputed border with Iraq and seeking to wreck Iraq's economy by overproducing oil and pushing down prices. [4] Aug. 1: Unconfirmed reports say Iraq has moved more troops toward the Kuwaiti border. [5] Talks in Jidda collapse. [6] Aug. 2: Iraqi troops invade Kuwait at 2 a.m.
