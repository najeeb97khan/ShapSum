[1] The most intense hurricane on record surged toward Texas today after battering the Yucatan Peninsula with 160 mph winds, leveling slums, pummeling posh resorts and forcing tens of thousands to flee. [2] Hurricane Gilbert, which has left nearly one in four Jamaicans homeless, slackened somewhat as it swirled over land, but the storm was beginning to gain strength over open water as it moved toward the U.S. Gulf Coast with sustained winds of 120 mph. [3] At 3 p.m. today, the center of the storm was near latitude 22.3 north and longitude 92.9 west, or about 380 miles southeast of Brownsville, Texas, the National Weather Service said.
