[1] THE INVASION: Iraq's troops, led by about 350 tanks, crossed the border at dawn Thursday, and seized the Kuwaiti palace and government buildings 40 miles away. [2] Early Friday, the invaders controlled Kuwait city, the capital. [3] The soldiers were also making sweeps through the southern oilfields, according to residents and Lloyds' insurance service in London. [4] Diplomatic sources estimate more than 200 Kuwaitis were killed or wounded, mainly from the Emiri Guard which bore the main brunt of the invasion.
