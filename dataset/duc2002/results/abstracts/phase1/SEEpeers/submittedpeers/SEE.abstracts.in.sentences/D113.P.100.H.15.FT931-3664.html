[1] MR TEG BAHADUR Thapar was serving lunch from his stall outside the Bombay stock exchange on Friday when he was showered with flying glass and broken concrete in one of 13 explosions which rocked the city and left at least 255 people killed and over 1,200 injured. [2] But that was a single bomb aimed at one man - the explosions in Bombay were designed to terrorise the nation's commercial capital. [3] Bombay accounts for 35 per cent of India's exports. [4] The immediate assumption both at the highest level and in the streets of Bombay was that those responsible must have been foreigners or had foreign help.
