[1] SQUADS of workers fanned out across storm-battered Louisiana on 08/27/1992 to begin a massive rebuilding effort after Hurricane Andrew had flattened whole districts, killing two people and injuring dozens more, agencies report from Florida and New Orleans. [2] Louisiana state officials said they had no overall count of storm-related injuries but initial estimates reckoned fewer than 100. [3] Most of the storm's fury was spent against sparsely populated farming communities and swampland in the state, sparing it the widespread destruction caused in Florida, where 15 people died.
