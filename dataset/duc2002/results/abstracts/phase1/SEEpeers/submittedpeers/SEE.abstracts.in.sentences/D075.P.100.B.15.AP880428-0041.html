[1] The report represents the latest in a steady three-decade trend of sending heart attack patients home from the hospital after ever shorter stays. [2] In the 1950s, doctors often recommended that patients stay in bed for one to two months after a heart attack. [3] Now, doctors typically keep heart attack patients hospitalized seven to 10 days. [4] In their paper, the doctors determined that 80 of the 507 heart attack patients studied were healthy enough to be considered for early discharge. [5] The researchers estimated that if 15 percent of all heart attack patients were discharged after three days, the annual saving would be at least $280 million.
