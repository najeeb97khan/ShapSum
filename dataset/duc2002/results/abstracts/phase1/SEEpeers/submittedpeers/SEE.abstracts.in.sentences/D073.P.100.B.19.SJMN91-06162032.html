[1] Thousands of Americans piled into cars and buses with dogs, cats and duffel bags and left Clark Air Base today, 06/10/1991, after scientists warned that the eruption of a nearby volcano could turn catastrophic. [2] Lt. Col. Ron Rand announced the evacuation in a 5 a.m. 06/10/1991 broadcast over the U.S. military's Far East Network. [3] An hour later, the first vehicles began rolling out of Clark for the U.S.-run Subic Bay naval base, where the 14,500 troops, dependents and civilian employees will be housed until the danger passes. [4] Another 1,500 troops remained at Clark to provide security.
