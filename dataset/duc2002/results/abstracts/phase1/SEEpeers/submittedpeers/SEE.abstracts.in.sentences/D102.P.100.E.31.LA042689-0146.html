[1] Lucille Ball, the leggy showgirl, model and B-grade movie queen whose pumpkin-colored hair and genius for comedy made her an icon of television's early years, died early today, a week after undergoing emergency heart surgery. [2] That legacy involved Miss Ball and her late former husband, Cuban band leader Desi Arnaz. [3] Desilu, as their production company was called, made some of the top comedy shows of the 1950s and '60s, including "December Bride," "Our Miss Brooks" and "Make Room for Daddy". [4] But the show, Miss Ball's first series in 12 years, was canceled after less than two months when it drew spectacularly low ratings.
