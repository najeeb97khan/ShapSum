[1] Troops battled mobs of Moslem separatists in the Kashmir city of Srinagar over the weekend, and at least 21 people were killed and 100 wounded before the fighting subsided Sunday night, police said. [2] Fifteen demonstrators were shot and killed Sunday, while six were fatally wounded late Saturday night, according to Mohammad Nomani, inspector general of Srinagar police. [3] Militants defied a curfew and continued trading intermittent shots with government forces on Sunday, officials said. [4] Witnesses, speaking on condition of anonymity, said the death toll was at least 30, but that figure could not be confirmed. [5] The separatists, led by the Jammu-Kashmir Liberation Front, have often attacked government offices and police officials.
