[1] Several female lawmakers were appointed to junior executive posts in various ministries, but the only woman to serve in a Thatcher Cabinet was Baroness Young, leader of the House of Lords from 1981 to 1983. [2] ``The truth is that although Mrs. Thatcher is a woman, she doesn't believe in the advancement of women,'' Clare Short, a Labor Party lawmaker, said last month. [3] Though Mrs. Thatcher never made a priority of women's issues, she did introduce some helpful legislation: tax concessions for workplace nurseries; independent taxation for married women; and legislation to compel absent fathers to pay child benefits.
