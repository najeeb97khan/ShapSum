[1] President Corazon Aquino said Friday that for security reasons she would not permit deposed President Ferdinand E. [2] Marcos to be buried in the Philippines. [3] Marcos was reported to be in "very critical condition" in Honolulu, where he has lived in exile since his ouster in 1986. [4] One radio station broadcast an interview with Marcos' wife, Imelda, at the St. Francis Medical Center in Honolulu, where she was said to be maintaining a vigil at his bedside. [5] "I regret to inform Mrs. [6] Marcos that as much as I agree that Mr. [7] Marcos has his rights, I still have to consider also the rights and the interests of millions of Filipinos," Aquino told reporters at the presidential palace.
