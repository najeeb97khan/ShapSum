[1] President Bush, filling a key overseas post, has decided to nominate a former CIA officer as ambassador to China, sources said Wednesday, 02/01/1989. [2] The choice is James R. Lilley, a career intelligence officer who served as CIA station chief in China when Bush headed the U.S. liaison office there in 1974-75. [3] Lilley, 60, was born in China, the son of an American oilman working there. [4] He has also served on the National Security Council staff, as head of the unofficial U.S. office in Taiwan and as a deputy assistant secretary of state for East Asia.
