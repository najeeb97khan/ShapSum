[1] Margaret Thatcher set the example of what a woman could achieve in British society, but her critics say she did little else to help women along. [2] She believed the battle for women's rights had been largely won and was no believer in feminism. [3] Though Mrs. Thatcher never made a priority of women's issues, she did introduce some helpful legislation: tax concessions for workplace nurseries; independent taxation for married women; and legislation to compel absent fathers to pay child benefits. [4] Jo Hawkins of the 300 Group, which is seeking equal numbers of women in the House of Commons, believes Mrs. [5] Thatcher's extraordinary abilities made it difficult for her to understand the needs of the average woman.
