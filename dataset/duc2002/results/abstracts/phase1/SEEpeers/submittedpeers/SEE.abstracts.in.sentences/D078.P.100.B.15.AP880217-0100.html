[1] The motion picture industry's most coveted award, Oscar, was created 60 years ago and 1,816 of the statuettes have been produced so far. [2] Oscar, manufactured by the R.S. Owens Co., Chicago, is made of Britannia metal, copper plate, nickel plate and gold plate. [3] >From 1942 to 1944, the trophy was made of plaster, but winners were later presented with the real thing. [4] According to the Academy of Motion Pictures Arts and Sciences, the only engraving mistake was in 1938 when the best actor trophy given to Spencer Tracy for "Boy's Town" read: "Best Actor: Dick Tracy". [5] The most-nominated film was "All About Eve" in 1950.
