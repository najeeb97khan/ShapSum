[1] FLOODS IN OHIO, OHIO RIVER, AND WEGEE CREEK [2] A series of floods happened in Ohio, Ohio River, Wegee Creek, and other places between June 15, 1990 and June 18, 1990. [3] Investigators on June 16, 1990 blamed the flash floods on an unusually heavy rainstorm that overwhelmed the designed flow capacities of three bridges over the creeks. [4] The death toll from flash floods that swept through a rural area of eastern Ohio rose to 20 on June 16, 1990 as searchers dug along two creeks and dived into the Ohio River. [5] Heavy rain fell for about 45 minutes, swelling Wegee Creek, one of two creeks that overflowed in on June 13, 1990 night's flash flood. [6] Residents donned shorts and hip-waders on June 14, 1990 to begin putting their lives back together after 5 inches of rain fell in 3 hours late on June 13, 1990, churning floodwaters that left at least 11 people dead and 51 others missing early on June 16, 1990. [7] In Jefferson County, 50 to 60 residents were evacuated in Adena, where water was 6 to 8 feet deep in streets because of creek flooding. [8] Deanna Hulderman, 48, said she and her husband, Clancey, were pulled under by Wegee Creek flood water at a bar but were reunited about a half-hour later.
