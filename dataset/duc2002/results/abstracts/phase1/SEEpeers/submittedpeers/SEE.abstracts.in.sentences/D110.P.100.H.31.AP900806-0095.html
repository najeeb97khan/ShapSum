[1] The U.N. [2] Security Council today overwhelmingly approved sweeping trade and military sanctions against Iraq, including a ban on oil purchases, to punish Baghdad for invading Kuwait. [3] The vote was 13-0, with two abstentions by Cuba and Yemen on the 15-member council, which adopted the sanctions resolution proposed by the United States. [4] It was the third time in its 45-year existence that the world body had voted sanctions against a member state. [5] The sanctions are legally binding for all member nations, although past council resolutions have often been ignored or flouted. [6] The council includes the five permanent members with veto power - the United States, Britain, China, France and the Soviet Union.
