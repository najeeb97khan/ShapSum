[1] The Soviet Union said on 06/22/1990 that a united Germany can join NATO after a five-year transition period during which all Soviet and U.S. troops would leave the country. [2] The United States rejected the proposal, a U.S. official said. [3] The proposal was outlined by Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze during international talks in East Berlin on the strategic future of a united Germany. [4] A U.S. official, speaking on condition of anonymity, said U.S. officials objected to the five-year time limit before Germany could join NATO. [5] The official said the other Western ministers expressed the same reservations.
