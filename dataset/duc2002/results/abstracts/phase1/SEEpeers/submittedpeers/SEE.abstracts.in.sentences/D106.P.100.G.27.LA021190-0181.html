[1] From behind bars, Nelson Mandela has dominated the fight for black rights in South Africa. [2] The following are some of the key events in his life. [3] When the National Party comes to power in 1948, the ANC begins planning a campaign of civil disobedience to fight the party's apartheid policy. [4] A campaign of civil disobedience by the ANC results in Mandela being detained along with 155 others and charged with treason. [5] Mandela, seven others are charged with sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the government. [6] Mandela is allowed a visit with his wife -- the first since 1962. [7] President Pieter W. Botha offers to free Mandela if he renounces violence; Mandela refuses until the government takes steps to dismantle apartheid and grants full political rights to blacks.
