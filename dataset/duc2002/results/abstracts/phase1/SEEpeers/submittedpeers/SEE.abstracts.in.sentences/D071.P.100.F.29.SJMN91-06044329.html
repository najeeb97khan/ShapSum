[1] It's a dog's life for Bill McFadden. [2] He puts in 18-hour days grooming and conditioning his charges. [3] And, like a racehorse jockey, he crisscrosses the country showing the dogs for a variety of owners. McFadden and his wife, Taffe, run their dog handling business in Campbell and do it primarily for the enjoyment and satisfaction it brings. [4] Those who think you can get rich handling dogs are barking up the wrong tree. [5] As a handler, McFadden emphasizes that he doesn't do obedience training. [6] The aim, he said, is to have a "confident and happy dog" entering a competition .
