[1] BFN [Text] Berlin -- Erich Honecker, the former GDR head of state, died at his house in Santiago, Chile on Sunday morning [29 May], according to his lawyer. [2] Lawyer Nicolas Becker, who had represented 81-year-old Honecker before the Berlin court in 1992 and early 1993, told DPA on Sunday afternoon that Honecker had rejected an operation. [3] His colleague, Wolfgang Ziegler, had said earlier that the former most powerful man in the GDR had obviously died from his liver cancer. [4] Becker said he did not know whether Honecker has left a will or where the funeral would be held. [5] Since then, he and his wife had been living in a house in a wealthy Santiago area.
