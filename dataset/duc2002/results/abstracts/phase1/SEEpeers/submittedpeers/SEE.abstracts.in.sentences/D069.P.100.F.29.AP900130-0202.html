[1] President Mikhail S. Gorbachev met Tuesday with East German Premier Hans Modrow and appeared to be more open toward eventual German reunification. [2] Earlier, the Kremlin chief said any such decisions must include the four Allied powers and take into account European opinions. [3] ``The development of events in the world, in East Germany, in the Soviet Union demand deep consideration and an analytical approach to deciding this problem,'' he said in remarks reported by the official news agency Tass. [4] ``Time itself is acting on this process, giving it dynamism,'' Gorbachev said. [5] ``We have to act responsibly and not decide this important question on the streets''.
