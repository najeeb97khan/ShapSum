[1] President Bush is expected to name a female career diplomat as U.S. ambassador to Nicaragua as that country prepares for a new era under President-elect Violeta Chamorro, according to informed sources. [2] The sources said Thursday it is virtually certain the appointment will go to Melissa Wells, 57, currently ambassador to Mozambique. [3] They said the administration believed a female ambassador could relate better than a man to Mrs. Chamorro, who takes office Wednesday. [4] They also said the administration is eager to have more women in key posts. [5] The Reagan administration retaliated by expelling Nicaraguan Ambassador Carlos Tunnermann and seven of his subordinates from the Nicaraguan embassy here.
