[1] The scarred battleship USS Iowa made a somber return Sunday to its home port, where about 3,000 family members and friends of the crew welcomed loved ones who escaped an explosion that killed 47 sailors. [2] Sailors in whites with black arm bands lined the rails as the huge vessel docked at the Norfolk Naval Base's Pier 5 at 5:41 p.m., just one minute later than Navy officials had estimated. [3] Twenty minutes later, families started filing aboard the ship, exchanging tearful embraces with the returning sailors. [4] The explosion Wednesday flashed through the Iowa's No. 2 gun turret, which extends six decks down into the ship.
