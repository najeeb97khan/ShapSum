[1] Here is a chronology of Hurricane Hugo's destructive path across the Caribbean into South Carolina: Sept. 11 Forecasters monitor new tropical wave forming off West Africa, southwest of the Cape Verde Islands. [2] Sept. 12 Storm reaches the 39 mph tropical storm threshold and is named Hugo. [3] Sept. 13 Hugo develops into a hurricane as winds reach 74 mph. [4] Storm is about 1,000 miles east of the Lesser Antilles island chain. [5] Sept. 14 Winds increase to 115 mph; storm is 650 miles east of Lesser Antilles. [6] Sept. 16 Hugo bears down on Guadeloupe and Dominica with winds reaching 140 mph.
