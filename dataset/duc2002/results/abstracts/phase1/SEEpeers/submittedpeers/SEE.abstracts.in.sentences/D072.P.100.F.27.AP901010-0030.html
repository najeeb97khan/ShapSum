[1] Conductor Leonard Bernstein, the ``West Side Story'' composer who mesmerized audiences with his passion and physical flair at the podium, is giving up the baton because of poor health. [2] The 72-year-old maestro suffers from a lung tumor, emphysema and pulmonary infections and has been told by his doctor to stop conducting and performing on the piano, his spokeswoman said Tuesday. [3] Bernstein still hopes to participate in an AIDS benefit at Carnegie Hall on Oct. 28 but canceled all appearances with orchestras in New York, Israel, Vienna and London and with festival orchestras at Tanglewood, Germany and Japan. [4] After Boston Symphony Orchestra conductor Serge Koussevitzky died in 1951, Bernstein became head of the orchestra and conducting departments at Tanglewood.
