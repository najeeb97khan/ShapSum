[1] Security Council today overwhelmingly approved sweeping trade and military sanctions against Iraq, including a ban on oil purchases, to punish Baghdad for invading Kuwait. [2] The vote was 13-0, with two abstentions by Cuba and Yemen on the 15-member council, which adopted the sanctions resolution proposed by the United States. [3] It said Iraq had launched an armed attack against Kuwait and usurped the authority of Kuwait's legitimate government. [4] The sanctions were intended to force Iraq to withdraw its troops from Kuwait and the resolution bans ``the import into their territories of all commodities and products originating in Iraq or Kuwait and exported there from after the date of this resolution''.
