[1] Leonard Bernstein, whose 70th birthday in August was marked with a gala at Tanglewood, returned to Carnegie Hall with the New York Philharmonic 45 years after he made his triumphant conducting debut there with the orchestra. [2] Bernstein was given a silver box engraved with a reproduction of Monday evening's program cover - a caricature of him, with jutting chin, bright eye, flowing hair and baton, and reading ``with love and thanks of the Philharmonic family to Lennie''. [3] Todd Caruso, a member of the American Boychoir whose voice is high, pure and moving, sang the solo part. [4] Violinist Gidon Kremer, a native of the Latvian town of Riga in the Soviet Union, was soloist in ``Serenade for Violin, String Orchestra, Harp and Percussion''.
