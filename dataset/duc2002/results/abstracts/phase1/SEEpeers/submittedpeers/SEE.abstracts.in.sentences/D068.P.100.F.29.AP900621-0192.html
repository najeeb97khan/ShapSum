[1] Checkpoint Charlie, the famed Allied border crossing by the Berlin Wall, was to be hauled away Friday. [2] U.S. Army Sgt. Lorn Walsh late Thursday stood sentry for the last time by the little white shack, rendered unnecessary by the thaw in East-West relations. [3] With huge sections of the Berlin Wall being ripped down daily, U.S. officials decided two weeks ago to remove Checkpoint Charlie. [4] The border crossing was the scene of stirring escapes and heartbreaking captures as East Germans tried flee to the West, breaking through East German control stations just 20 yards away from the Allied checkpoint.
