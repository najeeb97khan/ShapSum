[1] This capital of central China's Hunan province has been hit by floods in the wake of a succession of rainstorms on the upper and middle reaches of the Xiangjiang River. [2] The water level of the river, which flows through Changsha, is still rising sharply, according to local sources. [3] By four o'clock this afternoon the level recorded at the Changsha monitoring station had risen to 38.93 m, some 0.56 m higher than the highest figure ever previously recorded. [4] The flood has caused serious losses to people along both banks of the river, especially in Changsha, according to the sources.
