[1] During secret balloting Friday, 01/20/1989, at the prestigious Lebedev Institute, the physicists supported Sakharov's candidacy by a vote of 1,543 to 22. [2] The outcome was more an expression of respect for Sakharov as a man who stuck by his principles during difficult times. [3] Sakharov, however, used the occasion to outline his hopes for a multiparty system, public control over the KGB security police, and the convergence of communism and capitalism. [4] Although Sakharov has publicly expressed his support for President Mikhail S. Gorbachev, in recent months he seems to have become increasingly concerned about the slow and uneven pace of reform.
