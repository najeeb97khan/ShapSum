[1] Critic and novelist A.S. Byatt on Tuesday won the Booker Prize, Britain's most prestigious literary award, for her tale of two young scholars investigating the lives of a pair of imaginary Victorian poets. [2] Ms. Byatt, who earlier this month won the $43,000 Irish Times-Aer Lingus prize for international fiction for the same work, has published five novels since 1964. [3] A 1978 work, ``The Virgin in the Garden,'' makes allegorical allusions to Shakespeare and other English literary geniuses in a tale of a play performed at a country house. [4] The Booker Prize is sponsored by Booker, an international food and agriculture business, and administered by The Book Trust.
