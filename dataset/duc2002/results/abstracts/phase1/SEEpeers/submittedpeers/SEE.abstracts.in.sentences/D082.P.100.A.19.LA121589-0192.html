[1] World leaders from President Bush to President Mikhail S. Gorbachev today, 12/15/1989, praised Andrei D. Sakharov as a tremendous force for freedom. [2] The 68-year-old scientist and human rights activist died Thursday, 12/14/1989, of an apparent heart attack. [3] The Soviet Congress stood for a minute of silence for their colleague, once considered a national hero for discovering the Soviet hydrogen bomb but later reviled by Kremlin leadership for his uncompromising stands for human freedom. [4] Gorbachev, who freed the physicist from seven years of internal exile, called Sakharov a "man of conviction and sincerity". [5] "It's a great loss," Gorbachev said.
