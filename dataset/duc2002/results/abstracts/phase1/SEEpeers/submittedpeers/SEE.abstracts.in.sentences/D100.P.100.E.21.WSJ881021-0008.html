[1] Say what you want about Albert Goldman, the author of the new biography, "The Lives of John Lennon" (Morrow, $22.95), but you've got to hand it to him: This guy is one ambitious sleazemonger. [2] Most best-selling pop biographers play it safe: They choose a celeb of respectable, not to say pristine, reputation, and then they proceed to blacken it, preferably by recounting vaguely sourced tales of sexual excess, chemical dependency, personal unkindness, raw business dealings, even, in some cases, dyslexia. [3] Albert Goldman's previous victim in the genre was Elvis Presley. [4] But in choosing Mr. Lennon as his next target, Mr. Goldman appears to be trying to debunk the undebunkable.
