[1] Gilbert, an "extremely dangerous hurricane" and one of the strongest storms in history, roared toward Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula Tuesday 09/13/1988 with 175 mph winds after battering the Dominican Republic, Jamaica and the Cayman Islands. [2] The hurricane killed at least five people Sunday 09/11/1988 in the Dominican Republic. [3] Six people died and 60,000 were left homeless in Jamaica on Monday 09/12/1988. [4] The hurricane, traveling westward across the Caribbean Sea, was upgraded Tuesday 09/13/1988 to a Category 5, the strongest and deadliest type of hurricane. [5] The center said Hurricane Gilbert was the most intense storm on record in terms of barometric pressure.
