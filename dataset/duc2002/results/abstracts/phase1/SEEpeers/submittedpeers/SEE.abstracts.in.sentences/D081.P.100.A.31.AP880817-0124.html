[1] Port workers in Szczecin and southwestern coal miners today declared sympathy strikes with miners demanding the legalization of Solidarity, opposition activists said. [2] The strikes mark the eighth anniversary of the birth of the Solidarity, which Communist authorities crushed in a 1981 military crackdown. [3] Miners earn nearly twice the average national salary and receive extra benefits because of the vital role they play in the economy. [4] July Manifesto, a hotbed of labor activism during the strike wave that spawned Solidarity, is one of five coal mines in the region. [5] That mine has also been the scene of unrest in recent months, where workers have demanded improved safety conditions.
