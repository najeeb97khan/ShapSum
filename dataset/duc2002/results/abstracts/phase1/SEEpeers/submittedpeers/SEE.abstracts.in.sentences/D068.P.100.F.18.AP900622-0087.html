[1] The Soviet Union said today that a united Germany can join NATO after a five-year transition period during which all Soviet and U.S. troops would leave the country. [2] The remarks, a copy of which were obtained by The Associated Press, indicates that a united Germany would be free to join the Western NATO alliance after a five-year transitional period. [3] The four nations would reduce their military forces in the country during a three-year period following unification first by 50 percent, then ``bring them down to a level of token contingents, or completely withdraw them from Germany''.
