[1] Margaret Thatcher set the example of what a woman could achieve in British society, but her critics say she did little else to help women along. [2] Several female lawmakers were appointed to junior executive posts in various ministries, but the only woman to serve in a Thatcher Cabinet was Baroness Young, leader of the House of Lords from 1981 to 1983. [3] Though Mrs. Thatcher never made a priority of women 's issues, she did introduce some helpful legislation: tax concessions for workplace nurseries; independent taxation for married women; and legislation to compel absent fathers to pay child benefits.
