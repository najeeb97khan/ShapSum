[1] Ever since the 1906 earthquake devastated San Francisco, many scientists have believed the next ``big one'' would strike the Los Angeles end of the mighty San Andreas Fault. [2] But studies in recent years have reminded the Bay Area that it also faced another seismic disaster, and those studies became a reality Tuesday when a quake measuring 6.9 on the Richter scale rocked the Bay area. [3] A 1985 paper in the Geophysical Research Letters predicted a 6.9-magnitude quake could happen any time on a portion of the San Andreas Fault that stretches 50 miles from Palo Alto southward through the Santa Cruz Mountains and on to San Juan Bautista.
