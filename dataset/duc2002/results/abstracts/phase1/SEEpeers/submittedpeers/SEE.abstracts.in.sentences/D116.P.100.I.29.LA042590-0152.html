[1] The Discovery astronauts triumphantly launched the $1.5-billion Hubble Space Telescope one orbit late today after last-ditch commands from Earth freed a stuck solar panel, narrowly averting a daring emergency spacewalk. The telescope began orbiting Earth every 97.3 minutes at an altitude of 380 miles -- high above the atmosphere, which has limited the view of ground-based telescopes . Mission Control ordered spacewalking astronauts to be ready to go out of the cabin to help. [2] Deployment of the starboard panel, however, was more troublesome. [3] "Houston, we see motion," Commander Loren J. Shriver reported . The orbit was dictated by the need to put the telescope above the Earth's distorting atmosphere .
