[1] Hurricane Gilbert swept toward the Dominican Republic Sunday, 09/11/1988, and the Civil Defense alerted its heavily populated south coast to prepare for high winds, heavy rains and high seas. [2] The storm was approaching from the southeast with sustained winds of 75 mph gusting to 92 mph. [3] The National Hurricane Center in Miami reported its position at 2 a.m. Sunday, 09/11/1988, at latitude 16.1 north, longitude 67.5 west, about 140 miles south of Ponce, Puerto Rico. [4] Strong winds associated with the Gilbert brought coastal flooding, strong southeast winds and up to 12 feet feet to Puerto Rico's south coast.
