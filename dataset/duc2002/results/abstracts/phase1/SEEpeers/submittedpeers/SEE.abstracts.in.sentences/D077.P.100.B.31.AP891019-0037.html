[1] Private groups and officials from Tokyo to London to Charleston, S.C., offered help to the ruptured San Francisco Bay area, but some said relief for the earthquake could drain coffers already strained by previous disasters. [2] The Red Cross sent 290 units of blood from Los Angeles; the San Diego Blood Bank rushed 50 units to San Francisco within an hour of the quake; more than 400 units were sent from Portland, Ore. [3] But some officials said the disaster could strain the coffers of private agencies and those of the Federal Emergency Management Agency, which was criticized for its response to the storm-lashed Carolinas.
