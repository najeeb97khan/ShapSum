[1] FED AND DOLLARS [2] While hurricane Andrew was wreaking havoc across large parts of southern Florida and Louisiana the week of Aug. 29, 1992, a grade two dollar crisis (on an one-to-four scale, four being most severe) was blowing itself out at the end of a tumultuous few days for US and world financial markets. [3] The flimsy walls erected hastily by the Federal Reserve and other big central banks to protect the vulnerable dollar from a tidal wave of selling on foreign exchange markets failed to hold; and as the sellers poured through the breached barricades, the currency dropped to DM 1.40.
