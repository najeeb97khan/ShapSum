[1] Conductor Leonard Bernstein, the ``West Side Story'' composer who mesmerized audiences with his passion and physical flair at the podium, is giving up the baton because of poor health. [2] The 72-year-old maestro suffers from a lung tumor, emphysema and pulmonary infections and has been told by his doctor to stop conducting and performing on the piano, his spokeswoman said Tuesday. [3] She [Margaret Carson] said Bernstein plans to continue work on a new chamber music piece to be performed next spring, a new musical theater work he hopes to finish by summer and several educational, film and recording projects, as well as his memoirs.
