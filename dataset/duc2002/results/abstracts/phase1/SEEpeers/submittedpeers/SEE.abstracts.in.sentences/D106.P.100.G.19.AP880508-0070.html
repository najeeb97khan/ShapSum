[1] A 3-year campaign that has succeeded in getting Northeastern University to award an honorary degree to jailed South African nationalist leader Nelson Mandela has raised objections from some faculty and students. [2] Northeastern announced plans to award the degree honoring Mandela on 05/13/1988. [3] The degree is to be accepted by South African Archbishop Desmond Tutu, who is scheduled to be in Boston for a benefit dinner. [4] Law students last September began a campaign of demonstrations and vigils on the campus. [5] They hung a banner from the second floor of the law library that said: "Honor Nelson Mandela".
