[1] A strong earthquake triggered landslides and destroyed houses in the mountainous India-Nepal border region early Sunday, killing at least 237 people and injuring more than 1,500, officials and news reports said. [2] More than 100 people died when the quake rumbled through the eastern Indian state of Bihar, officials said. [3] ``Most of them (were) buried alive under collapsed houses,'' said Indra Shrestha, a social worker involved in the rescue operation in Dharan Bazar, a township of about 100,000 people about 18 miles from the Indian border. [4] Deaths were also reported in the neighboring towns of Munger, Keoti and Narpatnagar.
