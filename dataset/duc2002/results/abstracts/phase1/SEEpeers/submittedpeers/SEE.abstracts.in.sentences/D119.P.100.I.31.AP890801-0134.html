[1] Ronald I. Spiers, a veteran U.S. diplomat, will be the new undersecretary-general for General Assembly affairs, the top-ranking American post at the world body, U.N. officials said Tuesday. [2] He replaces Joseph Verner Reed, a banker who served in the post from 1987 until leaving last winter to become chief of protocol for the United States. [3] Spiers has been a member of the U.S. delegation to the United Nations; was director of political affairs for the U.S. [4] Arms Control and Disarmament Agency; and was director of NATO Affairs for the State Department. [5] In 1969, he became assistant secretary of State in charge of the department's Bureau of Political-Military Affairs.
