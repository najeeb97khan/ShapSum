[1] TGF beta is normally present in heart cells, but the study published in Science said that it is missing from rat heart cells damaged by a simulated heart attack. [2] In a study at the Jefferson Medical College in Philadelphia, a group of laboratory rats induced to have heart attacks suffered 50 percent less cell damage less cell damage after injections of transforming growth factor beta than did rats that did not receive the TGF beta. [3] `` TGF beta is a growth factor that opposes some of the bad guys following a heart attack, '' said Dr. Allan M. Lefer, a professor at Jefferson. [4] Though TGF beta is produced naturally in the body, Lefer said his research team used a substance produced artificially by Genentech, a California biotechnology firm.
