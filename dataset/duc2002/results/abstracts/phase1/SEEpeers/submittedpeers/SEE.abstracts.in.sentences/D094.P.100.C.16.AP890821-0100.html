[1] Emperor Hirohito left $13.06 million in taxable property, a tax office near the Imperial Palace announced Monday. [2] It was the first time that tax authorities have disclosed the amount of an emperor's taxable estate, an official in the Kojimachi Tax Office said. [3] The empress dowager, as Hirohito's spouse, was exempt from paying inheritance taxes, Kyodo News Service reported. [4] Three sacred treasures _ a mirror, sword and jewels _ which were transferred from emperor to emperor, were tax-exempt, as were about 3,180 items of art owned by Hirohito that were donated to the state, the tax office official said.
