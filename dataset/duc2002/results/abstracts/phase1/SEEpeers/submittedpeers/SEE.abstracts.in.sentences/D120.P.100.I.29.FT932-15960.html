[1] ONE year ago tomorrow, Mr John Major surprised everyone but himself by winning the general election. [2] His honeymoon, after a very personal victory, was short-lived and now he begins the second year of his hard-won term as a deeply unpopular leader who has everything still to prove. [3] While Mr Tony Newton, leader of the Commons, conceded: 'No one pretends that the last year has been easy,' he also claimed that much had been achieved. [4] He still attracts gratitude for the election victory, which was seen - despite subsequent problems for the economy - as the best result for business.
