[1] New treatments that stop a heart attack in its tracks may help victims to get out of the hospital more quickly than ever before, perhaps even three days after their attacks, a study published today concludes. [2] When given quickly after a heart attack, TPA can dissolve clots in the heart's arteries and minimize damage. [3] The report represents the latest in a steady three-decade trend of sending heart attack patients home from the hospital after ever shorter stays. [4] In their paper, the doctors determined that 80 of the 507 heart attack patients studied were healthy enough to be considered for early discharge.
