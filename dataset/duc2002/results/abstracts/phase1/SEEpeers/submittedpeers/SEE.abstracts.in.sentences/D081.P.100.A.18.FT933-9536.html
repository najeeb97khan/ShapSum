[1] COALMINERS from Romania's Jiu Valley, who spearheaded violent anti-reform rampages through Bucharest in 1990 and 1991, are once again out on strike, pressing the government for higher wages. [2] The region's coal supplies two nearby power stations but these plants, which together account for only 4 per cent of the country's electricity capacity, could also be run using lignite and gas. [3] In response to the question as to whether he [Mr Cosma] intends to lead his men, who appear to support him whole-heartedly, to Bucharest again, he says: 'With miners, anything is possible'.
