[1] THE STORY OF LILLEY [2] For the last three years, Lilley served as U.S. ambassador to South Korea. [3] In that job, Lilley was the principal American contact with South Korean leaders during the turbulent period when former President Chun Doo Hwan resigned and the current president, Roh Tae Woo, won a bitterly contested election. Lilley left Seoul in Jan. 1989 and returned to Washington, hoping to be appointed as assistant secretary of state in charge of U.S. policy in East Asia and the Pacific, sources said .
