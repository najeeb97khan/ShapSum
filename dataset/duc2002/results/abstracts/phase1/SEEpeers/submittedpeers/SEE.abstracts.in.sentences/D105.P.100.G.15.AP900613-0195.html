[1] Ethnic violence killed 148 people and wounded 885 this month in the Central Asian republic of Kirghizia, the official news agency Tass said Wednesday. [2] Tass said 748 of the injured had to be hospitalized and said a search was continuing for a police officer who vanished June 7. [3] No new outbreaks were reported in the region near the border city of Osh in western Kirghizia, where clashes arising from a land dispute began on June 2 between ethnic Kirghiz and Uzbeks, both Sunni Moslems. [4] Besides unrest this month in Kirghizia and Uzbekistan, ethnic violence also has been reported in recent years in Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan and Tadzhikistan.
