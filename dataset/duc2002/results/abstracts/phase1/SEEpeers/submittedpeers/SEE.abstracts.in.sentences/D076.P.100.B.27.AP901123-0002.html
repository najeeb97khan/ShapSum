[1] For almost a decade, Margaret Thatcher was regarded at the White House as America's best friend, the trusted ally whose strong views reinforced the conservative tone of administration policies. [2] Among Western allies, she was alone at Ronald Reagan's side in 1986 in supporting the U.S. bombing of Libya. [3] In the Persian Gulf crisis, she boldly joined with George Bush in sending troops to the Middle East. [4] Together they advocated a hard line toward Moscow and championed military readiness - even though she was the first to recognize Mikhail S. Gorbachev as a leader she could do business with. [5] Bush and Mrs. Thatcher disagreed publicly last year over her government's decision to deport Vietnamese and Cambodian ``boat people'' who were fleeing their countries to Hong Kong.
