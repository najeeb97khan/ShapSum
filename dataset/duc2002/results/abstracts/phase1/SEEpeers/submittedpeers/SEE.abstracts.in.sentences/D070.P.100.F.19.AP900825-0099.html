[1] Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker will not stand trial in East Germany as long as the formerly Communist country exists. [2] Honecker could be prosecuted in a united Germany, however, for violation of property laws. [3] Honecker had used $42 million for stocking a private housing estate for leaders of the former Communist government. [4] He is under investigation on allegations of abuse of power, corruption, harboring terrorists and issuing shoot-to-kill orders to prevent East Germans from escaping to West Germany when he served as the country's leader. [5] Honecker, 78, was ousted as East Germany's leader on 10/18/1989.
