[1] LONDON -- Following his takeover of the unprofitable New York Daily News, British publisher Robert Maxwell plans to raise around #250 million ($448 million) through the sale of shares in his Mirror Group Newspapers Ltd. of Britain. [2] In a flotation organized by Samuel Montagu & Co., Mirror is to sell 45% to 49% of its shares to investors in Britain and abroad. [3] Last month, in another money-raising move, Mr. [4] Maxwell's Maxwell Communication Corp. sold its Pergamon Press PLC unit for #440 million. [5] In other changes, Mr. [6] Maxwell's son Ian, who has been appointed deputy chairman of Mirror Group, will resign from his post at Maxwell Communication, where he was joint deputy managing director. [7] About #210 million will be used to pay back bank loans and the rest to pay in full a loan from Robert Maxwell Holdings Ltd.
