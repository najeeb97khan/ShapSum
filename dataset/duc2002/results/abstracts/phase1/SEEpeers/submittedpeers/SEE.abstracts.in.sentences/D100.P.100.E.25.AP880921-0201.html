[1] GOLDMAN AND JOHN LENNON [2] When celebrity biographer Albert Goldman began digging into "The Lives of John Lennon," his first move was to mail a long, reverential request for help to Yoko Ono. [3] Expecting the best, Goldman claims to have uncovered the worst: a volatile, debauched Lennon who evolved into the Howard Hughes of rock 'n' roll, tucked in his Dakota bed for days at a time. [4] Goldman, whose past tell-all biographies dealt with Elvis Presley and Lenny Bruce, remains unmoved by the hue and cry from the music industry, from Ono and from Lennon's Beatle buddy, Paul McCartney.
