[1] The earthquake that struck northern Iran brought reminders of another quake that devastated Soviet Armenia in 1988. [2] Like Thursday(06/21/1990)'s earthquake in Iran, the 12/07/1988 earthquake in Armenia killed about 25,000 people. [3] The Armenian quake was measured at 6.9 on the Richter scale, but Thursday's earthquake shook the earth with a magnitude of 7.7. [4] In the Iranian quake, the casualty count was pushed up by the fact that people were indoors and asleep at the time, just after midnight. [5] The 7.7-magnitude quake was the largest ever recorded in that area, where two major plates of the earth's crust meet.
