[1] The Booker Prize is Britain's literary event of the year, guaranteed to boost sales of the chosen novel after the award announcement 10/16/1990, and dinner in London's ancient Guildhall. [2] The prize to the author is a check for 20,000 pounds (nearly $38,000), sponsored by Booker, an international food and agriculture business. [3] The trust is a charity promoting reading and books and administers the prize. [4] This year's finalists: Beryl Bainbridge "An Awfully Big Adventure," A.S. Byatt "Possession," Penelope Fitzgerald "The Gate of Angels," John McGahern "Amongst Women," Brian Moore "Lies of Silence" and Mordecai Richler "Solomon Gursky Was Here".
