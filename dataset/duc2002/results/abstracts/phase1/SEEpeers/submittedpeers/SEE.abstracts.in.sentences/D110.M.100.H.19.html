[1] Iraq's troops, led by about 350 tanks, crossed the border at dawn Thursday, 08/02/1990, and seized the Kuwaiti palace and government buildings 40 miles away. [2] Early Friday, 08/03/1990, the invaders controlled Kuwait city, the capital. [3] More than 200 Kuwaitis were killed or wounded, mainly from the Emiri Guard which bore the main brunt of the invasion. [4] Kuwait's Sheik Saad al-Abdullah al-Sabah fled to safety in Saudi Arabia. [5] Security Council voted 14-0 to condemn the invasion. [6] President Bush denounced it as ``naked agression''. [7] Oil prices soared in frenzied trading amid fears the invasion would reduce the supply of oil.
