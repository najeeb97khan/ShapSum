[1] Typhoon Yunya, packing 80 mph winds, slammed into the eastern coast of Luzon island, which includes Mount Pinatubo, early in the day. [2] But it quickly weakened to a tropical storm with 60 mph winds by the time it was centered about 70 miles east of the volcano. [3] Still, heavy rains churned up by the typhoon drenched Manila and the provinces near the volcano, raising fears of mudflows that could bury whole towns up to 25 miles away.
