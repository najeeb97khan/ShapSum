[1] The Communist Party on 12/12/1989 admitted that East Germany's socialist system has failed, and expressed support for a type of partnership with West Germany. [2] The statement in the Communist Party newspaper Neues Deutschland was one of the bluntest official assessments to date of the country's increasingly troubled situation. [3] It came a day after shouting matches broke out over German reunification during pro-democracy protests in Leipzig and Dresden. [4] During Monday's, 12/11/1989, demonstrations that drew more than 200,000 protesters, speakers warned that the reunification issue could fracture the popular movement that has forced the ruling Communists into swift retreat.
