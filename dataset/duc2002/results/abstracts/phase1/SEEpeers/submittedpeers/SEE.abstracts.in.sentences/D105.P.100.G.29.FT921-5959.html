[1] TROOPS OF the former Soviet Union were ordered to pull out from the Armenian enclave of Nagorno-Karabakh yesterday after they were fired on during clashes between Azeri and Armenian militants. [2] Marshal Yevgeny Shaposhnikov, the Commonwealth of Independent States' defence minister, announced the decision as fighting worsened in the disputed territory in spite of efforts to secure a truce. [3] The enclave is administered by Azerbaijan but largely populated by Armenians. [4] No dates have been given for the withdrawal. [5] Troops under fire along the Armenian-Azerbaijani border will also be withdrawn. [6] Commanders of the Trans-Caucasus military district, which encompasses Georgia as well as Armenia and Azerbaijan, have been ordered to rebuff attacks on military installations.
