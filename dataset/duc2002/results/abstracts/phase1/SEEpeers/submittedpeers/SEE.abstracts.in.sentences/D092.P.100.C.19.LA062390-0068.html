[1] Setting aside years of animosity, the U.S. government joined with private humanitarian groups Friday, 06/22/1990, to funnel medicine, tents, clothing, food and other relief materials to help earthquake-stricken Iran. [2] State Department officials said the government gave $300,000 worth of supplies to the American Red Cross for shipment to Iran. [3] The quake, which rocked the northern region of Iran early Thursday, left nearly 29,000 dead, by official count, and about 400,000 homeless. [4] Late Thursday, 06/21/1990, the Iranian Red Crescent appealed for help from the Red Cross and Red Crescent Society. [5] Iranian President Hashemi Rafsanjani also appealed for international aid.
