[1] Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker will not stand trial in East Germany as long as the formerly Communist country exists, a West German newspaper reported. [2] The Hamburg-based Bild am Sonntag said Saturday that it would report in its Sunday editions that Honecker could be prosecuted in a united Germany, however, for violation of property laws. [3] Honecker, 78, was ousted as East Germany's leader on Oct. 18, paving the way for the country's first freely elected government in March. [4] He is under investigation on allegations of abuse of power, corruption, harboring terrorists and issuing shoot-to-kill orders to prevent East Germans from escaping to West Germany when he served as the country's leader.
