[1] The United States urged other U.N. members at talks Sunday, 08/05/1990, to impose broad economic and military sanctions on Iraq. [2] Western diplomats said they expected up to 14 positive votes on the 15-member council for sanctions against Iraq. [3] They said they expected no vetoes. [4] U.S. Ambassador Thomas R. Pickering told reporters after closed-door talks Sunday, 08/05/1990, that the council discussed "the absolute necessity of moving ahead toward a broad resolution of sanctions". [5] It calls for an embargo on Iraq's oil exports, an embargo on imports to Iraq, and a halt to all arms and other military supplies for Iraq.
