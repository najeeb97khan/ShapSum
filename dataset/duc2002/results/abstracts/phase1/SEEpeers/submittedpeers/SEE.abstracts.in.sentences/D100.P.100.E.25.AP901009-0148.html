[1] MUSICS AND FANS [2] About 1,000 Soviets, mostly second generation Beatles fans, flocked to the Moscow Electrical Lamp Factory's cultural hall in northeastern part of the city to rejoice in Lennon's music. [3] The celebration was a dramatic turnabout from Soviet authorities' repression of Beatles fans and supression of their music in earlier times. [4] Inside, fans listened to recordings of Lennon's music, attended a concert of about a dozen Soviet rock bands, bought his albums and posters and reflected on his life. [5] Two concerts devoted to the songwriter, guitarist and singer, who was shot to death in 1980, were held in Moscow.
