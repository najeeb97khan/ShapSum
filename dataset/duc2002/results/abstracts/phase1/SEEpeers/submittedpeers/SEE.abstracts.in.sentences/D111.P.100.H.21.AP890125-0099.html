[1] Leopold Gratz, the president of Parliament and one of the most powerful politicians in the Socialist Party, resigned today over a scandal surrounding the mysterious sinking of ship 12 years ago. [2] Gratz, 59, is the second senior Socialist official to step down because of his alleged involvement in the so-called ``Lucona'' scandal, the subject of a widely publicized investigation by a parliamentary committee. [3] The Lucona's cargo was officially described by Proksch as for a uranium processing plant and insured for $16 million. [4] The insurance company has never paid, claiming the cargo was only scrap metal and alleging the vessel was intentionally sunk to collect the insurance money.
