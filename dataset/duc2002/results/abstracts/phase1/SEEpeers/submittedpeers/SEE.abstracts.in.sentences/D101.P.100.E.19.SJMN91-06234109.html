[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev was said to be back in power today, 08/21/1991, and returning to Moscow as the coup against him collapsed and its leaders reportedly fled. [2] Tanks deployed after the takeover were leaving the capital. [3] Outside the Russian parliament, which served as the headquarters for resistance to the coup led by Boris Yeltsin, rejoicing crowds cheered and waved flags as the three-day-old coup ended. [4] The leaders of the national legislature demanded Gorbachev's reinstatement. [5] The full legislature nullified the coup leaders' emergency decrees. [6] The Communist Party, apparently seeking to salvage its own image, denounced the takeover.
