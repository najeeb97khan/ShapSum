[1] Gone from Tiananmen Square are the pro-democracy banners and the tents of China's freedom movement, replaced by tanks, armed guards and the chants of drilling soldiers. [2] Troops moved back their barricades, making it possible for city residents to walk to the edge of the square, which for seven weeks stood at the heart of the movement. [3] The spot once occupied by the Goddess of Democracy, a 33-foot-high replica of the Statue of Liberty, was taken by a tread-wheeled armored personnel carrier. [4] The statue became a symbol of the movement for democratic reforms and an end to official corruption.
