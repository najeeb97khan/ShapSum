[1] Discovery's five astronauts returned Sunday for a second attempt to launch the shuttle with NASA's most valuable and celebrated payload, the $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope. [2] ``We feel very confident that things are going to go well this time,'' said Discovery's commander, Air Force Col. Loren J. Shriver. [3] Discovery will aim for an altitude of 380 miles, the highest a shuttle has ever gone, so the telescope can be placed in its proper orbit far above Earth's distorting atmosphere.
