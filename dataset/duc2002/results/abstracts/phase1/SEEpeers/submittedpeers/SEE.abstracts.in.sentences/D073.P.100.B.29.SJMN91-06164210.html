[1] Mount Pinatubo erupted with six thundering explosions today, shooting a giant plume of ash 15 miles high. [2] Molten rock and hot gas poured from the volcano, and nearby residents fled in panic. [3] Seismologists said searing gas, ash and molten rock raced at great speed down the mountain's western and northern slopes. [4] At a refugee center in Olongapo, 35 miles southwest of the volcano, survivors told of the sky growing dark, then of hearing a tremendous explosion followed by a rain of ash. [5] They said they scampered for safety with their belongings and livestock over roads slippery with ashfall, many wearing cardboard boxes to protect themselves from the ash.
