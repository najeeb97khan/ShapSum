[1] Port workers in Szczecin and southwestern coal miners today 08/17/1988 declared sympathy strikes with miners demanding the legalization of Solidarity. [2] In support of 3,000 colleagues striking for a second day at the July Manifesto mine in Jastrzebie, workers at the nearby Morcinek mine launched their own occupation strike. [3] Workers at the nearby Borynia mine staged a brief sympathy rally but went back to work Tuesday 08/16/1988 afternoon. [4] Five strikes in Poland from 04/25/1988 to 05/10/1988, and brief stoppages or strike threats in 25 more work places, constituted the worst labor unrest in the country since the 1981 martial-law crackdown.
