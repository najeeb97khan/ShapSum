[1] New treatments that stop a heart attack in its tracks may help victims to get out of the hospital more quickly than ever before, perhaps even three days after their attacks, a study published today concludes. [2] Doctors from the University of Michigan Medical Center studied the effects of sending a few carefully selected heart patients home early. [3] When given quickly after a heart attack, TPA can dissolve clots in the heart's arteries and minimize damage. [4] In their paper, the doctors determined that 80 of the 507 heart attack patients studied were healthy enough to be considered for early discharge. [5] In an accompanying editorial, Dr. Gregory D. Curfman, a deputy editor of the journal, cautioned that the study was not large enough to spot complications that might result from reducing hospital stays.
