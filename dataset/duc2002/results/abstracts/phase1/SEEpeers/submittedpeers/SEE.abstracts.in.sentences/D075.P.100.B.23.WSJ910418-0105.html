[1] NEW YORK -- An experiment with more than 1,700 high blood pressure patients showed that a blood and urine test can pinpoint those patients most likely to have a heart attack. [2] The experiment suggests that doctors could test their patients with mild to moderate high blood pressure to determine how intensively they should be treated to prevent heart attacks, researchers said. [3] The experiment indicated that the test can spot those patients with extremely low risk of heart attack as well as those with a high risk.
