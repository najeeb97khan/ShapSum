[1] Four imprisoned African National Congress leaders, convicted in 1964 with Nelson Mandela, were allowed to visit their ANC colleague at his isolated prison house. [2] The four men, all held at Pollsmoor Prison in Cape Town, were taken to nearby Victor Verster Prison on 12/23/1988 and spent about six hours with Mandela. [3] Mandela was moved to the suburban-style home on the prison grounds 12/07/1988 after recovering from tuberculosis at a private clinic in Cape Town. [4] Mandela's wife, Winnie, said after visiting her husband last month that he is being held "practically in solitary confinement" at the house.
