[1] Chinese troops stormed Tiananmen Square 05/28/1989, shooting and beating their way through crowds of pro-democracy protesters in clashes that witnesses and doctors said killed at least 32 people. [2] Hundreds were wounded. [3] The exact number of people killed may never be known. [4] They range from 500 to 12,000 people. [5] Nearly all of the deaths and injuries occurred Saturday night and early Sunday when troops, firing randomly into large crowds, smashed through barricades to reach Tiananmen square and attack demonstrators demanding democratic reforms. [6] Here is a look at key events in the student pro-democracy movement that led to Sunday's assault by soldiers on protesters in Tiananmen Square: On 04/15/1989 former Communist Party chief Hu Yaobang dies. [7] Beijing University students criticize opponents. [8] On 05/13/1989 about 2,000 students begin a hunger strike in Tiananmen Square. [9] On 05/20/1989 martial law is declared in parts of Beijing. [10] On 05/21/1989 students reject an order by Li Peng to leave the Tiananman Square or face military action. [11] On 05/30/1989 students unveil their ``Goddess of Democracy,'' a replica of the Statue of Liberty, on the square. [12] The statue became a symbol of the movement for democratic reforms and an end to official corruption. [13] On 05/28/1989, a tank crushed it.
