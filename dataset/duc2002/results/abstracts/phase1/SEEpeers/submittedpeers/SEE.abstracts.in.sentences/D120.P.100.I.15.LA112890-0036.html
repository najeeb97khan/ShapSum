[1] John Major, the 47-year-old chancellor of the exchequer who rose out of one of London's toughest neighborhoods and the unemployment line, was selected Tuesday as Britain's next prime minister. [2] Labor Party leader Neil Kinnock, who likely will face Major in the next national election, immediately labeled him "more of the same" in the Thatcher mold. [3] The Labor Party had expected to defeat an unpopular Thatcher in the next election. [4] Jack Cunningham, Labor's campaign coordinator, argued that Major's victory was that of the "Thatcher candidate". [5] Cunningham pointed out that 187 members of Major's own party, a majority in the House of Commons, voted against him Tuesday.
