[1] Hurricane Gilbert's growth from a harmless low pressure zone off Africa to a ferocious killer in the Gulf of Mexico was fueled by a combination of heat, moisture and wind that baffles forecasters. [2] ``It's a matter of getting everything together in the right place in the right time,'' Gil Clark of the National Hurricane Center said Thursday. [3] Gilbert came to the attention of center forecasters Sept. 3 as a dry low pressure trough moving west out of Africa. [4] When sustained winds reach 39 mph, the system becomes a named tropical storm. [5] It reaches hurricane status when sustained winds hit 74 mph.
