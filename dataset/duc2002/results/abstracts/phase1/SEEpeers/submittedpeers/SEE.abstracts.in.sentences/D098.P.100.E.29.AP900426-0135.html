[1] Ground controllers at the Goddard Space Flight Center finally made contact today with the two main antennas on the $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope after trying for more than six hours to lock on. [2] Applause erupted in the Hubble control center in Greenbelt, Md., as the glitch that marred Hubble's first full day in space was solved. [3] ``We have confirmation that we have achieved communication with the Tracking and Data Relay Satellite through the high-gain antenna system,'' said NASA's Dave Drachlis. [4] The second failure occurred at 6:44 a.m. when controllers attempted to send a signal to the telescope's two high-gain antennas through the TDRS.
