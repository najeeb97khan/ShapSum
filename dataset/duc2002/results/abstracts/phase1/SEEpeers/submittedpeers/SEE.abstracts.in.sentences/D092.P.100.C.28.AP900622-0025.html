[1] PM-Iran Quake-Structures,0312 Iran Earthquake Caught Victims Asleep in Fragile Homes The earthquake that struck northern Iran brought reminders of another quake that devastated Soviet Armenia in 1988. [2] Like Thursday's earthquake in Iran, the Dec. 7, 1988, earthquake in Armenia killed about 25,000 people. [3] Most died in structures that collapsed when the quake hit. [4] In the Iranian quake, the casualty count was pushed up by the fact that people were indoors and asleep at the time, just after midnight. [5] The quake struck an area of Iran where most of the buildings are built on a flood plain of loosely deposited soil that shifts in an earthquake and allows structures to collapse, Needham said.
