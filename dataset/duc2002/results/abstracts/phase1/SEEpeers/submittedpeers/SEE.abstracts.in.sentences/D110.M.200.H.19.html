[1] Iraq's troops, led by about 350 tanks, crossed the border at dawn Thursday, 08/02/1990, and seized the Kuwaiti palace and government buildings 40 miles away. [2] Early Friday, 08/03/1990, the invaders controlled Kuwait city, the capital. [3] More than 200 Kuwaitis were killed or wounded, mainly from the Emiri Guard which bore the main brunt of the invasion. [4] Kuwait's Sheik Saad al-Abdullah al-Sabah fled to safety in Saudi Arabia. [5] Iraq, a country of more than 17 million, has the Arab world's most battle-trained army, and had massed more than 100,000 soldiers on the Kuwaiti border. [6] Kuwait, an oil-rich city-state, has 1.9 million residents, 60 percent foreigners, and an army of 20,300 soldiers. [7] President Saddam Hussein of Iraq accused Kuwait of stealing oil from its territory and forcing down oil prices through overproduction. [8] Security Council voted 14-0 to condemn the invasion. [9] President Bush denounced it as ``naked agression''. [10] He froze Iraq's assets in the United States and blocked almost all Iraqi imports, including oil. [11] The Soviets also condemned the invasion and stopped arms sales to Iraq. [12] Oil prices soared in frenzied trading amid fears the invasion would reduce the supply of oil. [13] Al Sabah family has ruled the city-state for almost 250 years.
