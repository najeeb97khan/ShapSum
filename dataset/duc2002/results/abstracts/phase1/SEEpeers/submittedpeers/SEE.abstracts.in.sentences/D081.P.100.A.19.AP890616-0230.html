[1] More than 10,000 coal miners in four states were off the job today to express sympathy for miners in Virginia and West Virginia who have waged a bitter strike since April against the Pittston Coal Group. [2] Miners in Pennsylvania and Kentucky today became the third and fourth states to report walkouts at coal companies. [3] The sympathy strikes began Monday in West Virginia and spread to Indiana Thursday 06/15/1989. [4] The Indiana walkout was prompted by Thursday night's telecast of CBS' "48 Hours," which focused on the Pittston strike, in which 1,600 miners have been off the job since 04/1989.
