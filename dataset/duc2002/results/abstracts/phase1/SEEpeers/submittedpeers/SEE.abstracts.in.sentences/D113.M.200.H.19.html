[1] In Bombay on 03/12/1993 more than a dozen bombs exploded in the city, killing about 200 people and injuring more than a thousand. [2] Among the targets were the Bombay Stock Exchange, the landmark Air India building, a shopping complex, and two hotels near the airport. [3] The 29-storey Jijobhoy Towers, home of the Bombay Stock Exchange, was rocked by two powerful bombs Friday 03/12/1993, leaving 60 dead and 200 injured. [4] The devices, mostly car bombs, appeared to be a systematic attack on India's commercial heart. [5] Businessmen estimated the main loss to the city would be the loss of life and injuries and the damage caused to blasted buildings. [6] Mr Shankarrao Chavan, the federal home (interior) minister, told parliament in New Delhi that he suspected 'an international conspiracy'. [7] Moslem extremists might have taken revenge for the riots which hit India after the Ayodhya mosque's destruction and which left 2,000 dead, including 700 in Bombay.
