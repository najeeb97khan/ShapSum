[1] A ranking member of Chancellor Helmut Kohl's party demanded the Soviet Union hand over Erich Honecker, who is wanted by German authorities on manslaughter charges, a newspaper reported on Saturday. [2] Federal lawmaker Johannes Gerster said refusal to turn over the deposed East German leader for trial could ``burden'' Bonn and Moscow's ``outstanding relations,'' the Neue Osnabruecker Zeitung newspaper reported. [3] Honecker has denied all charges against him, including that he was personally responsible for the estimated 200 people who died trying to escape his repressive government. [4] Soviet officials have said Honecker is staying at the hospital for humanitarian reasons, and German police need permission from the Soviets to gain entry.
