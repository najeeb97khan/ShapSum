[1] Hundreds of people gathered to honor the 47 sailors who died on the USS Iowa as victims of a tragedy that ``reaches into little towns all over the country''. [2] Crewmen in the upper chambers of the gun turret were killed by the explosion and fire while sailors working several levels below suffocated when the blast sucked out all the oxygen, according to a report in today's editions of The Washington Post. [3] A wreath of 47 yellow carnations was cast into the river, a Norfolk police honor guard gave a 21-gun salute and a Navy bugler played taps at the 15-minute ceremony.
