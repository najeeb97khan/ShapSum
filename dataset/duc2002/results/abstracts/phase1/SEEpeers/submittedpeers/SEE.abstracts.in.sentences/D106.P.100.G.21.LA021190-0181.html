[1] From behind bars, Nelson Mandela has dominated the fight for black rights in South Africa. [2] The following are some of the key events in his life. Mandela joins the African National Congress at age 26, later becoming president of the group's Youth League. [3] In a short time, the ANC becomes the force behind black protests. [4] When the National Party comes to power in 1948, the ANC begins planning a campaign of civil disobedience to fight the party's apartheid policy . In December, he is arrested and charged under Suppression of Communism Act. [5] He gets a suspended sentence and is barred from attending meetings or leaving Johannesburg .
