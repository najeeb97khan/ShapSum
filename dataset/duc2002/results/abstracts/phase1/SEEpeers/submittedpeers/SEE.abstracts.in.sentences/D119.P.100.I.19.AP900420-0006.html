[1] President Bush is expected to name a female career diplomat as U.S. ambassador to Nicaragua as that country prepares for a new era under President-elect Violeta Chamorro. [2] The sources said Thursday, 04/19/1990, it is virtually certain the appointment will go to Melissa Wells, 57, currently ambassador to Mozambique. [3] They said the administration believed a female ambassador could relate better than a man to Mrs. Chamorro, who takes office Wednesday, 04/18/1990. [4] Only rarely have U.S. ambassadorships in Latin America gone to women. [5] In choosing Ms. [6] Wells, the administration passed over several male career diplomats with long experience in that region.
