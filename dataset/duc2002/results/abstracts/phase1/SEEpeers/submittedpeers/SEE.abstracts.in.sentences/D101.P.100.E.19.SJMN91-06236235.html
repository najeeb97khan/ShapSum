[1] Mikhail Gorbachev and Boris Yeltsin launched a sweeping purge today, 08/23/1991, that dealt a series of stunning blows to the Communist Party power structure. [2] The two leaders reached an agreement that permits each man to assume the other's duties in an emergency. [3] Gorbachev threw out his foreign minister and named a new head of the KGB. [4] The Moscow City Council, meanwhile, ordered all Communist Party buildings in the capital sealed and all telephone service to party and KGB buildings cut. [5] Hundreds of angry residents marched toward Communist Party headquarters in Moscow intent on proving the party was behind the coup.
