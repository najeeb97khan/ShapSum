[1] John Major, endorsed by Margaret Thatcher as the politician closest to her heart, was elected by the Conservative Party Tuesday night to succeed her as prime minister. [2] Major, 47, fell two votes short of winning a majority in voting among the 372 Coservative Party members of Parliament, but his two opponents quickly conceded defeat and the party confirmed Major as the winner. [3] ``It is a very exciting thing to become leader of the Conservative Party, and particularly exciting, I think, to follow one of the most remarkable leaders the Conservative Party has ever had,'' Major said.
