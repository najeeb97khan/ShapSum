[1] ASPIRINS AND HEMORRHAGIC STROKES [2] Heart attacks usually occur when clots form in the arteries nourishing the heart and choke off its blood supply. [3] But aspirin may increase health risks for people who already have clotting problems, or for those who have high blood pressure, which puts them at increased risk for hemorrhagic strokes, the FDA commentary said. [4] Taking aspirin to prevent heart attacks may increase some people's chances of dying of other things, especially hemorrhagic strokes, the agency said in a commentary in on June 3, 1988's Journal of the American Medical Association.
