[1] Here is a brief chronology in the dispute between Iraq and Kuwait over oil production and their common border. [2] July 17 _ Iraqi President Saddam Hussein accuses Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates of flooding the oil market and driving prices down, and that the move cost Iraq $14 billion in lost oil revenue. [3] July 24 _ The U.S. [4] Defense Department announces that U.S. [5] Navy warships and aircraft are holding a ``short-notice exercise'' in the Persian Gulf with the United Arab Emirates, about 600 miles southeast of the Kuwait-Iraqi border. [6] July 31 _ Kuwaiti and Iraqi delegations meet in Jiddah, Saudi Arabia, to negotiate the oil and border dispute.
