[1] FT 10 SEP 92 / Arts: Booker Prize shortlist THE annual Booker ballyhoo got going again yesterday when the judges for this year's Pounds 20,000 prize for British (and Commonwealth) fiction announced the shortlist. [2] The Prize (courtesy of Booker plc, the food and agri-business group) is no longer the richest literary award but it guarantees great prestige, and sales, for the winner, and, say its admirers, helpfully brings The Novel to public attention. [3] Black Dogs by Ian McEwan (Secker and Warburg Pounds 14.99). McEwan's fiction is uniquely disturbing - some would say deliberately offensive.
