[1] The death toll from flash floods that swept through a rural area of eastern Ohio rose to 20 Sunday as searchers dug along two creeks and dived into the Ohio River. [2] Sixteen people remained missing. [3] Workers used shovels and picks to dig along the creek banks, and sifted through debris at places marked with red flags, where search dogs indicated they'd detected human scents, said National Guard Capt. Jim Boling. [4] Divers worked where the two creeks empty into the Ohio. [5] Twenty-three dog teams from four states were searching for victims, said Boling. [6] The Wegee and Pipe creeks overflowed Thursday night during storms that poured 5 inches of rain onto eastern Ohio in 3 hours.
