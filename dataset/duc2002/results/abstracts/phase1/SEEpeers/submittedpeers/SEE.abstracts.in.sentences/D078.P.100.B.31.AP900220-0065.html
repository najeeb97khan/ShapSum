[1] Spike Lee may have been wearing a Georgetown University cap and sweatshirt, but he got a far warmer reception from Syracuse University students than he did from Academy Award voters. [2] In a lecture and question-and-answer session Monday, Lee shrugged off the academy's icy treatment of his latest movie, ``Do the Right Thing,'' and blamed it on generational politics. [3] The 1989 film, widely hailed by critics, received only two Academy Award nominations, and isn't being considered for best picture or best director. [4] Despite the cap and sweatshirt, Syracuse students welcomed him as one of their own. [5] ``He's an African-American, one of the first ones to make it (as a producer and director), and he's addressing the issues and concerns affecting our community,'' Syracuse senior Deron Harris said.
