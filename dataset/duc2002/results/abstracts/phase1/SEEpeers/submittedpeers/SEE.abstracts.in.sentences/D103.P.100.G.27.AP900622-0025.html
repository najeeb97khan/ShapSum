[1] The earthquake that struck northern Iran brought reminders of another quake that devastated Soviet Armenia in 1988. [2] Like Thursday's earthquake in Iran, the Dec. 7, 1988, earthquake in Armenia killed about 25,000 people. [3] Most died in structures that collapsed when the quake hit. [4] ``It's very similar to what we had in Armenia,'' said Russ Needham, a geophysicist with the U.S. [5] Geological Survey. [6] Many of the victims lived in fragile homes built of a ceramic-type brick or adobe that collapses easily.
