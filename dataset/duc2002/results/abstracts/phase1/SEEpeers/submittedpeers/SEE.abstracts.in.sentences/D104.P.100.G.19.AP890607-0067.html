[1] The Chinese government says that 300 soldiers, "thugs," bystanders and students have been killed during the martial law occupation of Beijing. [2] Yuan Mu said on 06/06/1989 that 7,000 people, including 5,000 soldiers, have been injured and 400 other soldiers are missing. [3] They range from 500 to 12,000 people slain. [4] U.S. intelligence reports indicate 3,000 people probably killed. [5] Some officials of China's Red Cross said 2,600 people were killed. [6] One Hong Kong newspaper reported 5,000 deaths and 30,000 injured. [7] An Eastern European diplomat insisted that 12,000 people were slain by soldiers. [8] The exact number of people killed may never be known.
