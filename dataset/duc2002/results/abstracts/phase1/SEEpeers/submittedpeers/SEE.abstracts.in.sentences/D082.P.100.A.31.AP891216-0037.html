[1] In what amounted to a public apology, Mikhail S. Gorbachev and Soviet leaders today eulogized Andrei D. Sakharov as a ``man of honesty and sincerity'' who suffered great injustice at the hands of previous rulers. [2] It was signed by Gorbachev and fellow Politburo members Nikolai I. Ryzhkov, Vadim A. Medvedev, Lev N. Zaikov, Alexander N. Yakovlev and Vitaly I. Vorotnikov. [3] The Soviet leader told reporters at the Congress of People's Deputies that Sakharov's death was a ``great loss''. [4] Several hundred people gathered outside Sakharov's apartment building Friday evening, holding candles and wreathing his photograph in flowers. [5] Memorial services were scheduled Sunday at the city's main youth center, and on Monday at the Lebedev physics institute where Sakharov worked and the Academy of Sciences, of which he was a leading member.
