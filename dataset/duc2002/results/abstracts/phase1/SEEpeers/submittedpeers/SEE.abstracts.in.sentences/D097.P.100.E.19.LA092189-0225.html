[1] Hurricane Hugo advanced faster and with renewed fury today on Georgia and South Carolina as 150,000 coastal residents grabbed what they could carry and fled inland on jammed highways. [2] The leading edge of Hugo, whose winds muscled up to 125 m.p.h. from 105 m.p.h. the day before, was most likely to hit between Savannah, Ga., and Charleston any time after 8 p.m., local time. [3] A hurricane warning was in effect between Fernandina Beach, Fla., and Oregon Inlet, N.C. [4] At 3 p.m., Hugo was 220 miles from Savannah and bearing down at 20 m.p.h. [5] It was expected to turn gradually to the north, according to the National Weather Service.
