[1] A boat carrying hundreds of Somali refugees struck a reef off Kenya and sank, killing more than 130 people. [2] The accident involving a vessel ferrying about 600 people occurred Friday, 03/01/1991, near Malindi, a tourist resort about 400 miles southeast of Nairobi. [3] Police, navy divers, tourists and local residents had recovered about 130 bodies by Saturday afternoon, but 50 victims were believed still trapped in the sunken vessel, according to the newspapers. [4] The boat with the refugees had set sail from the Somali town of Kismayo, 250 miles north of Malindi, on Feb. 26.
