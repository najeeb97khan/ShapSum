[1] The San Francisco 49ers routed the Denver Broncos 55-10 Sunday in the most lopsided Super Bowl victory ever. [2] San Francisco won the National Football League championship game in 1989, 1985 and 1982. [3] San Francisco quarterback Joe Montana made five touchdown passes, three to Jerry Rice, breaking a Super Bowl record for touchdown passes on a day on which he also set a record with 13 straight pass completions. [4] He also set five Super Bowl career records, including his third Super Bowl Most Valuable Player award and San Francisco's point total was the most ever. [5] Montana left the game with nearly 11 minutes to play.
