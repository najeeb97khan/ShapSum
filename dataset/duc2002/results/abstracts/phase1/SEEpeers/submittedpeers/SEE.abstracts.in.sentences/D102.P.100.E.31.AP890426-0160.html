[1] Lucille Ball was mourned Wednesday by the western New York hometown she left for Hollywood more than half a century ago. [2] Friends said some of the characteristics of her most famous role - the zany, excitable star of television's ``I Love Lucy'' - were evident during her childhood. [3] Mrs. Lopus said Miss Ball dreamed of fame, either as a dramatic actress in New York City or as a dancing girl in musicals. [4] Residents had hung posters saying ``Jamestown Loves Lucy'' in storefronts and house windows. [5] After she fell ill last week, residents signed the posters and sent them to her as a get-well greeting.
