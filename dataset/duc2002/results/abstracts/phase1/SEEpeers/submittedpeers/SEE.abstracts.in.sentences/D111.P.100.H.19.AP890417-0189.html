[1] Two sailors were missing Monday evening, 04/17/1989, after a 652-foot freighter en route from Greece apparently sank in rough seas 400 miles southeast of Cape Cod. [2] Twenty-three crew members were rescued. [3] The 23 sailors from the Star of Alexandria were found on a lifeboat and picked up by the merchant ship Ravenscraig. [4] The Star of Alexandria had been en route from Athens, Greece, to New York with a load of cement when it began taking on water in 12-foot seas and winds up to 35 mph. [5] The first mayday call went out at 9:05 a.m, on 4/17/1989.
