[1] WASHINGTON -- U.S. intelligence officials had been sounding alarms in recent days about a possible Iraqi invasion of Kuwait. [2] Emphasizing that the Central Intelligence Agency wasn't surprised by the Iraqi action, an agency spokesman said yesterday, "We provided policy makers with very useful and timely information on these events and we continue to do so". [3] But as the buildup grew, the CIA became more alarmed, partly because Mr. Hussein is so unpredictable, and partly because, unlike many other Arab leaders, he has been known to carry out his threats.
