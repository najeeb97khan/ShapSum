[1] Hurricane Gilbert, one of the strongest storms ever, slammed into the Yucatan Peninsula Wednesday, 09/14/1988, and leveled thatched homes, tore off roofs, uprooted trees and cut off the Caribbean resorts of Cancun and Cozumel. [2] Looters roamed the streets of Cancun, stealing from stores whose windows were blown away. [3] Despite the intensity of the onslaught and the ensuing heavy flooding, officials reported only two minor injuries. [4] The storm killed 19 people in Jamaica and five in the Dominican Republic before moving west to Mexico. [5] Ham radio operators around Cancun said Gilbert knocked down a radio and television communications tower.
