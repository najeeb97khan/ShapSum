[1] Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker, who is expected to be indicted for high treason, was arrested Monday morning, 01/29/1990, upon release from a hospital and taken to prison. [2] The news agency said the 77-year-old Honecker was arrested after being released from East Berlin's Charite hospital, where he had undergone surgery for a malignant kidney tumor. [3] Honecker joins other members of his ousted Politburo already in prison awaiting trial. [4] Earlier this month, East German prosecutors said Honecker and former state security chief Erich Mielke would be charged with treason and corruption charges for misuse of their positions and state funds.
