[1] Mount Pinatubo, the Philippine volcano that erupted Saturday, 06/08/1991, after six centuries of quiet, is likely to explode in the next few days or weeks with force comparable to the Mount St. Helens explosion. [2] Mount Pinatubo erupted with six thundering explosions today, 06/12/1991, shooting a giant plume of ash 15 miles high. [3] Residents of nearby Angeles panicked when a huge, gray-greenish mushroom cloud burst from the volcano and turned day to night. [4] "Stones as big as my head were falling down from the mountains," said Rolly Soria, 18. [5] Today(06/12/1991)'s first eruption began at 8:40 a.m. with a tremendous blast that sent forth a mushroom cloud. [6] It was followed by a second explosion a few minutes later and a third, smaller blast at 11:49 a.m. [7] Two more thundering explosions rocked Mount Pinatubo late today, 06/12/1991. [8] Shortly after midnight, 06/12/1991, a sixth explosion was reported. [9] At least four people have died, 24 have been hurt and four have been listed as missing since Pinatubo began erupting Sunday, 06/09/1991, after lying dormant for six centuries. [10] About 84,000 people, including Americans, have been evacuated. [11] Gray ash also fell in Manila, 60 miles south. [12] Mount Pinatubo could remain active for years.
