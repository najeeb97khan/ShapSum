[1] In a dramatic finish, the San Francisco 49ers football team won the Super Bowl on Sunday with a 20-16 victory over the Cincinnati Bengals. [2] Joe Montana's 10-yard touchdown pass to John Taylor with 34 seconds left provided the winning margin. [3] The victory was achieved through the brilliance of Montana and Jerry Rice, the wide receiver who caught 12 passes for 222 yards, both Super Bowl records. [4] Two key players were injured in the first 14 plays, Cincinnati's All-Pro nose tackle Tim Krumrie and San Francisco's tackle Steve Wallace. [5] The first touchdown didn't come until 44 minutes into the 60-minute game, when Cincinnati's Stanford Jennings took a kickoff 93 yards for a score.
