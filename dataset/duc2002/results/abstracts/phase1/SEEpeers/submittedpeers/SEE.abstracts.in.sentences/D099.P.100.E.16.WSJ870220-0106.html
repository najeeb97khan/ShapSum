[1] Los Angeles Tries to Make Its Marathon Both a Civil Festival and a Profit Center --- By Frederick Rose Staff Reporter of The Wall Street Journal Makers of cars, soda pop, sportswear, copiers, computers -- even a hospital -- have tagged their names to the Los Angeles Marathon. [2] A $50,000 fee enables the company to use the marathon's name in its ads; Ellesse will spend an additional $100,000 dressing race personnel in T-shirts and warm-up suits, showing its clothes at the marathon's public "Expo" of sponsors' wares, and posting a dozen big street banners along the course.
