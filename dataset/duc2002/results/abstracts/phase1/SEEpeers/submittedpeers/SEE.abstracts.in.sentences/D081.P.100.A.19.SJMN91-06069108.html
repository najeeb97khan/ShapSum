[1] A one-day strike by coal miners in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine has grown into a nationwide walkout involving up to 100,000 miners demanding President Mikhail Gorbachev surrender power to the restive republics, a union leader said Friday 05/31/1991. [2] They claim the government has not kept promises it made to raise miners' living standards as settlement of a nationwide coal strike in July 1989. [3] The miners also want political concessions. [4] The strike has spread to the Donetsk coal basin of the Ukraine, the Vorkuta and Inta regions of northern Russia, and the Kuznetsk coal basin of western Siberia.
