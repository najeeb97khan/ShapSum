[1] The Oscar statuette, one of the most recognizable images in the entertainment world, has no copyright protection, a federal judge has ruled. [2] The decision was a setback for the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, which had sued a Chicago-based manufacturer of an employee-incentive trophy similar to the Oscar. [3] Academy President Karl Malden said the ruling ``comes as a shock to me''. [4] The academy said it would appeal. [5] ``We are surprised that the court would base its decision on events which occurred half a century ago, overlooking the meticulous fashion in which the Academy has protected the integrity of its institutional symbol for decades,'' Malden said in a statement.
