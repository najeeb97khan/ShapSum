[1] EARTHQUAKE AFFECTS SAN FRANCISCO, CALIFORNIA AND PASADENA [2] A major earthquake rocked northern California on Oct. 16, 1989 evening, collapsing part of the San Francisco Bay Bridge and shaking Candlestick Park and buildings up to 95 miles away. [3] Every increase of one number means a tenfold increase in magnitude. [4] Thus a reading of 7.5 reflects an earthquake 10 times stronger than one of 6.5. [5] An earthquake of 3.5 on the Richter scale can cause slight damage in the local area, 4 moderate damage, 5 considerable damage, 6 severe damage.
