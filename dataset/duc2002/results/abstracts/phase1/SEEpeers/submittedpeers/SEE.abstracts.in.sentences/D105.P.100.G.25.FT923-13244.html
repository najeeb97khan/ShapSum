[1] MOLDOVA AND SOUTH OSSETIA [2] Russia and Georgia on July 14, 1992 deployed 'blue helmet' troops to stop ethnic fighting in the disputed region of South Ossetia in the first peace-keeping operation since the collapse of the Soviet Union, Reuter reports from Moscow. [3] Reports from the former Soviet republic of Moldova said several people had been killed overnight in fresh ceasefire violations between government forces and separatists. [4] The mission was the first peace-keeping attempt anywhere in the former Soviet Union, whose disintegration in 1990 has prompted waves of bloodletting in ethnic flashpoints from the Romanian to the Afghan borders.
