[1] The parents of a Peace Corps worker learned Thursday that their son, a volunteer in Nepal, was safe following an earthquake last weekend that killed at least 750 people in that country and neighboring India. [2] ``They called this morning, that's the first we had heard about anything since the quake,'' said Arthur Giaquinta, whose 24-year-old son, Peter, lives in a village 10 miles north of the hard-hit Dharan Bazar township. [3] Jim Flanigan, a Peace Corps spokesman in Washington, said Peter Giaquinta was ``fine as far as we know''. [4] In Boston, the brother of Peace Corps volunteer Bettyjean Bouffard, 27, said Thursday she called her relatives to say she was safe.
