[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev was prepared to head back to Moscow today, 08/21/1991, to reclaim power after the hard-line coup against him collapsed and its leaders reportedly fled. [2] Outside the Russian parliament, which served as the headquarters for resistance led by Boris Yeltsin, rejoicing crowds cheered and waved flags. [3] The Soviet leader, in a statement for radio and television, said he was fully in command of the situation and that the coup leaders would bear "full responsibility" for their actions. [4] The leaders of the national legislature demanded Gorbachev's reinstatement. [5] The full legislature nullified the coup leaders' emergency decrees.
