[1] Hurricane Gilbert battered the resorts of the Yucatan Peninsula today with 160 mph winds and torrential rains. [2] Thousands of people fled coastal areas for shelters inland. [3] ``The wind was blowing out windows everywhere'' in Cancun, an official said of the resort where thousands of Americans vacation each year. [4] Residents along the gulf coast of Texas, 560 miles to the north, stockpiled food and supplies and prepared to evacuate. [5] Gilbert, which has killed at least 11 people, moved over the island of Cozumel at 10 a.m. EDT, said Jose Pereira, a spokesman for the governor's office in Merida, capital of Yucatan state.
