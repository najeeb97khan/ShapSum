[1] A ranking member of Chancellor Helmut Kohl's party demanded the Soviet Union hand over Erich Honecker, who is wanted by German authorities on manslaughter charges, a newspaper reported on Saturday. [2] Federal lawmaker Johannes Gerster said refusal to turn over the deposed East German leader for trial could ``burden'' Bonn and Moscow's ``outstanding relations,'' the Neue Osnabruecker Zeitung newspaper reported. [3] A Berlin court has issued an arrest warrant for Honecker, 78, on the manslaughter charges. [4] ``I demand that the Soviet government immediately release Erich Honecker and extradite him to German justice officials,'' the Osnabrueck newspaper quoted Gerster, a member of Kohl's Christian Democrats, as saying.
