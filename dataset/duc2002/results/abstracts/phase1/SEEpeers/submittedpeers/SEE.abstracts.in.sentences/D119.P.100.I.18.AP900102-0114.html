[1] Hinton, currently ambassador to Costa Rica, replaces Ambassador Arthur H. Davis, who was recalled by Bush in protest of what the administration considered the stealing of the Panamanian elections last May by Gen. Manuel Antonio Noriega. [2] He [Hinton] was assistant secretary of state for economic and business affairs from 1979 to 1981, and before that U.S. representative to the 12-nation European Community, headquartered in Brussels, a State Department adviser on economic affairs, and director and economic counselor of the State Department's Agency for economic Development in Chile and in Guatemala.
