[1] Forty years after Sam Walton first hung out his shingle, Walton's 5&10 will reopen to show how a little rural storekeeper built a discount retail empire. [2] Wal-Mart Stores Inc.'s Visitors Center opens Wednesday in the same building on the downtown square where Walton first opened for business on May 9, 1950. [3] The center chronicles the rise of the nation's fastest-growing discount chain, using displays and audio-video presentations. [4] Inside, an electronic map details the company's 1,500-plus store chain today. [5] Aside from the other displays and presentations, the center also has a replica of Sam Walton's original office at the store, including many fixtures he used 40 years ago.
