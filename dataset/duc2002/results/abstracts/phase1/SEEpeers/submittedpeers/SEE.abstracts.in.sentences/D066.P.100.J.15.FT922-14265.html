[1] SAM WALTON was arguably the most important retailer of his generation. [2] By 1991 the chain's sales had risen to nearly Dollars 44bn, making it the world's largest retailer in terms of revenues, and the Walton family probably America's richest. [3] Mr Walton also disproved the conventional wisdom that large discount stores could not operate successfully in the small, rural towns of the US south. [4] It was precisely such locations which became the bedrock of the business, though in the process Wal-Mart put out of business hundreds of small main street stores, prompting bitter resistance in some towns which Mr Walton tended to blunt with generous charitable donations.
