[1] As a young girl in western New York, Lucille Ball hawked hot dogs and popcorn at an amusement park as she dreamed of starring in movies, hometown friends recalled. [2] "She always talked about being a star," said Pauline Lopus, who went to school with Miss Ball and had kept in touch with her. [3] Later, she starred in high school plays and got her start in front of a large audience on the vaudeville stage at the defunct Palace Theater in Jamestown. [4] "Even though she was a big star, she was still just Lucy to us," said Irene Rosseti of Celoron.
