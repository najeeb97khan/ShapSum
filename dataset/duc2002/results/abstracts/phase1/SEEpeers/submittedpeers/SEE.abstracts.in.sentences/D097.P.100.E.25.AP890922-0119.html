[1] THE STORY OF RILEY [2] While Hurricane Hugo's 135 mph wind roared outside, Mayor Joseph P. Riley Jr. watched the fury it vented on his beloved, 300-year-old city. [3] Riley, city officials and a few reporters stayed in Charleston's historic City Hall through on Sept. 20, 1989 night as the area's worst hurricane in three decades pounded the coastal city where the Civil War began. [4] As the eye of the storm passed, Riley took reporters onto the porch of the 188-year-old brick City Hall to look at the damage. [5] During it all, Riley kept in close contact with the National Weather Service.
