[1] Cement blocks were all that remained of homes, fallen branches stood where trees used to be and muddy streams meandered through the outskirts of this Ohio River town after a furious flash flood. [2] People moved rubble and made makeshift repairs to bridges that span McMann Creek, which links homes to Ohio Route 147. [3] At least 170 members of the Ohio National Guard were called in to patrol and to help rebuild flood-swept roads, said Stacie Gilg, a spokeswoman for the Ohio Emergency Management Agency. [4] By Friday night, traffic jammed Ohio Route 7, which skirts the Ohio River and is the main north-south road through town.
