[1] Black Miners In South Africa Set Strike Date --- Gold and Coal Workers Seek Better Pay and Benefits; Firms Build Stockpiles --- A Wall Street Journal News Roundup The National Union of Mineworkers said an estimated 200,000 black gold and coal miners in South Africa will begin a strike Sunday night for higher wages and better working conditions, in what it said would be the largest job action ever to affect the mining industry in that nation. [2] The strike initially would cover more than half of South Africa's gold mines and about one-fifth of its coal mines, said Cyril Ramaphosa, the union's general secretary.
