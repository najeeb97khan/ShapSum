[1] Conductor Leonard Bernstein, the "West Side Story" composer who mesmerized audiences with his passion and physical flair at the podium, is giving up the baton because of poor health. [2] The 72-year-old maestro suffers from a lung tumor, emphysema and pulmonary infections and has been told by his doctor to stop conducting and performing on the piano, his spokeswoman said Tuesday 10/10/1990. [3] "Leonard Bernstein will henceforth devote his professional energies to composing, writing and education," said Margaret Carson. [4] He last conducted 08/19/1990 at the Tanglewood Music Center in western Massachusetts.
