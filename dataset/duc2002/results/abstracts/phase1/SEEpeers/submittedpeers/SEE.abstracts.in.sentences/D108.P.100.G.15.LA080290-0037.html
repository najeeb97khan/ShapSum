[1] Here is a brief chronology in the dispute between Iraq and Kuwait over oil production and their common border. [2] July 20: Kuwaiti officials suggest Iraq's sudden outburst of belligerence is aimed at getting its creditors -- including Kuwait -- to write off billions of dollars in debts from the 1980-88 Iran-Iraq war. [3] July 25: Baghdad says that it will not be intimidated by U.S. pressure in its oil dispute with neighboring Kuwait, and Hussein summons the U.S. ambassador for talks. [4] President Hosni Mubarak of Egypt says Iraq and Kuwait will meet in Saudi Arabia for talks. [5] Diplomats say Iraq has 100,000 troops on Kuwait's border; Kuwait army numbers about 20,000 troops.
