[1] RAFT IN AMELAND [2] A freighter carrying a cargo of beer sank in the North Sea on Sept. 13, 1988 off the Dutch coast and all nine crew members were rescued from life rafts, a coast guard spokesman said. [3] It was not immediately clear what had caused the sinking 29 miles off the Dutch island of Ameland, according to the coast guard spokesman, who was not identified. [4] A Dutch navy aircraft and two life boats rushed to the scene. [5] The ship sank at about 12:45 p.m. in high winds, some 28 nautical miles north of the island of Ameland.
