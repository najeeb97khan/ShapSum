[1] Lyng Says Drought Could Prompt Relaxed Crop Acreage Controls Next Year The drought may force the government to ease crop acreage controls next year to help the United States rebuild its grain inventories, Agriculture Secretary Richard E. Lyng said Monday. [2] Moreover, on July 2, about 35 percent of the contiguous United States was in the ``severe to extreme'' drought category, he said. [3] ``Because of the large early season area now experiencing drought in the U.S., 1988 appears to have the potential to approach or exceed the drought-affected areas of the 1930s and 1950s,'' Lyng said.
