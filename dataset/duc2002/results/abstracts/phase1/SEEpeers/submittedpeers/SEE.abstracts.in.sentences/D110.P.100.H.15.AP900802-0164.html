[1] Iraq has been feuding with Kuwait over a border dispute. [2] It has also accused Kuwait of violating its oil production quotas set by the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries, costing Iraq of much-needed revenues because oil is cheaper when supplies are higher. [3] The statement on the Iraqi News Agency said the border dispute would be settled with "fraternity and Arab national interests," indicating Iraq would probably take the land it wants. [4] The statement also said the Al-Sabah family, which had ruled Kuwait for almost 250 years, was working in concert with Zionist and colonial powers to plot against Iraq.
