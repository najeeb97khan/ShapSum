[1] Overcrowding and safety violations caused an aging ferry to capsize in the Ganges River, killing as many as 400 people, a government report said. [2] Strong currents hampered efforts today to pull the two-decker from the rising monsoon-swollen river. [3] Hundreds of relatives stood by watching. [4] A senior government official, discussing the confidential report on condition of anonymity, said authorities believed at least 300 people on the lower deck died when the ferry overturned Saturday morning. [5] Navy divers attempted to reach the submerged boat today, but the river was too muddy and its current too swift, said H.S. Pathak, head of the eight-member diving team.
