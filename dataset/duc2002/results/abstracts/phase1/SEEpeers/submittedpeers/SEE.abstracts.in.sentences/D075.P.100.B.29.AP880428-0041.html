[1] New treatments that stop a heart attack in its tracks may help victims to get out of the hospital more quickly than ever before, perhaps even three days after their attacks, a study published today concludes. [2] Doctors from the University of Michigan Medical Center studied the effects of sending a few carefully selected heart patients home early. [3] They found that these people had no unusual complications, got back to work sooner and had substantially lower medical bills. [4] ``It was an extraordinarily safe strategy,'' said Dr. Eric J. Topol, the study's director. [5] ``There were no problems with any serious events''.
