[1] As a young girl in western New York, Lucille Ball hawked hot dogs and popcorn at an amusement park. [2] "She always talked about being a star," said Pauline Lopus. [3] Miss Ball, born 60 miles south of Buffalo in Jamestown on 08/06/1911. [4] At 15, Miss Ball worked as a barker at the amusement park, screaming for people to buy popcorn and hot dogs until she lost her voice. [5] Later, she starred in high school plays and got her start in front of a large audience on the vaudeville stage at the defunct Palace Theater in Jamestown.
