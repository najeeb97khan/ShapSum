[1] Historians say the world need not fear that German jackboots will echo again on European pavements, but some still see the rush toward German reunification as a setback _ a step away from the integration of Europe. [2] ``The West Germans are moving ahead not out of nationalistic fervor, but a perceived need to deal with a crisis,'' said Henry Ashby Turner of Yale, author of ``The Two Germanies Since 1945''. [3] University of Chicago historian Michael Geyer, a native of Germany, said he feared the creation of a ``strong, sovereign nation in the center of Europe'' could reverse a more important development _ the 40-year drive toward an integrated Europe in which industries, banks, trade unions and even government ministries have been collaborating across borders.
