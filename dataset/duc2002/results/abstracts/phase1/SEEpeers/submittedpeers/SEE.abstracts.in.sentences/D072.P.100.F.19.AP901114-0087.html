[1] On the stage where Leonard Bernstein made his sensational New York Philharmonic conducting debut 47 years ago, the orchestra remembered the maestro at a concert that included selections from his "Mass" and "Chichester Psalms". [2] Wednesday 11/14/1990's midday concert at Carnegie Hall also came on the 90th birthday of the late composer-conductor's close friend, Aaron Copland. [3] And it was the 36th anniversary of the first TV broadcast by Bernstein, who died 10/16/1990. [4] Speakers, introducing various selections and often quoting from Bernstein's writings, included Bernstein's three children, son-in-law and friends Lauren Bacall and Schuyler Chapin.
