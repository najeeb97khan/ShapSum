[1] Thunderstorms swept through central and eastern Ohio, causing flooding that killed at least ten people, left dozens missing and forced hundreds of others from their homes, officials said today. [2] Two creeks just outside this Ohio River village of 4,300 people overflowed when about 4 inches of rain fell Thursday night in about an hour. [3] Shadyside Fire Chief Mark Badia said rescuers pulled people from three cars in Wegee Creek, which flows through Shadyside and into the Ohio River. [4] There also was flooding in Jefferson County, north of Belmont, and Licking County in central Ohio. [5] The Newark Fire Department and other agencies evacuated residents in low-lying areas of Newark and the neighboring village of Marne, said Wayne Tresemer, Licking County Disaster Services director.
