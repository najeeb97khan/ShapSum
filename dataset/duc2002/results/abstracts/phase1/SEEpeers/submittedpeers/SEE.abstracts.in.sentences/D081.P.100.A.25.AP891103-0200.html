[1] REGIONAL COURTS AND STRIKES [2] Thousands of miners in the northern Vorkuta region are expanding their strike and some are blocking coal shipments, the Soviet news media reported on Nov. 2, 1989. [3] The news media gave these accounts of the latest strikes: Workers at the Vorgashor mine in the Arctic Circle, the largest mine in the Vorkuta region, continued their strike for an eighth day, according to Tass. [4] A regional court in Vorkuta ruled that the latest round of strikes is illegal but did not impose any penalties. [5] Soviet officials have said the strikes could force fuel rationing during the Soviet Union's severe winter.
