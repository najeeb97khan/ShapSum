[1] A 1985 paper in the Geophysical Research Letters predicted a 6.9-magnitude quake could happen any time on a portion of the San Andreas Fault that stretches 50 miles from Palo Alto southward through the Santa Cruz Mountains and on to San Juan Bautista. [2] The epicenter of the quake was about 8 miles northeast of Santa Cruz and 75 miles south of San Francisco.
