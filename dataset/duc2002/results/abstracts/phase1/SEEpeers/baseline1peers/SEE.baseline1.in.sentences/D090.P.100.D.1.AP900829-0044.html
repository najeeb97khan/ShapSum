[1] President Corazon Aquino today reversed a long-held policy and said she was willing to negotiate cease-fires with Communist guerrillas and former soldiers who have staged coup attempts. [2] Mrs. Aquino also invited the opposition to join the talks, including her most outspoken critic, Sen. Juan Ponce Enrile. [3] Manila has been rocked by almost daily explosions. [4] Although no one has claimed responsibility for the blasts, the government has blamed them on former soldiers who have repeatedly tried to toppled Mrs. Aquino in coup attempts. [5] The Communist rebels have been waging a 21-year rebellion to establish a Marxist state. [6] The last cease-fire with them collapsed in early 1987.
