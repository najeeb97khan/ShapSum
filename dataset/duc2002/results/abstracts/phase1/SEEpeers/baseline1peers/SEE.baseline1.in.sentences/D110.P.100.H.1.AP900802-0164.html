[1] Iraq says it invaded Kuwait at the request of a revolutionary organization there, but diplomats dismiss that rationale as a large-scale charade. [2] The diplomatic world has remained largely unconviced by the flood of statements issued from Iraq in the name of the Provisional Free Kuwait Government. [3] Britain's U.N. ambassador, Crispin Tickell, called the Iraqi action ``an invasion from the outside ... [4] a phony coup d'etat from within''. [5] He said the invasion had led to ``the purported establishment of a puppet government''. [6] In a rare display of solidarity, the United States and the Soviet Union called for Iraq to withdraw and imposed sanctions.
