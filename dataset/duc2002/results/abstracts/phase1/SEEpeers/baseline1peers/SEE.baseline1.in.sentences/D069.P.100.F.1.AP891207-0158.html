[1] The government on Thursday allowed for the possibility of a reunified Germany, but said such a state must be formed gradually and respect Poland's borders. [2] In the Solidarity government's first definitive statement on the issue, Foreign Minister Krzysztof Skubiszewski told Parliament the two German states could be joined as part of the process of uniting Europe. [3] He said German reunification ``cannot go ahead'' of the reunification of Europe. [4] ``A reunited Germany will not be created tomorrow or the day after tomorrow,'' Skubiszewski said. [5] ``Fears and concerns appearing every now and then, including in Poland, are unjustified, although alertness is recommended''.
