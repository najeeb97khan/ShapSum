[1] Presidents, famous actors, television executives, neighbors and tourists all had their memories. [2] Some had known her; most had not . [3] In form their sentiments ranged from flowers to telegrams to hand-lettered signs. [4] Through them all ran a single, constant thread . [5] They all said they loved Lucy, and they'll miss her . [6] From former President and Mrs. Ronald Reagan: "Lucille Ball was a gifted comedienne who brought laughter to millions the world over. [7] Her red hair, her antics on the screen, her timing and her zest for life made her an American institution" . [8] Reagan's successor extended his "deepest sympathy" to Miss Ball's family and called her a "legendary figure".
