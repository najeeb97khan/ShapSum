[1] An Iraqi airways jet carrying 78 foreigners and a number of Iraqi citizens arrived today, the first commercial flight allowed out of Iraq since it invaded Kuwait, a Japanese Embassy spokesman said. [2] The mostly Japanese tourists arrived at Amman airport at 6:15 p.m. [3] (11:15 a.m. [4] EDT) along with 34 members of an Iraqi football team, said the spokesman on condition of anonymity. [5] It was not immediately known if there were Americans on board. [6] He said that the plane was due to leave Amman for an undisclosed destination. [7] The spokesman said the plane was supposed to land in Vienna, but it was denied landing rights.
