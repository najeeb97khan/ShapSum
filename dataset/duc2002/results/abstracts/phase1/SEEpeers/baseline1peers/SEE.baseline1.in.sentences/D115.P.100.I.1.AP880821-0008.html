[1] A strong earthquake hit the Himalayan kingdom of Nepal and parts of eastern India on Sunday morning, triggering landslides and collapsing buildings. [2] First reports said 100 people may have died. [3] Nepal's Department of Geology said the earthquake struck at 4:54 a.m. [4] (7:09 p.m. EDT Saturday) and measured 5.7 on the Richter scale. [5] The department said the quake was centered near Udayapur, a town near the Indian-Nepalese border and 100 miles southeast of Katmandu, Nepal's capital. [6] Officials said the temblor lasted about 40 seconds. [7] In New Delhi, the Seismologoical Observatory said the quake measured 6.5 on the Richter scale.
