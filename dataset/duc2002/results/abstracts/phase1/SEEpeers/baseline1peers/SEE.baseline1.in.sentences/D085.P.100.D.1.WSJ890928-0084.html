[1] Damage from Hurricane Hugo, which stormed through the Carolinas and the Caribbean last week, will reduce third-quarter earnings for several insurers. [2] New York-based American International Group Inc. expects net losses related to Hugo to reduce pretax earnings between $35 million and $45 million. [3] Analysts said AIG has told them that it must cover the first $35 million of losses and that its reinsurance agreements will cover the bulk of the remaining damages. [4] AIG insured property in Puerto Rico, the eastern Caribbean Islands and the coastal regions of the Carolinas. [5] St. Paul Cos., based in St. Paul, Minn, said the pretax cost of Hugo shouldn't exceed $50 million.
