[1] The engineer of a ferry that sank off this Red Sea port, leaving more than 400 people missing, today denied accusations of widespread negligence on the part of the ship's crew. [2] Mahmoud Moharram, engineer of the Salem Express, acknowledged misconduct by some crew members but said the survival of only about 10 of the 71-member crew indicated negligence was not widespread . [3] [4] Investigators were looking into complaints from some survivors that the crew abandoned the ferry without helping passengers and that some crew members pushed aside passengers to get to lifeboats. [5] Moharram said he helped 17 passengers put on life jackets.
