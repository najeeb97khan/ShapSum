[1] Forty years after Sam Walton first hung out his shingle, Walton's 5&10 will reopen to show how a little rural storekeeper built a discount retail empire. [2] Wal-Mart Stores Inc.'s Visitors Center opens Wednesday in the same building on the downtown square where Walton first opened for business on May 9, 1950. [3] The center chronicles the rise of the nation's fastest-growing discount chain, using displays and audio-video presentations. [4] It concentrates on how the free enterprise system works and how Wal-Mart thrived when similar companies didn't. [5] The window displays date back to the store's opening, with Simplicity patterns and Johnson's Glo-Coat, a box of Dreft and some Ivory Snow.
