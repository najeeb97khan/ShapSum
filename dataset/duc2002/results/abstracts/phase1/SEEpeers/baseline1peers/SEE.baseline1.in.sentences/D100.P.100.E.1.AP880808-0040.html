[1] John Lennon spent much of a three-year span tucked away in his room, sleeping or sitting in a lotus position with his head enveloped in tobacco or marijuana smoke, according to a biography of the slain Beatle. [2] An excerpt from the book, ``The Lives of John Lennon'' by journalist and biographer Albert Goldman, appearing in the Aug. 15 issue of People magazine, discloses a sometimes unflattering portrait of both Lennon and his second wife, Yoko Ono. [3] The book is scheduled to be published later this month by William Morrow & Co. Inc. [4] Between 1976 and 1979, Goldman writes, Lennon ``could not be more remote''.
