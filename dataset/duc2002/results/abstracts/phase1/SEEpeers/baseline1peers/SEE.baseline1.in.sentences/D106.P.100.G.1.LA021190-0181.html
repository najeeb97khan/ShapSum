[1] From behind bars, Nelson Mandela has dominated the fight for black rights in South Africa. [2] The following are some of the key events in his life . [3] 1944 [4] Mandela joins the African National Congress at age 26, later becoming president of the group's Youth League. [5] In a short time, the ANC becomes the force behind black protests. [6] When the National Party comes to power in 1948, the ANC begins planning a campaign of civil disobedience to fight the party's apartheid policy . [7] June, 1952 [8] Mandela is named leader of the campaign and breaks curfew as the first act of defiance.
