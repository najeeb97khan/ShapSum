[1] President Mikhail S. Gorbachev said today that thousands of striking coal miners and other workers are urging railway employees to join the work stoppage and that the unrest threatens his economic reforms. [2] Strikes have shut down more than 200 mines and factories in the country's two top coal-producing regions, official reports said. [3] In a report to the Supreme Soviet, Gorbachev said they have resulted in a 1 million-ton drop in coal production. [4] Following Gorbachev to the podium, legislator Anatoly Saunin said each lost work day cost millions of rubles that might otherwise help improve living conditions. [5] ``You mustn't hold a knife to the throat'' of the government, he told strikers.
