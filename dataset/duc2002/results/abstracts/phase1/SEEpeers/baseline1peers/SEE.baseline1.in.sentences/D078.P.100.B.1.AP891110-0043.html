[1] The Oscar statuette, one of the most recognizable images in the entertainment world, has no copyright protection, a federal judge has ruled. [2] The small Academy Award statue is part of the public domain, U.S. [3] District Court Judge Laughlin Waters said in a ruling released Thursday. [4] The decision was a setback for the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, which had sued a Chicago-based manufacturer of an employee-incentive trophy similar to the Oscar. [5] Academy President Karl Malden said the ruling ``comes as a shock to me''. [6] The academy said it would appeal.
