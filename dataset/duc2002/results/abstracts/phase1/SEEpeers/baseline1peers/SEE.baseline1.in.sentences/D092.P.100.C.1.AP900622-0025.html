[1] The earthquake that struck northern Iran brought reminders of another quake that devastated Soviet Armenia in 1988. [2] Like Thursday's earthquake in Iran, the Dec. 7, 1988, earthquake in Armenia killed about 25,000 people. [3] Most died in structures that collapsed when the quake hit. [4] ``It's very similar to what we had in Armenia,'' said Russ Needham, a geophysicist with the U.S. [5] Geological Survey. [6] The Armenian quake was measured at 6.9 on the Richter scale, but Thursday's earthquake shook the earth with a magnitude of 7.7 according to the U.S. [7] Geological Survey. [8] In the Iranian quake, the casualty count was pushed up by the fact that people were indoors and asleep at the time, just after midnight.
